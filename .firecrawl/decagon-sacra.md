Sacra estimates that [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) hit $35M annualized revenue in October 2025, up from $10M at the end of 2024. The company's Q3 2025 GAAP revenue and ARR each grew more than 3x year over year. [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) added over 100 new global enterprise customers in 2025, including Avis Budget Group, Mercado Libre, and Deutsche Telekom.

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) generates revenue through two primary pricing models: per-conversation and per-resolution. The per-conversation model charges a fixed rate for each incoming customer inquiry, with volume discounts available. The per-resolution model, which is higher priced, charges only when the AI successfully resolves an issue without human intervention.

The company's customer base spans F100 enterprises across airlines, banks, telecom, and retail, as well as high-growth tech companies including [Notion](https://sacra.com/c/notion/), Duolingo, [Rippling](https://sacra.com/c/rippling/), [Bilt](https://sacra.com/c/bilt/), Eventbrite, [Substack](https://sacra.com/c/substack/), Oura Health, Affirm, and [Chime](https://sacra.com/c/chime/). These customers typically save millions annually by implementing Decagon's AI agents, with one client reducing their support team by 80%.

## Valuation & Funding

Decagon was valued at $4.5 billion in January 2026, following a $250 million Series D funding round led by Coatue Management and Index Ventures. The round included participation from new investors ChemistryVC, Definition Capital, and Starwood Capital, along with continued support from existing investors including a16z, A\*, Accel, Avra, Bain Capital Ventures, Elad Gil, Forerunner, Ribbit Capital, and T.Capital.

In March 2026, [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) completed its first employee tender offer at the same $4.5 billion valuation, led by Series D investors including Coatue, Index, a16z, Definition, [Forerunner](https://sacra.com/c/forerunner/), and Ribbit Capital, giving more than 300 employees the opportunity to sell a portion of their vested shares.

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) previously raised a $131 million Series C in June 2025 at a $1.5 billion valuation, co-led by Accel and Andreessen Horowitz's growth fund. The company was valued at $650 million as of October 2024, following a $65 million Series B funding round led by Bain Capital Ventures. [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) completed a $35 million Series A round in July 2024. The company has raised approximately $481 million to date. Key early investors include Bain Capital Ventures, Accel, Andreessen Horowitz, and Elad Gil.

## Product

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) was founded in August 2023 by Jesse Zhang and Ashwin Sreenivas, who brought technical expertise from their backgrounds at Google, Citadel Securities, Palantir, and their previous startups Lowkey and Helia.

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) found product-market fit as an AI-powered customer support platform for high-growth startups and enterprises seeking to automate their support operations while maintaining quality customer experiences.

The platform builds AI agents that handle end-to-end customer support tasks, from answering product questions to processing refunds and cancellations. These agents integrate with existing customer workflows and knowledge bases, allowing them to access real-time information and take concrete actions within a company's systems. For example, when a customer asks about postponing a shipment, [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s AI can access order management systems to complete the task without human intervention.

Companies like [Notion](https://sacra.com/c/notion/), Duolingo, Eventbrite, [Bilt](https://sacra.com/c/bilt/), Avis Budget Group, [Block](https://sacra.com/c/blockfi/), and Deutsche Telekom use Decagon to automate routine support inquiries while freeing human agents to focus on complex issues requiring personal attention.

Beyond chat and email, [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) has extended its platform into enterprise-grade AI phone support through [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) Voice, built in partnership with [ElevenLabs](https://sacra.com/c/elevenlabs/). Voice 2.0 (launched September 2025) delivers a 65% reduction in latency and adds cross-channel memory, outbound calling, SMS integration, and finer brand controls—enabling agents to engage customers consistently across every channel rather than treating each interaction in isolation. Rounding out the operator toolset, [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) University supports agent training, AOP Copilot assists operators in managing deployments, and Simulations allow teams to test agent behavior before going live.

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s most recent expansion shifts the platform from reactive support to proactive engagement. Proactive Agents combines user memory with outbound voice so agents can initiate conversations and remember customers across interactions—effectively turning support agents into personal concierges rather than passive responders. Agent Workbench complements this by giving teams a dedicated environment for debugging and improving agent performance over time.

## Business Model

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) is an AI-powered customer support platform that automates customer service interactions across chat, email, and voice channels. The company offers AI agents that can handle complex tasks including answering questions, processing refunds, canceling subscriptions, disputing transactions, and replacing credit cards.

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) employs two primary pricing models. Their more popular per-conversation model charges clients a fixed rate for each incoming customer conversation, with volume discounts for higher usage. Alternatively, [their per-resolution model charges a higher fixed rate only when conversations are fully resolved without human intervention](https://sacra.com/chat/h/bcf08909-1e48-4c9e-bcc9-3ce3b91d26b1/), with no charge for escalations. Larger resolution commitments lower the per-resolution rate.

The platform integrates with existing ticketing systems and customer databases, allowing for seamless implementation within a client's existing infrastructure. [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s AI agents are built using a combination of third-party models from [OpenAI](https://sacra.com/c/openai/), [Anthropic](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/), and [Cohere](https://sacra.com/c/cohere/), along with proprietary fine-tuned models trained on enterprise data including knowledge bases and historical support conversations.

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) has expanded its go-to-market reach through a strategic partnership with TaskUs, which deploys [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) across digital and voice channels in customer-experience transformations, and through offices in New York City (opened July 2025) and London (opened November 2025) that extend its presence to the U.S. East Coast and Europe.

## Competition

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) operates in the AI-powered customer service automation market, which has seen rapid growth as enterprises seek to reduce support costs while maintaining or improving customer satisfaction.

### Enterprise AI customer service platforms

[The most direct competitors to Decagon are comprehensive AI customer service platforms targeting enterprise clients.](https://sacra.com/chat/h/b2863a03-6298-4b2c-ac7c-1c3a14528c66/) [Sierra](https://sacra.com/c/sierra/), founded by former Salesforce and Google executives, has achieved a $4.5 billion valuation with $175 million in Series B funding as of October 2024. [Sierra](https://sacra.com/c/sierra/) differentiates itself by focusing on reducing AI "hallucinations" and has secured notable clients like WeightWatchers and SiriusXM.

Established CRM giants have also entered this space. Salesforce offers Einstein GPT for customer service, [leveraging its dominant position in the CRM market to provide integrated AI solutions](https://sacra.com/chat/h/0a4e730d-8a1d-4e34-9216-6fa4f0b70717/). [Zendesk](https://sacra.com/c/zendesk/) has incorporated AI capabilities into its platform, focusing on ticket routing and agent assistance rather than full automation.

Forethought, having raised over $90 million from New Enterprise Associates, provides AI customer service solutions with a focus on enterprise knowledge management. Their approach emphasizes integrating with existing support workflows rather than replacing them entirely.

### Vertical-specific AI support solutions

Some competitors focus on specific industry verticals or use cases. [Gorgias](https://sacra.com/c/gorgias/) targets e-commerce businesses, particularly Shopify merchants, with 40% of Shopify's top 250 merchants using their platform. Their pricing is usage-based, charging per ticket rather than per seat, with add-on services for voice, SMS, and AI automation.

[Intercom has pivoted from general chat solutions to focus more on B2B companies](https://sacra.com/chat/h/83cd3461-ed04-4b98-bb13-28578fda2f4e/), recently switching from [OpenAI](https://sacra.com/c/openai/) to [Anthropic](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/)'s Claude for their AI capabilities. They've maintained strong traction with SaaS companies while expanding their AI offerings.

### AI infrastructure providers

Large technology companies provide the underlying AI models that power many customer service solutions. [OpenAI](https://sacra.com/c/openai/)'s GPT models, [Anthropic](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/)'s Claude, and Google's Gemini are used by various players in this space, including [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/). [These companies sometimes compete directly with specialized solutions through their own enterprise offerings.](https://sacra.com/chat/h/7dcbe720-917e-43c3-a110-8489260950cd/)

Amazon's AWS offers Amazon Bedrock and Amazon Q, which enable businesses to build their own AI agents for customer service. Google provides Contact Center AI and Dialogflow for building conversational agents, leveraging their expertise in natural language processing.

The competitive landscape is further complicated by the emergence of specialized tools for specific aspects of customer service automation, such as voice AI, knowledge management, and analytics.

## TAM Expansion

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) has tailwinds from the growing demand for AI-powered customer service solutions and has the opportunity to grow and expand into adjacent markets like voice-based support, revenue generation tools, and enterprise-wide AI orchestration.

### Customer support automation

The customer support automation market is projected to grow from $308 million in 2022 to $2.89 billion by 2032. [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) is well-positioned in this expanding market with its AI agents that can handle complex support tasks autonomously. Their technology already achieves impressive results, with one client reporting a 90% resolution rate for customer inquiries without human intervention, and [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) added 100+ new global enterprise customers in 2025, including Avis Budget Group, [Block](https://sacra.com/c/blockfi/), and Deutsche Telekom.

### Voice and multimodal expansion

A significant growth avenue for [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) is expanding into voice-based customer support. Despite the growth of chat, many customers still prefer phone support. [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s voice platform now supports outbound calling and SMS with cross-channel memory, and its Proactive Agents capability enables agents to initiate conversations with customers rather than waiting to respond—shifting [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s scope from reactive support to proactive engagement across channels.

### Revenue generation and customer lifecycle

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) is evolving from cost-saving to revenue-generating AI solutions through its Proactive Agents capability, which combines user memory with outbound voice to enable personalized, initiative-taking interactions across the customer lifecycle. The company aims to transform their AI agents into "personal concierges" for customers, providing proactive support and personalized recommendations. This approach not only increases the value proposition for existing clients but also opens up new markets beyond traditional customer support.

### Geographic and enterprise expansion

[Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) has extended its go-to-market presence to the U.S. East Coast and Europe through offices in New York City (opened July 2025) and London (opened November 2025). The Deutsche Telekom commercial pilot and strategic investment from T.Capital, Deutsche Telekom's corporate venture arm, signals traction with large multinational telcos and validates [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s expansion beyond U.S. tech-native clients.

## Risks

**Reliance on third-party AI models**: [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s platform relies heavily on third-party AI models from providers like [OpenAI](https://sacra.com/c/openai/), [Anthropic](https://sacra.com/c/anthropic/), and [Cohere](https://sacra.com/c/cohere/). This creates dependency risk if these providers change their pricing, access policies, or experience technical issues. While [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/) has built systems to select optimal models for specific tasks, significant changes in the foundational AI market could impact their cost structure and service reliability.

**Competitive convergence with established players**: [As larger CRM and customer service platforms like Salesforce and Zendesk integrate more sophisticated AI capabilities, Decagon faces the risk of feature parity.](https://sacra.com/chat/h/96e0145d-0ac6-43c2-a0dd-047661808c45/) These incumbents have existing customer relationships, integrated workflows, and significant resources to close the technology gap. [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s early technological advantage could erode as the market matures, potentially squeezing margins and growth.

**AI hallucination management at scale**: [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s value proposition hinges on delivering accurate, reliable AI customer service. As deployment scales across diverse industries with complex policies, maintaining low error rates becomes exponentially challenging. Highly publicized AI failures could damage both [Decagon](https://sacra.com/c/decagon/)'s reputation and broader market confidence in AI customer service solutions.

[**Sierra vs Decagon**](https://sacra.com/research/sierra-vs-decagon/)

While Sierra has focused on inbound support, voice agents that can dial any phone number & converse with humans are effectively a universal API into legacy systems, enabling expansion into outbound sales (lead qualification, cold calling), collections, insurance claims follow-up, supplier management, and any workflow where enterprises currently pay humans to sit on hold and talk to other companies' phone systems & human agents. In turn, the AI support market is fragmenting into full-stack replacements that rip out existing tools (Sierra, Decagon), lightweight AI layers that sit on top of existing helpdesks (Parahelp), and incumbents bundling AI into their existing platforms (Intercom)—with full-stack players becoming the inputs for existing systems of record like Salesforce with the potential to integrate with them or displace them.
