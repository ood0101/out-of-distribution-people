I lead the Health AI and AGI Benefits teams at OpenAI, working towards safely securing the benefits of AI.

Our work aims to:

- **Improve human health with AI**
  - make health intelligence accessible to everyone
  - improve performance and safety for 230M+ people who use AI for health weekly
  - enable new 0→1 use cases where greater intelligence will most improve human health
- **Make models deeply beneficial**: understand, measure, and improve the ways models can be made broadly and persistently beneficial to humanity
- **Make tangible AGI benefits real**: scale AI’s benefit to humanity across domains

Questions I’m interested in currently:

- Where will increases in intelligence most marginally improve human wellbeing?
- How can we differentially accelerate societal resilience to advanced AI?
- Where could today’s models be better, for patients, clinicians, healthcare, or scientists?

My previous work at Google includes [Med-PaLM](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-023-06291-2) and [Med-PaLM 2](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-024-03423-7), launched in Google Cloud and featured in Nature, Scientific American, The Economist, BBC, and other outlets.
