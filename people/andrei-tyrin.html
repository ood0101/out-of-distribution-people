◆ Investment Thesis — T0 (Active Pursuit)

Andrei Tyrin is the genuinely-technical ML/agentic co-founder of Onepot AI — the other half of the Daniil Boiko bet, now a 2-founder node in the index. Where Boiko (Coscientist) owns the chemistry, lab automation, and robotics, Tyrin owns the AI: LLM orchestration, agentic coordination, and long-horizon multi-step synthesis planning. In his own words (Rowan interview): “we have this general-layer intelligence and this specialized intelligence that captures things at the atom level,” and the system does “planning across a longer horizon” for autonomous multi-step (e.g. taxol) synthesis.

The pedigree is the real, end-to-end “why synthesis is the bottleneck” arc: MIT BS Computer Science → Schrödinger (generative models for molecular design) → Genesis Therapeutics (protein-ligand folding models) → co-founding a wet-lab company. He has personally watched pure-compute molecular design stall at the synthesis step (his line: “the models could generate ideas in hours, but it could take months for the lab to catch up”). First author on “Digitization of molecular complexity with ML” (Chemical Science 2025 — a ~300K-datapoint Learning-to-Rank model, w/ Boiko + Ananikov) and on onepot CORE (arXiv 2026, w/ Brandon Wang et al.).

The honest read — T0, with caveats that don’t change the action. The $13M round is closed with the lead spot gone (Fifty Years), but a $500K–$1M inception/strategic allocation is still writable for a co-founder of this caliber — so active pursuit, reach out now. Caveats (price, don’t ignore): his academic citation count is modest (~29 vs Boiko’s ~1,880) — his spike is engineering/agentic-systems + the Schrödinger/Genesis/MIT credential stack, not citation volume; no source uses a formal “CTO” title (consistently “co-founder,” but the technical ownership is explicit and verbatim-sourced); and the widely-repeated “Boiko + Tyrin met at MSU” is a hypothesis — the verified link is the Ananikov Lab (GitHub org membership + co-authorship), not a shared MSU degree (Tyrin’s path is HS chem competitions → MIT CS, no MSU degree).
