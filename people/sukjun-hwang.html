◆ Investment Thesis

Sukjun Hwang is the architectural engine at the leading edge of the post-Transformer state-space-model lineage. The lineage runs S4 (Albert Gu, 2021) → Mamba (Gu + Dao, 2023) → Mamba-2 (Dao + Gu, 2024) → Hydra (Hwang + Lahoti + Dao + Gu, NeurIPS 2024) → H-Net (Hwang + Wang + Gu, ICLR 2026) → dnaHNet (ICML 2026 Spotlight) — and on the latest two foundational papers, Hwang is first author. He is the rare PhD student whose advisor writes a public blog post (“H-Nets — the Past”) saying “Sukjun refused to listen to me because he had a much better idea” and “it was like watching an artist at work.” That is not a phrase Albert Gu writes about ordinary students.

What this is, mechanically. H-Net is the first credible architecture paper from outside the frontier labs that plausibly retires BPE tokenization — a default that has held since 2018 and that Karpathy has spent five years calling a top-three bottleneck in modern LLMs. At matched compute, H-Net 2-stage hits 0.743 BPB vs 0.756 for a 760M BPE Transformer, jumps to 58.2% downstream avg vs 55.5% at 1.3B, and most strikingly scores 42.8 on text-perturbation robustness vs 22.2 for the Transformer — a 2x gap that demonstrates the architecture is doing the thing tokenization was supposed to do but never really did. On DNA the team reports ~4x data efficiency over baselines; the dnaHNet follow-up (Feb 2026, ICML Spotlight) extends this to a full genomic foundation model that beats StripedHyena2 and delivers 3x inference speedup over Transformers. Hydra (NeurIPS 2024) is the bidirectional Mamba extension and the paper that beats BERT by 0.8 GLUE and ViT by 2% ImageNet Top-1 as a drop-in attention replacement — co-authored with Tri Dao, who founded Together AI and is the most important young researcher in the SSM commercial play.

The Spike — With Evidence

(1) H-Net is a foundation-paper-shaped object, and Hwang is 1st author

Dynamic Chunking for End-to-End Hierarchical Sequence Modeling — Hwang, Wang (Cartesia), Gu (CMU + Cartesia). Submitted arXiv 2025-07-10 (v1), 2025-07-15 (v2). Accepted as ICLR 2026 Poster. 846 GitHub stars on goombalab/hnet. HuggingFace checkpoints released for English / Chinese / code, Large and XL sizes.

The paper introduces dynamic chunking — a differentiable mechanism that automatically segments raw byte sequences into content-dependent chunks, replacing the tokenize → transformer → detokenize pipeline with a single end-to-end model. The two key submodules: a routing module (similarity-score boundary predictor) and a smoothing module (EMA-style interpolation that attenuates uncertain boundaries and makes discrete chunking learnable). Encoder/decoder use Mamba-2 layers; the main network is a Transformer operating on compressed chunks. Two-stage H-Net composes the chunking recursively.

Model (760M params, matched compute)BPB ↓
Transformer (BPE tokens)0.756
MambaByte (byte-level)0.845
SpaceByte++0.760
H-Net 1-stage0.755
H-Net 2-stage (recursive hierarchy)0.743

At XL scale (1.3B), H-Net 2-stage hits 58.2% downstream avg vs 55.5% Transformer. Robustness story: 42.8 vs 22.2 (2x) on text perturbation. DNA: ~4x data efficiency. Chinese and code — the languages where BPE is genuinely worst — show the largest gains in Cartesia’s blog post on the work.

Author ordering. Hwang is sole 1st author (no equal-contribution asterisk on H-Net — this is his paper). Brandon Wang (then-Cartesia, now onepot) is 2nd. Albert Gu (PI, Cartesia CTO) is senior. The Goomba Lab blog post by Albert Gu credits Hwang explicitly for “the technical breakthroughs” behind the model and writes that Hwang “refused to listen to me because he had a much better idea” — an unusual public statement of advisor deference.

(2) Hydra (NeurIPS 2024) — bidirectional Mamba, co-author with Tri Dao

Hydra: Bidirectional State Space Models Through Generalized Matrix Mixers — Hwang*, Lahoti*, Dao, Gu. Submitted 2024-07-13. NeurIPS 2024 Poster. 171 GitHub stars; 58 citations.

Technical contribution: a unifying “matrix mixer” framework for sequence models (Transformers, Mamba, structured SSMs all special cases). Key axis is sequence-alignment of the matrix parameterization. Hydra is the natural bidirectional extension of Mamba via quasiseparable matrix structure — meaning it works on non-causal tasks where vanilla Mamba can’t. Plug-in result: Hydra beats BERT by 0.8 points on GLUE and ViT by 2% Top-1 on ImageNet as a drop-in attention replacement.

Why this co-authorship matters. Tri Dao is the most important young researcher in the SSM & efficient-attention space (FlashAttention, Mamba-2, founded Together AI which has raised $300M+). Hwang is the only Albert Gu PhD student to have first-authored a paper jointly with Dao. The Hwang-Dao bridge is the single most valuable network edge in the entire post-Transformer architecture community: it puts him one degree from Together AI in addition to Cartesia. Aakash Lahoti (Hwang’s co-equal-1st-author) is the other Goomba Lab PhD student in this cluster.

(3) dnaHNet (ICML 2026 Spotlight) — H-Net for genomics

dnaHNet: A Scalable and Hierarchical Foundation Model for Genomic Sequence Learning — Shah, Li, Idehpour, Fallahpour, B. Wang, S. Hwang, B. Wang, Hsu, Goodarzi, Gu. arXiv 2602.10603 (Feb 2026). ICML 2026 Spotlight forthcoming.

Tokenizer-free genomic LM that segments raw nucleotide sequences end-to-end via the H-Net dynamic chunking primitive. Outperforms StripedHyena2 (the current state-of-the-art DNA SSM, from Hazy Research / Arc Institute) on scaling and efficiency. Recursive chunking yields quadratic FLOP reductions and enables >3x inference speedup over Transformers. Zero-shot superiority on protein-variant fitness and gene-essentiality prediction. Automatically discovers hierarchical biological structures (codons, genes) without supervision.

Cluster significance. The senior author list is Hsu (Arc Institute alum) + Goodarzi (UCSF, RNA biology + cancer) + Gu (CMU/Cartesia) — this is one of the rare biology-PI × SSM-architecture collaborations and positions H-Net as the architecture of choice for the next-generation genomic foundation models. Direct competitors are HyenaDNA (Hazy Research), Evo (Hazy Research / Arc), Caduceus (Princeton), and Nucleotide Transformer (InstaDeep). The dnaHNet paper is therefore Hwang’s extension into a totally different modality — demonstrating the architecture is general, which is the founding-narrative-shape of an SSM-platform pitch.

Yonsei University BS (summa cum laude, ranked 1st in his class) followed by Yonsei MS (2020–2022) under Seon Joo Kim in computer vision — specifically video instance segmentation, where he and Miran Heo built the IFC / VISOLO / VITA / GenVIS line of papers (NeurIPS 2021, CVPR 2022 Oral, NeurIPS 2022 with VITA placing #3 on YouTube-VIS 2022 Challenge, CVPR 2023, ECCV 2024). Internships at Qualcomm AI Research (2019–2020) and Adobe Research (2022). Started CMU ML PhD in August 2023 under Albert Gu — Gu’s first cohort of students after moving from Princeton to CMU. Within a year of starting, Hwang was first-authoring Hydra with Tri Dao at NeurIPS 2024; within two years, sole-1st-authoring H-Net at ICLR 2026. CVPR 2022 Outstanding Reviewer Award. Jane Street Graduate Research Fellowship Finalist 2026.
