The Spike — With Evidence

Nature 2023 — “Human mobility networks reveal increased segregation in large cities” (DOI 10.1038/s41586-023-06757-3, Dec 2023, PMID 38030732). 4th of 10 authors. Senior authors: David Grusky (Stanford Sociology) + Jure Leskovec (Stanford CS). Her affiliation on the byline is Stanford Department of Biomedical Data Science. First author Hamed Nilforoshan; co-authors include Emma Pierson (now Cornell Tech faculty), Nic Fishman (Stanford CS), Beth Redbird (Northwestern). The paper uses mobile-phone mobility data on 1.6 billion real-world exposures among 9.6 million people across 382 US MSAs to show that the ten largest metros have 67% higher exposure segregation than small metros — the empirical kill-shot to the long-standing “big cities mix people” conjecture. A top-tier Leskovec-lab Nature paper is the kind of credential that carries weight across both the CS and clinical/health-policy audiences she now needs.

Apple Hearing Study (JASA 2022) — “Toward a better understanding of nonoccupational sound exposures and associated health impacts: Methods of the Apple Hearing Study” (Journal of the Acoustical Society of America, March 2022, PMID 35364926). Author list ends with the Apple Inc. team: Neitzel, Smith, Wang, Green, Block, Carchia, Mazur, DePalma, Azimi, Villanueva. This is the canonical methods paper describing the nationwide study launched in the Apple Research app in Nov 2019 that measured headphone audio levels, environmental sound (Apple Watch noise app), and cardiovascular metrics in hundreds of thousands of US Apple Watch / iPhone users — the single largest non-occupational sound-exposure study ever run. Her role per author position and her own bio (“shipping Apple Health algorithms”) is on the ML / signal-processing side of the noise and hearing-features pipeline that ships in iOS Health / watchOS.

JGIM 2019 — “Characterizing Subgroups of High-Need, High-Cost Patients Based on Their Clinical Conditions: a Machine Learning-Based Analysis of Medicaid Claims Data” (Journal of General Internal Medicine, 2019, PMID 30887432). 3rd author of 6. Healthcare claims-data ML — early evidence of the clinical-data-ML interest that later shows up at Stanford Biomedical Data Science and the current Stanford Med affiliation.
