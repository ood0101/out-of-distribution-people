◆ Investment Thesis

Samuel Stanton is a strong researcher at the intersection of foundational ML and drug discovery who just executed a remarkable outcome: co-founding Coefficient Bio, operating for ~8 months in stealth with fewer than 10 people, and selling to Anthropic for ~$400M in stock (April 3, 2026). Dimension, which held 50% of the company, boasted a 38,513% IRR. He is now embedded inside Anthropic’s Health Care & Life Sciences team — one of the 20 non-negotiable departure-tracking companies. The investment window has closed for now. He is not a near-term founder candidate; he just got acquired. However, his trajectory (PhD → Genentech → co-founded and exited in <1 year → Anthropic) marks him as someone with both the technical depth and the founder instinct to build again. Track for future departure signals from Anthropic. The lab-in-the-loop work at Genentech is genuine IP, and his co-author network (Andrew Gordon Wilson’s lab alumni now at OpenAI, Meta FAIR, Anthropic, Jump Trading) is elite.

The Spike — With Evidence

1. Coefficient Bio: 8-Month Stealth to $400M Exit

Stanton co-founded Coefficient Bio in approximately August 2025 with Nathan Frey (CTO, former Principal ML Scientist & Group Leader at Prescient Design/Genentech, PhD UPenn Materials Science, ICLR Outstanding Paper 2024) and Aris Theologis (CEO, MBA Harvard Business School, BS Biochemistry Stanford, formerly CBO at Evozyne where he raised $150M+). The company was backed by Dimension (50% ownership) and had fewer than 10 employees.

Coefficient Bio built a platform for AI-driven biopharma tasks: drug R&D planning, clinical regulatory strategy, and drug candidate discovery. Stanton tweeted in January 2026: “We’re ushering biopharma into the Intelligence Age. It will change everything about how the industry learns and makes decisions.” Anthropic acquired them on April 3, 2026, for ~$400M in stock. The team joins Anthropic’s Health Care Life Sciences group led by Eric Kauderer-Abrams.

2. Lab-in-the-Loop at Genentech/Prescient Design

At Genentech, Stanton was a core contributor to the lab-in-the-loop therapeutic antibody design system — a semiautonomous pipeline orchestrating generative ML models, multi-task property predictors, active learning, and in vitro experimentation. Over 4 iterative rounds and 1,800+ unique antibody variants across 4 clinically relevant targets (EGFR, IL-6, HER2, OSM), the system achieved:

  • 3–100x binding improvement over starting lead molecules
  • 99% expression rate by round 4
  • 26%+ of designs showing ≥3-fold binding improvement
  • Best binders in the therapeutically relevant ~100 pM range

Paper submitted to Nature Biotechnology (minor revisions requested, March 2025). This is the kind of validated, wet-lab-closed-loop result that most AI bio companies claim but few demonstrate.

3. “Does Knowledge Distillation Really Work?” (NeurIPS 2021)

First-author paper at NeurIPS 2021 that challenged a core assumption of model compression. Showed that knowledge distillation often fails to transfer the teacher’s predictive distribution to the student, even when the student has capacity to match — identifying optimization difficulties as the root cause. This paper became one of his most cited works and is referenced in the Wikipedia article on knowledge distillation.
