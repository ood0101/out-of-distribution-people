Continual Learning Thesis — What Does “Building Continual Learning” Mean?

Continual learning ≠ continual pretraining. It is the problem of building models that adapt over time on new data and tasks without catastrophic forgetting. The commercial wedge: agents and assistants that genuinely improve with use, personalized models that drift toward each user, edge AI that updates locally without going back to base.

The space is small but heating up. Public reference points in 2026:

The a16z piece “Why We Need Continual Learning” frames the institutional thesis: the next wave of value in LLMs comes from models that do not freeze at deployment. The commercial primitives are: episodic memory, on-device gradient updates, replay buffers without forgetting, online RL, parameter-efficient continual finetuning (LoRA-style but at runtime). If Jennifer is building in this space with a Coho-attached technical co-founder, the structural advantage is access to Cartesia (SSM expertise), Coho Lab GPU credits, and the Midjourney research network.
