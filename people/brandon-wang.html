(2) H-Net is a foundation-paper-shaped object, and Brandon is 2nd author

Dynamic Chunking for End-to-End Hierarchical Sequence Modeling — Hwang (CMU, Albert Gu’s student), Wang (Cartesia), Gu (CMU + Cartesia). Submitted arXiv Jul 10 2025 (v1), Jul 15 2025 (v2). Accepted as ICLR 2026 Poster. Cartesia’s blog post on the work is co-authored by Albert Gu and Brandon Wang.

The technical claim: replace the entire tokenize → transformer → detokenize pipeline with a single end-to-end model that learns content-dependent chunk boundaries via a similarity-based routing module + an EMA smoothing module that makes discrete chunking differentiable. Encoder/decoder use Mamba-2 (state-space layers, efficient byte-level compression); the main network is a Transformer operating on the compressed chunks.

Model (760M params, matched compute)BPB ↓
Transformer (BPE tokens)0.756
MambaByte (byte-level)0.845
SpaceByte++0.760
H-Net 1-stage0.755
H-Net 2-stage (recursive hierarchy)0.743

At XL scale (1.3B params), the 2-stage H-Net hits 58.2% downstream avg vs 55.5% for the Transformer baseline. The robustness story is even more striking: H-Net 2-stage scores 42.8 on text-perturbation robustness vs 22.2 for the Transformer — nearly 2x. On DNA the team reports a ~4x improvement in data efficiency, and the Cartesia blog flags Chinese and code as domains where the gains are pronounced (because BPE is bad at them).

Why this matters for thesis-building: Karpathy has spent five+ years saying tokenization is one of the three top bottlenecks in modern LLMs. H-Net is the first architecture paper from outside the frontier labs that is plausibly going to flip the default. The author ordering — Hwang (CMU PhD doing the bulk of the experimental work) → Wang (Cartesia, the engineering counterpart) → Gu (PI, Cartesia CTO) — is the standard PhD-student / industry-research / senior-author order. Brandon being on a paper of this caliber as an MIT-undergrad-just-graduated researcher is a very strong signal.

(3) STOC 2024 first-author with Mohsen Ghaffari

Lenzen’s Distributed Routing Generalized: A Full Characterization of Constant-Time Routability — Ghaffari, Wang. STOC 2024. Brandon presented in June 2024. The paper extends Lenzen and Wattenhofer’s foundational distributed routing result to general network topologies, characterizing networks capable of routing packets in constant rounds via a positive-minimum-normalized-cut condition. Theory venue, not ML — a different type of paper from H-Net, and a different type of taste. Two papers in two different fields, both at top venues, both before turning 24.

What He’s Building (and Could Build)

The onepot bet

onepot AI was founded by Daniil Boiko (CMU PhD, lead author of the seminal Nature 2023 Coscientist paper on autonomous LLM-driven chemistry) and Andrei Tyrin (MIT CS, met Boiko at Lomonosov Moscow State chemistry lab pre-emigration). The company runs an automated small-molecule synthesis lab (POT-1) with an in-house LLM agent (“Phil”) that designs, executes, and analyzes synthesis experiments. The thesis: the bottleneck in early drug discovery isn’t protein structure prediction (AlphaFold 3 / Boltz / Chai have that covered) — it’s making the molecule, which still costs thousands of dollars per compound and takes weeks to months. onepot CORE (Brandon’s 2nd-author paper) defines the synthesizable subspace: 3.4B molecules, all on-demand from automated synthesis.

Brandon’s role. He is described on his own site as an ML engineer training models for chemical synthesis. Given his 6-7 background (CS + Molecular Biology), the H-Net architecture toolkit, and the dnaHNet paper (H-Net applied to DNA sequences with 4x data efficiency), the obvious bet is: he is building hierarchical-byte-level foundation models for SMILES/SELFIES/reaction strings, where standard tokenization is known to be a problem. This is technically the same problem H-Net solved for code and Chinese — molecular string spaces have weak natural tokenization boundaries and discrete-but-similar substructures.

Adjacent thesis: Cartesia/CMU H-Net cluster as architecture-IP machine

Three H-Net-flavored papers in 12 months: H-Net (ICLR 2026), dnaHNet (ICML 2026 Spotlight) on genomic sequences, and the Cartesia voice-modeling pipeline that almost certainly uses H-Net or a descendant for Sonic 2/Sonic Turbo. Brandon, Sukjun Hwang, and Albert Gu are the recurring core. If H-Nets become the architecture default for any modality where BPE is bad, this trio is collectively the next Tri Dao. Brandon being the one who left Cartesia for chemistry is either (a) an early-team move at the most interesting chemistry-foundation-models company on the seed market, or (b) a stepping stone to founding his own next-gen molecular-architecture lab. Either way, T1 minimum.
