The Spike — With Evidence

Scaling Laws for Precision — ICLR 2025 Oral Award (co-1st author)

arXiv:2411.04330. Tanishq Kumar*, Zachary Ankner*, Benjamin F. Spector, Blake Bordelon, Niklas Muennighoff, Mansheej Paul, Cengiz Pehlevan, Christopher Ré, Aditi Raghunathan. ICLR 2025 Oral — one of ~80 of ~5,000+ submissions (top ~1.6%). 112 Google Scholar citations in the ~7 months since publication — the canonical pre-print on how training/inference precision affects scaling laws.

  • Headline contribution: derives precision-aware scaling laws that predict loss as a function of data, parameters, and the precision of weights/activations/attention during training and inference
  • Empirical scope: over 465 pretraining runs, with validation on language models up to 1.7B parameters trained on up to 26B tokens
  • Key findings:
    • Training in lower precision reduces effective parameter count — quantifies the loss cost
    • Post-training quantization degradation increases with the amount of pretraining data — meaning over-trained models become worse candidates for low-precision inference. This is the direct theoretical cousin of his Overtrained paper
    • For low compute budgets, optimal training precision is independent of compute; for large compute budgets, optimal precision  grows with compute
  • Co-author density: first joint paper of the Pehlevan + Aditi + Chris Ré + Niklas axes — basically every major scaling-laws node aligned on one paper. The fact that Tanishq is co-first author signals he was the primary intellectual driver
  • Commercial reach: referenced inside the precision-aware training discussions at Anthropic, DeepMind, and Inception Labs. Direct relevance to D-Matrix / Tenstorrent / Cerebras / Groq inference-precision roadmaps

Overtrained Language Models Are Harder to Fine-Tune — SCOPE Outstanding + ICBINB Best Paper (ICLR 2025) + ICML 2025

arXiv:2503.19206. Jacob Mitchell Springer, Sachin Goyal, Kaiyue Wen, Tanishq Kumar, Xiang Yue, Sadhika Malladi, Graham Neubig, Aditi Raghunathan. 56 citations. Tanishq is 4th of 8 authors and the only Stanford-side author at the time of publication — bridges Aditi’s CMU lab with the Liang/Stanford side. This is the “catastrophic overtraining” paper: instruction-tuned OLMo-1B pretrained on 3T tokens scores >2% worse on multiple benchmarks than the 2.3T-token counterpart. Theoretical analysis: catastrophic overtraining arises from systematic increase in broad sensitivity of pretrained parameters to perturbations. VentureBeat covered it Apr 2025: directly relevant to enterprise-scale training-budget decisions (Anthropic, OpenAI, Mistral pretraining teams).

No Free Prune: Information-Theoretic Barriers to Pruning at Initialization — ICML 2024 (co-1st author)

Mark Sellke is Harvard Statistics — the paper signals Tanishq crossed the math/CS boundary as an undergrad, working with both Pehlevan (SEAS/applied math) and Sellke (stat).

If you believe scaling laws are the most important applied science of the LLM era (the position of Jared Kaplan, Sam McCandlish, Dario Amodei), then Pehlevan/Bordelon are the theoreticians and Tanishq is the rising empiricist who can implement at scale
