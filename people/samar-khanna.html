Samar Khanna

The rare researcher who made “diffusion + foundation models for real-world generalization” a career thesis — and first-authored the proof at each modality jump: SatMAE (the standard satellite-imagery backbone) → DiffusionSat (the first generative foundation model for satellite imagery) → now Mercury, the first commercial diffusion LLM. First author of the Mercury technical report. Status: founding ML research scientist at Inception (Stefano Ermon’s diffusion-LLM startup, $50M seed). Stanford MS CS (Ermon) / Cornell BS. Indian-origin (Mumbai / DAIS).

◆ The Alpha — what’s actually special, measured against the field

The through-line is the alpha. Most people will read him as “the Mercury guy” or “the satellite-ML guy” — both miss it. His actual thesis, in his own words, is “making ML models generalize effectively to new, real-world data” — and he has pursued it by treating diffusion + foundation-model pretraining as a general modeling tool across modalities, first-authoring the field-defining artifact at each jump:

  • Pixels: SatMAE (NeurIPS 2022, co-1st, ~713 cites) — the masked-pretraining backbone the remote-sensing-ML field now benchmarks against; then DiffusionSat (ICLR 2024, sole 1st, ~254 cites) — the first generative foundation model for satellite imagery (conditions on lat/long/timestamp/GSD instead of scarce captions).
  • Domain shift: ExPLoRA (ICML 2025, sole 1st) — extended pretraining that moves a ViT across a domain gap at <10% of params, +up to 8% top-1. This is literally his stated mission as a method.
  • Language: Mercury (arXiv 2506.17298, 1st author) — Inception’s commercial diffusion LLM. Same person, same diffusion-as-general-tool instinct, two years after DiffusionSat.

The non-obvious edge: he holds both muscles at once — foundation-model-pretraining-under-domain-shift and discrete-diffusion generation — and Mercury is exactly their intersection (a Transformer that denoises many tokens in parallel rather than autoregressing left-to-right). Very few people have first-authored across that whole arc.

Calibrated honestly: the research spike is real and first-authored. Mercury’s throughput claims are real (~1,109 tok/s on H100, “up to 10× faster”) but carry the honest dLLM catch — weak on hard reasoning (LiveCodeBench 17–25), so the pitch is “SOTA on the speed-quality frontier,” not “best model.” His ~1.8K Scholar cites are partly inflated by middle-author papers (DDBM 3rd; “LLMs are Geographically Biased” 2nd) — but the first-authored portion alone (~1,000+ cites) is the attributable spike. No PhD; the first-author ICLR/NeurIPS run landed during/right after his MS.

What He’s Excited About + Working On Now

  • The standing thesis (verbatim, his site): “making ML models generalize effectively to new, real-world data, especially to solve societally relevant tasks such as sustainability.” The sustainability application has receded; the generalization-of-generative-models engine got redirected into language.
  • His sharpest current conviction (verbatim, pinned tweet): “Diffusion reasoning models enable a meaningfully different design space: you can spend test-time compute not just on generating more tokens, but also refining them in parallel. Most reasoning models think one step at a time. Diffusion changes this.”
  • The hill he’ll defend (verbatim, quote-replying Noam Brown): “performance per unit of time is a key metric of intelligence” — dLLMs can spend more test-time compute within the same wall-clock. That he picked this fight with Mr. test-time-compute himself tells you it’s what most animates him.
  • Still curiosity-driven, not just product: co-author on “Squeezed Diffusion Models” (Aug 2025) — anisotropic noise along the data’s principal component, inspired by quantum squeezed states / Heisenberg uncertainty. He does theory-flavored diffusion on the side.
  • Working on now (CONFIRMED): Mercury 2 (“first reasoning dLLM”), Mercury Edit 2, the Mercury Coder family; amplifying Inception’s “real-time subagents” thesis (diffusion owns the high-frequency subagent layer — routing, summarizing, context-prep). INFERRED: parallel test-time reasoning for diffusion (his pinned-tweet frontier); possibly a multimodal dLLM (his DiffusionSat pedigree) — unconfirmed, pose as a question.
