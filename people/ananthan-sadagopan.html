◆ The Alpha — what’s actually special, measured against the field

The field’s default verb is degrade — PROTACs and molecular glues (Arvinas, Monte Rosa, Kymera; the Crews/Fischer/Nomura/Gray labs) use induced proximity to destroy a target. Sadagopan’s edge is that he treats proximity as a way to control a protein’s fate and location — relocalize it, switch a gene’s state, or exploit a target rather than remove it — and he first-authored the cleverest example.

The flagship inversion (Nat Chem Biol, co-1st author). TP53 is the most-mutated gene in cancer and the canonical “undruggable” tumor suppressor — 45 years of failed attempts to fix it. His move: don’t fix it, don’t degrade it — use the fact that the mutation makes p53 abnormally pile up as a homing beacon. A bifunctional molecule binds the p53^Y220C hotspot mutant and forms a p53^Y220C–PLK1 ternary complex, concentrating a PLK1 (mitotic-kinase) poison inside TP53-mutant cells — selective killing, wild-type cells spared. Abundance-as-an-AND-gate. That’s a genuine conceptual twist on the whole induced-proximity paradigm, aimed at the field’s whitest whale.

The toolkit breadth is the rest of the signal: relocalization (JACS 2023 — hijack nuclear BRD4 as a “carrier” to drag cytosolic cargo into the nucleus, with quantified import kinetics), a transactivation↔repression toggle (a single molecule that flips a gene’s regulatory state — logic-gated, reversible), and a parallel dry-lab spike: a sole-first-author Cell Systems paper as a college sophomore + an ML “virtual CRISPR screen” (TrPLet) that predicts cancer dependencies from RNA-seq alone.

Calibrated honestly: he is a 2nd-year PhD student; the senior intellectual weight on the flagship is his PI William Gibson + Schreiber/Meyerson (he and Gibson “contributed equally,” he holds the first slot and did the synthesis + molecular biology). The novelty is the proximity/payload twist, not the Y220C binder (the PMV chemotype predates it); the paper is 2026-fresh so citations haven’t accrued. His ~1.3K Scholar cites are partly inflated by middle-author papers (DeepDEP; the Cell 2024 tRCC paper, 2nd author). But a sole-first-author Cell Systems paper as a sophomore + co-first Nat Chem Biol + the chemistry in his own hands is what separates him from pedigree-only.
