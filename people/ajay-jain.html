The Spike — With Evidence

One sentence: Ajay Jain co-authored the paper that birthed modern generative AI (DDPM), then co-founded a company that ships the largest open-source descendant of his own paper (Mochi 1), with his brother as CEO and Pieter Abbeel, Joseph Gonzalez, and Ion Stoica all listed as Genmo advisors.

1. DDPM (NeurIPS 2020) — the foundational paper

Ho, Jain, Abbeel. 40,013 citations on Google Scholar (May 2026 retrieval). Approximately 86% of Ajay’s 46,282 total citations come from this single paper. h-index 13, i10-index 15 — the citation distribution is bimodal: one foundational hit + a series of well-cited follow-ups. DDPM is the work that turned latent variable diffusion from a curiosity (Sohl-Dickstein 2015) into the dominant generative paradigm. Every major image/video generation system in production today — Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 2/3, Midjourney, Imagen, Sora, Veo, Runway Gen-3, Luma Dream Machine, Kling, Pika, Mochi 1 — is a descendant of this paper.

2. DreamFusion (Google Research, 2022) — text-to-3D era opener

Poole, Jain, Barron, Mildenhall. 3,753 citations. Introduced Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) — the technique that allowed a 2D diffusion prior to optimize a 3D NeRF without 3D training data. This kicked off the entire text-to-3D research wave (Magic3D, ProlificDreamer, Zero-1-to-3, GS-Dreamer, etc.). DreamFusion was the cover paper of the text-to-3D moment.

3. Genmo + Mochi 1 (Oct 22, 2024) — productizing his own lineage

Mochi 1 is a 10B-parameter diffusion model trained from scratch on a novel Asymmetric Diffusion Transformer (AsymmDiT) architecture. Released Apache 2.0. Largest open-source video generation model ever released. Genmo simultaneously announced a $28.4M Series A led by NEA (Rick Yang lead) with WndrCo, The House Fund, Gold House Ventures, Eastlink Capital Partners, Essence VC, and angels Abhay Parasnis, Amjad Masad, Sabrina Hahn, Bonita Stewart, Michele Catasta. ~1.5M active users on Genmo Playground at time of launch.

Mochi 1 Technical Deep Dive

Asymmetric Diffusion Transformer (AsymmDiT) is the architectural bet that differentiates Mochi 1 from the rest of the open video generation field.

  • 10B parameters, trained from scratch (not fine-tuned from an image model)
  • Asymmetric design: visual stream has ~4x more parameters than the text stream — separate MLP layers per modality, joined through non-square QKV/output projections
  • Single T5-XXL encoder for the text stream (vs. Pika/Runway-era multi-encoder stacks)
  • 3D rotary positional embeddings (RoPE) with learnable mixing frequencies across space + time
  • Full 3D attention over a 44,520-token context window — jointly reasons over the entire video, not frame-by-frame
  • SwiGLU FFNs, query-key normalization, sandwich normalization (modern stabilization stack)
  • AsymmVAE: custom asymmetric encoder-decoder, 96x video compression (8×8 spatial + 6× temporal) to a 12-channel latent space
  • License: Apache 2.0 — full commercial use, weights downloadable from Hugging Face
  • Output: 480p base, 30fps, up to 5.4s clips. HD 720p targeted late 2025

Why open-source — the Linux strategy

Genmo’s positioning: video models will follow the trajectory of databases (Postgres) and OS (Linux) — the dominant standard ends up open. Mochi 1 + AsymmDiT being Apache 2.0 seeds the developer ecosystem before the closed players (Sora, Veo, Gen-4) can lock in enterprise standards. NEA’s thesis was explicit on this: “NEA has experience scaling open-source companies like Databricks and MongoDB.”

Risks

  • Compute economics. Video models are ~10–100x more expensive to train than image models. $28.4M Series A is thin for the category — Runway raised $300M+ at $3B, Luma $43M at $2B. Genmo will need a Series B within 12–18 months at scale or risk being out-spent
  • Closed-model quality gap. Sora 2, Veo 3, Kling 2.0 are likely ahead on raw quality benchmarks. Mochi 1 wins on openness, not absolute quality. The bet is that openness compounds faster than the closed gap widens
  • No API yet. Paras confirmed Genmo has no developer API as of his Humanloop interview — only Playground. Monetization path = subscription B2C + future API. Slow
  • Apache 2.0 license means competitors can use Mochi 1 weights commercially. The bet is that being the canonical open model gathers ecosystem gravity (like Llama for LLMs)
  • Talent compression. 11–50 employees competing against Sora team (100+), Runway (200+), Luma (100+). Research-heavy team is strength + risk
  • Author identity dilution. Jonathan Ho was 1st author on DDPM; Ben Poole 1st on DreamFusion. Ajay’s credentials are 2nd-author on the two biggest papers in the field — strong but not first chair
  • Brother dynamic risk. Brother-co-founder pairs are the most durable when they work and the most catastrophic when they don’t. No public signal of friction; this is a watch item not a flag
