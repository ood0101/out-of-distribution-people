  • VBGS — Variational Bayes Gaussian Splatting (arXiv 2410.03592, 2024) — 1st author (Van de Maele, Çatal, Tschantz, Buckley, Verbelen). Closed-form conjugate variational update for 3D Gaussian splatting → continual learning from streaming data, no replay buffer. Code at github.com/VersesTech/vbgs. His cleanest, most differentiated spike.
  • AXIOM — object-centric expanding world model (arXiv 2505.24784, May 2025) — 2nd author (Heins 1st; with Pezzato, Çatal, Friston, Verbelen, Buckley). Learns Atari-style games in <10,000 steps, gradient-free. The most literal “think-before-you-act” artifact — maps directly onto auto·motion.
  • A hierarchical active inference model of spatial alternation tasks (hippocampal–prefrontal circuit) — 1st author, Nature Communications 15 (2024), ~26 cites. A genuine first-author Nature-family paper — a stronger credential than the cite count alone implies.
  • Active vision for robot manipulators using the free energy principle — 1st author, Frontiers in Neurorobotics 2021, ~39 cites.
  • Robot navigation as hierarchical active inference — 3rd author, Neural Networks 2021, ~107 cites (his most-cited). Calibration: he is on it, does not lead it.
