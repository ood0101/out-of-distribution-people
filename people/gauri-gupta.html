NeoSigma — What She’s Building

The Product

NeoSigma is an “intelligence layer that closes the loop between customers, products, and AI systems” via custom evaluation and post-training for real-world agents. The website (neosigma.ai) currently shows “Building, Stay tuned” — classic pre-launch stealth. They are actively hiring for 6–8 week internships or part-time roles for researchers/engineers with hands-on post-training experience (infrastructure, data, or algorithms).

The positioning suggests a picks-and-shovels play for the agentic AI era: rather than building agents themselves, NeoSigma would provide the evaluation and post-training infrastructure that every agent company needs. This maps directly to Gauri’s experience: she built Basis (evaluation/grounding) at Parallel, studied decentralized agent collaboration at MIT, and interned on foundation models at Apple.

Technical Problem

LLM post-training is the critical phase between pre-training (expensive, done by frontier labs) and deployment. It includes RLHF, DPO, instruction tuning, evaluation, and alignment. The problem: most companies building AI agents lack the tooling to (1) evaluate whether their agents actually work in the real world, and (2) continuously improve them based on real deployment data. NeoSigma aims to close this loop.

Gap in the market: Evaluation companies (Evidently, Braintrust) measure but don’t improve models. Fine-tuning platforms (Together, Unsloth) improve but don’t evaluate in-the-loop. NeoSigma’s thesis is that evaluation and post-training must be coupled — the eval signal directly drives the training. This is what “closing the loop” means.
