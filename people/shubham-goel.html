HMR 2.0 / 4D-Humans: The Flagship Work

“Humans in 4D: Reconstructing and Tracking Humans with Transformers” (ICCV 2023) is Goel’s highest-impact work. Core contributions:

  • HMR 2.0: A fully “transformerized” network for human mesh recovery using a Vision Transformer backbone + cross-attention decoder. Achieves SOTA on standard benchmarks and handles unusual poses, extreme truncations, and heavy occlusions
  • 4D tracking: Operates in 3D space (not 2D), maintaining identity through occlusion events across multiple people in video
  • Downstream impact: Significant improvements on action recognition benchmarks. Used as backbone by subsequent works (WHAM, VIMO, HSfM)
  • Adoption: 1,600+ GitHub stars, 158 forks. Hugging Face + Google Colab demos. Stability AI provided free HPC compute for this research
