◆ Investment Thesis

Daniil Boiko is the person who, as a CMU PhD student, built and first-authored Coscientist (Nature 2023) — the GPT-4-driven agent that autonomously designed, planned, and executed real chemistry experiments, including reaction optimization of palladium-catalysed cross-couplings on physical lab hardware. That paper (~1,880 citations) is one of the canonical references for the entire autonomous-AI-for-science wave. He has now done the obvious-in-hindsight thing: turned the thesis into a company. Onepot AI came out of stealth on Nov 19, 2025 with $13M (pre-seed + seed, led by Fifty Years), building POT-1 (a small-molecule synthesis lab) and "Phil," an AI organic chemist. The cap table is a tell: Wojciech Zaremba (OpenAI co-founder) and Jeff Dean (Google chief scientist) are angels. Honest caveat: this is a T0 by quality but he is past the inception inflection — the round is done and a frontier-tier syndicate is already in. The Spike — With Evidence

Coscientist (Nature 624, 7992, 570–578, Dec 2023). Author order: D. A. Boiko (first), R. MacKnight, B. Kline, G. Gomes (PI/corresponding). The system coupled GPT-4 to internet/documentation search, code execution, and physical experimental automation, and demonstrated (semi-)autonomous experimental design and execution across six tasks — the headline result being autonomous reaction optimization of palladium-catalysed Suzuki and Sonogashira cross-couplings run on real automated lab equipment, not in simulation. Citations: ~1,880 (Google Scholar, Jun 2026). The open-source repo (github.com/gomesgroup, mirrored on his profile) has ~206 stars.

This is not his only first-author work. He first-authored MEDUSA — "Fully automated unconstrained analysis of high-resolution mass spectrometry data with machine learning" (JACS 2022, ~105 cites) — and co-authored SIMG ("stereoelectronics-infused molecular graphs," Nature Machine Intelligence, 2025), a representation-learning paper injecting quantum-chemical structure into molecular GNNs. Aggregate: ~2,562 citations, h-index 15, i10-index 22 — unusually high for someone who was until recently a PhD student, almost entirely driven by the one Nature paper but with a real spine of first-author ML-for-chemistry behind it.

The throughline is consistent and rare: he is a real organic chemist who became a real ML engineer, and has spent his entire research life on the exact problem Onepot now sells — using agents and ML to compress the synthesis bottleneck. His own framing of why this matters (Onepot launch): "The best ideas in drug discovery were often blocked not by biology, but by synthesis."
