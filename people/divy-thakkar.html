◆ The Spike

Divy Thakkar is the rare person who operates simultaneously in three registers that almost never overlap: business strategy (MBA from India’s premier international business school, corporate competition wins at L’Oreal/ITC/RPG, angel investing), deep HCI/AI research (PhD, CHI Best Paper, CSCW Honorable Mention, 364+ citations), and institution-building at civilizational scale (founding member of both Google Research India and Google DeepMind India, strategic lead for Gemini foundational science investments).

The SAHELI system is the proof of concept for this combination: an AI system using Restless Multi-Armed Bandits for maternal and child health, deployed with NGO ARMMAN, reaching 130K+ beneficiaries and targeting 1M. It won the IAAI Best Innovative Application Award. This is not a paper project — it is a production system saving lives, built by someone who understands both the ML and the go-to-market.

Now at DeepMind Mountain View, he leads strategic research engagements for Gemini and was acknowledged in the Gemini Deep Think paper (IMO gold-medal standard) and the Aletheia system that solved open Erdős problems. His current focus: “Building the next interaction and collaboration paradigm for advanced AI systems” — human-AGI collaboration. The through-line from vocational workers in Bangalore to maternal health in rural India to the future of human-AGI interaction is a moral and intellectual compass that most AI researchers lack.

Background

Divy Thakkar is from India (likely Gujarat). B.Tech in Computer Science from DA-IICT (Dhirubhai Ambani Institute, Gandhinagar, Gujarat, 2011–2015). Active in college festivals, student government, and debating. MBA in International Business from the Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT), New Delhi (2015–2017) — India’s premier trade and international business school. Won corporate case competitions from L’Oreal, ITC, and RPG during MBA. Exchange semester at Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. Interned at eBay India (brand management).

Joined Google post-MBA and became a founding member of Google Research India in Bangalore. Led strategy & operations for research and education programs. Helped establish what became a world-class research lab. Completed a PhD in Computer Science at City St George’s, University of London (2022–2024) — fully funded by Google, completed while a full-time employee. Advisors: Alex Taylor (University of Edinburgh) and Anoop Sinha (Google).

In 2024, helped establish Google DeepMind India. Moved to Mountain View in September 2024 as Staff Program Manager & Researcher at Google DeepMind. Led Foundational Science Research Investments for Gemini (foundation models, evals, systems, AI for Math). Acknowledged in the Gemini Deep Think paper (IMO gold-medal standard) and Aletheia (solved open Erdős problems). Now focused on human-AGI collaboration. Actively angel invests in and advises AI startups.

The Overlooked Angle

The standard read is “Google researcher who does AI for social good.” That misses the structural rarity of the profile. Thakkar has an MBA from India’s top trade school AND a PhD funded by Google AND was a founding member of two major Google labs in India AND now has strategic influence over Gemini. The business + research + institution-building trifecta at ~32 is almost unheard of.

The SAHELI deployment is the most concrete proof: a production AI system saving lives at 130K+ scale, published at IAAI, that he co-built with one of the world’s top AI-for-social-good researchers (Milind Tambe). Most people in AI talk about impact. Thakkar has 130K beneficiaries to point to.

The move from Bangalore to Mountain View signals the next phase: from building India’s AI research infrastructure to shaping the human-AGI interaction paradigm at the frontier. His acknowledged role in Gemini Deep Think (IMO gold-medal math) and Aletheia (open Erdős problems) shows he has access to and influence over DeepMind’s most ambitious research directions. The angel investing activity suggests he’s building toward something beyond Google.
