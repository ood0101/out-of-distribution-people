The Adam optimizer (2015, with Kingma) is the default training algorithm for essentially every large language model. Layer Normalization (2016, with Kiros and Hinton) is a core building block inside every transformer. These are not influential papers — they are infrastructure. Background

Jimmy Lei Ba is Canadian, now based in San Francisco. He completed his entire academic career at the University of Toronto:

  • BSc in Computer Science (2011) — supervised by Brendan Frey and Ruslan Salakhutdinov
  • MSc in Electrical & Computer Engineering (2014) — supervised by Frey and Salakhutdinov
  • PhD in Computer Science (2018) — supervised by Geoffrey Hinton. Dissertation: “Learning to Attend with Neural Networks”

During his PhD, he interned at Google DeepMind, Microsoft Research, and Meta FAIR. He received the Facebook Graduate Fellowship (2016).

After graduating, he joined UofT as Assistant Professor (2018) and was granted tenure as Associate Professor in July 2024. He is a member of the Vector Institute, holds a CIFAR AI Chair, and was named a Sloan Research Fellow in 2023.

In July 2023, he co-founded xAI with Elon Musk and 11 others. At xAI he led research, safety, and enterprise divisions. He helped scale the Colossus supercomputer to 200,000 H100 GPUs and optimized Grok’s training pipeline at that scale. He departed on February 10, 2026, reportedly after friction with Musk following the SpaceX acquisition of xAI. He is the 6th of 12 original co-founders to leave.

The Overlooked Angle

The standard framing is “xAI co-founder leaves.” That misses the real story. Jimmy Ba is not primarily an xAI person — he is one of approximately 20 people whose research is literally embedded in the foundation layer of modern AI. Adam and LayerNorm are not influential papers; they are infrastructure. They run every time any LLM on Earth processes a token.

The other overlooked dimension is the lineage. Ba was trained by Geoffrey Hinton, who won the 2024 Nobel Prize. He was trained alongside Ilya Sutskever (also a Hinton student). The Hinton tree is arguably the most consequential advisor tree in AI history, and Ba is one of its highest-citation nodes.

What makes the current moment interesting: Ba has both the foundational research credibility (313K citations, infrastructure-level papers) and the operational experience of scaling to 200K GPUs at xAI. Very few people in the world have both. His signals about recursive self-improvement and MPI optimization suggest he is building at the intersection of these two capabilities — which is exactly where the next unlock in AI training is likely to come from.
