The Spike — With Evidence

First-author ICML 2025 workshop oral as a Harvard undergraduate. The paper “Generalizing Trust: Weak-to-Strong Trustworthiness in Language Models” was accepted as an oral presentation at two ICML 2025 workshops (MOSS and CFAgentic). It introduces “weak-to-strong trustworthiness” — the first work to study whether stronger LLMs can inherit trustworthiness properties (fairness, robustness, privacy) when fine-tuned on weaker model labels. Key result: fairness, adversarial robustness, and OOD robustness transfer well; privacy does not. Co-authored with Martin Pawelczyk, Zhenting Qi, Aounon Kumar, and advisor Himabindu Lakkaraju (Harvard Business School / SEAS).

Clarification: This paper did not win the ICML 2025 Outstanding Paper Award. The 6 Outstanding Paper Awards went to different papers (Kim/Shah et al., Nagarajan et al., Givens/Liu/Reeve, Wu et al., Fischer Abaigar et al., Snell/Griffiths). Sun’s paper was a workshop oral — still a strong signal for an undergraduate first-author, but the distinction matters.

Second ICML 2025 paper: “DP-AdamW: Investigating Decoupled Weight Decay and Bias Correction in Private Deep Learning” (DIG-BUGS Workshop). Proposes DP-AdamW-BC, a differentially private AdamW variant that outperforms DP-SGD, DP-Adam, and DP-AdamBC by 1–15% across tasks. Co-authored with HUQT peers Jay Chooi, Kevin Cong, and Russell Li.

HRT AI Labs (HAIL): As Algorithm Developer Intern (Summer 2025), worked on “systematic equities — trained diffusion models and developed trading strategies using large language models.” This is production ML at one of the most technically demanding quant firms, not just research.
