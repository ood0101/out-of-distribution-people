Background

Jaroslaw “Jerry” Tworek is Polish, now based in San Francisco. He studied Applied Mathematics at the University of Warsaw (BSc 2008–2012, MSc 2012–2013). He has no PhD.

His early career moved through finance and graphics: an equity derivatives structuring internship at JP Morgan (2012), a stint as quantitative analyst at Decura (2013), and a role in realtime graphics at Platige Image (2013). He then spent approximately 5 years (2014–2019) as a senior quantitative researcher at Independent View, a hedge fund in Amsterdam. This quant background — derivatives pricing, stochastic processes, optimization under uncertainty — directly informed his later work on reasoning models.

He joined OpenAI as a Research Scientist in 2019 and rose to VP of Research by 2022. Over 7 years, he was central to an extraordinary sequence of products: the Rubik’s Cube robot hand, Codex, GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, GPT-4, ChatGPT Plugins, Code Interpreter, o1, o3, and GPT-5. He was known internally as the “Father of Reasoning Models” (for the o-series) and the “Father of Code” (for Codex/Copilot).

He left OpenAI in January 2026 to found Core Automation.

The Overlooked Angle

The standard narrative is “senior OpenAI researcher starts a company.” That undersells what’s actually happening. Tworek’s background is not a typical ML researcher trajectory. He has a math degree and 5 years of quantitative trading before ever touching AI research. The quant world teaches you to think in terms of optimization under uncertainty, stochastic processes, and rigorous mathematical modeling — skills that map directly onto reasoning model design.

The product shipping record is the real anomaly. Most researchers ship one landmark result in a career. Tworek was central to nine in seven years, spanning robotics (Rubik’s Cube), code generation (Codex, Copilot), conversational AI (ChatGPT), foundation models (GPT-4, GPT-5), and reasoning (o1, o3). That breadth of execution is nearly unprecedented.

The Core Automation thesis is the most ambitious signal: rethinking optimization “up to and including gradient descent” and building architectures beyond transformers. This is not an incremental startup. This is someone with the deepest possible knowledge of current LLM limitations betting that the entire paradigm needs to change. The $5B+ valuation target for a pre-product company reflects the market’s assessment of that knowledge.
