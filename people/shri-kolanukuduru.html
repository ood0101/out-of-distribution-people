◆ Why Shri — Co-Investor / Deal-Flow Peer

Shri is one of a small number of partners at a sub-$250M enterprise-software-focused fund who (a) has actually shipped code, (b) has already done day-0 investing (Pioneer’s $20K/2% program — literally pre-seed of pre-seed), and (c) has an articulated preference for engineer-founders building deeply technical software. His writing makes the taste explicit: he looks for technical conviction, is allergic to formulaic pattern-matching, and evaluates opportunities with a three-part frame (good problem, intuitive solution, early traction) rather than credential heuristics.

The stage overlap is clean, not competitive. Category writes $1.5M–$5M seed checks (avg ~$3M, ~25 investments per fund, per Villi on the Venture Unlocked pod). An inception-stage fund writing $500K–$1M at day-0 sits exactly one step earlier in the funnel. That’s the textbook upstream relationship: we find the team pre-idea or pre-product, Category leads the seed 12–18 months later. Shri’s Pioneer experience means he understands the day-0 motion — he was doing it himself two years ago.

The ask is not capital. It’s: (1) deal-flow swap on technical AI/infra/dev-tools founders, (2) co-invest on rounds where Category leads seed and we want to pre-position at inception, (3) introductions into the Villi Iltchev / Two Sigma Ventures / ex-Salesforce Ventures network, (4) potentially him writing a personal angel check on a specific deal. Frame this as peer-to-peer, not as asking a favor.

Villi’s investing philosophy (relevant context for Shri’s taste)

Meeting Prep

◆ What to Lead With (this is a peer meeting)
  • Specific day-0 founder you both would recognize. Name 1–2 technical founders on the current pipeline — ideally ones who fit Category’s dev-tools/AI-infra seed thesis — and say who’s committed, who’s still open. This proves you’re a real source before anything else
  • Pioneer reference. “I read your Joining Pioneer post — the $20K/2% and 1% + SF flyout — that’s literally the stage we’re at. We’re the institutional version of what Pioneer did for day-0 founders.” Instant shared language
  • Klima DAO rubric. His own public rubric is: good problem, intuitive solution, early traction. Pitch any deal he’d want to see through that frame. Not accidentally — deliberately
  • Engineer-founders thesis alignment. His Category bio and Insight bio both emphasize “deeply technical software” and “engineer-founders.” That’s the fund’s exact thesis — frontier-lab departures, competition winners, researchers turning founders. Show him that pipeline
◆ The Four Collaboration Angles
  1. Deal-flow swap. Explicit exchange — he sees technical founders too early for Category ($1.5M+ check floor); we see founders too late for Category (post-seed, needing Series A). Trade them. Concrete cadence: monthly 30-minute sync with live pipeline docs shared both ways
  2. Follow-on pre-positioning. When we lead pre-seed at $500K–$1M, show Category 3–6 months in. Category is an ideal seed lead at $3M average check — natural graduation path. Clear signal that we’re not stage-conflict
  3. Co-invest at seed. When Category leads a $4M seed, we take $250K–$500K pro-rata. Our technical-founder diligence (competition winners, lab alumni tracking) adds something they can’t easily buy — we’ve been watching these people for years
  4. Personal angel. Some partners write personal checks into rounds their fund can’t do (too early, outside mandate). Pioneer-era Shri wrote $20K checks himself. Ask directly: does he angel? Would he look at the pipeline for personal conviction bets?
◆ What to Avoid
  • Don’t pitch him as an LP. Category is a $160M early-stage fund, not a fund-of-funds. Villi is allergic to the LP-from-GP framing (“seed funds are the right seed investors”). Don’t even imply it. This meeting is peer-to-peer
  • Don’t open with fund-thesis paragraphs. He’s on the other side of that table 20 times a week. Open with a specific founder and a specific question
  • Don’t over-index on credentials. His own writing is anti-credential — “non-target school,” “your network is your net worth.” Don’t hype Stanford/MIT resumes. Hype specific technical work
  • Don’t ignore the HFT / crypto / poker signals. They’re telling you his cognitive style: quantitative, edge-seeking, comfortable with asymmetric bets. Talk in that register
  • Don’t pretend to know Villi. If you haven’t met Villi, say so. Ask for the intro if relevant. Shri is the relationship now — Villi can come later

Collaboration Angles — Specific Shapes

Shape 1: Deal-flow swap (lowest friction)

Shared Airtable or Notion. 10–15 founders per month each direction. Tagged by sector (AI infra / dev tools / security / apps) and stage (day-0 / pre-seed / seed). Monthly call to triage. Zero economic exchange — pure relationship. This is the baseline and should be settable in the first meeting.

Shape 2: Structured co-investment at seed

When Category leads a $3M–$5M seed, an inception-stage fund participates at $250K–$500K pro-rata. Terms: we got there first, we have ownership floor, we maintain our position through their seed. Reserves-friendly. Shri would want to know: do we have a signaling problem if we pass on something he wants to lead? Answer in advance: our pass reasons are stage-fit, not quality. Document that.

Shape 3: Personal angel pre-seed

Offer Shri first look at pre-seed rounds (sub-$2M) where Category as a fund can’t play. He writes $25K–$100K personal check. His conviction signal + his Pioneer-era day-0 muscle would be valuable. Only pitch this once rapport is established — Shape 1 first, this in meeting 2 or 3.

Shape 4: Villi introduction (asymmetric value)

If Shape 1 goes well, ask to meet Villi. Villi is ex-Salesforce Ventures (Airtable, Box, Gusto, HubSpot, MuleSoft, Zapier), ex-August Capital (GitLab, SendBird), ex-Two Sigma Ventures. His network is 10+ years deeper than Shri’s. LP prospect in a future fund? Possibly — but don’t frame it that way. Frame it as two-way operator-network access.
