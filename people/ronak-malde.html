◆ The Spike

Ronak Malde is one of a very small number of people who has trained frontier software engineering models from scratch at startup scale. At Windsurf (Codeium), he trained SWE-1, SWE-1-lite, and SWE-1-mini — models that competed with outputs from labs 100x their size. Google then acquired Windsurf’s talent in a ~$2.4B deal, and Malde moved to Google DeepMind specifically for his post-training expertise, where he now works on RL post-training for agentic coding (Gemini).

The combination is rare: startup-scale model training (knows what it takes to ship with constrained resources) + DeepMind RL credentialing (frontier post-training techniques) + Stanford MS (deep RL, generative models, diffusion) + a stated lifelong ambition to be an entrepreneur. Named on Exceptional Capital’s Exceptional 100 (2025) — a list specifically designed to identify future founders. His 2026 thesis: “2026 will be the year of continual learning.” That could be the seed of a company.
