◆ Investment Thesis — T0 (Active Pursuit)

Emmett Bicker is an out-of-distribution profile in the most literal sense: a ~19-year-old who dropped out of college three weeks in, spent a year as a frontier-lab RL researcher, and now runs a one-person AI research lab whose pitch is autonomous scientific discovery. The rate of change is exactly what we index on — high-schooler → Nature Communications co-author → Magic post-training researcher → solo-author arXiv founder in ~24 months. The talent signal is real and dated, not a launch-thread invention (see Origin — a 2021 GitHub repo learning matrix multiplication “in a machine learning course” at 14; a 2024 ThoughtsFormer repo that prefigures the exact Aster reasoning thesis at 17).

The honest caveat — price it, don’t ignore it. Aster’s headline claims run measurably hot, which matters for a fund whose discipline is “evidence over claims.” Of five SOTA claims in the sole-authored, non-peer-reviewed arXiv paper: one is independently confirmed (a kernel record merged into Keller Jordan’s canonical modded-nanogpt repo, and reportedly listed among METR’s “real improvements”) — but it was revised down ~4× (−1.6s claimed → −0.4s realized); one is verifiably not SOTA (the TriMul kernel: the public GPU-MODE leaderboard shows K-Search at 1030µs and a plain CUDA entry at 1074µs both beating Aster’s 1114µs, with Aster absent from the official ranking); and three are self-reported with zero released code (his public GitHub has 0 repos). This isn’t fraud — there’s a real, adjudicated win underneath — it’s normal founder hype, but it should be priced in.

Two of the owner’s stated preferences create the friction. (1) Aster is YC Spring 2026 — and the playbook says YC cohorts are “too late for us; we invest at inception.” Counter: solo / team-of-1 / four months old is the most inception-stage a YC company can be, and the round is happening now. (2) The claims-run-hot pattern is exactly what the “numbers over adjectives” rule is meant to catch. Net read: back-the-person conviction is high (would absolutely want a relationship with this human today), but the deal is already at YC stage and the company’s core thesis — autonomy — is its least-verified claim. Owner call (Jun 2026): T0 — pursue now, co-investing alongside YC and pricing the hype. Move on the YC-round clock, not a monthly cadence; resolve the autonomy + reproducibility questions in the first conversation.

◆ The Read — interpretation, not fact

Calibration first (the most decision-relevant honesty signal). The pattern across every checkable claim is the same: a real result, overstated. The kernel record is real but 4× smaller than announced; the TriMul “SOTA” is contradicted by the public leaderboard (the paper’s abstract claims blanket SOTA while its body only beats one baseline, TTT-Discover); the splashiest claims (Erdős, scRNA-seq, ZAPBench) have no released code and no independent reproduction. Three independent adversarial verifiers all confirmed this skeptical read. Crucially, the one confirmed result is human-co-credited — so “autonomy,” the entire pitch, is the single least-verified thing about the company.

Our read on the human: a continuously-building autodidact whose defining trait is rate of acquisition — taught himself ML at ~14, CUDA and paper-reimplementation at 17–18, competition piano from a cold COVID start in ~2 years, and ran a 1,000-volunteer youth-CS nonprofit’s operations before turning 18. The ThoughtsFormer→Aster throughline shows the reasoning-agent idea is a line he’s pulled for years, not a pivot to the hype cycle. Why it matters: this is a genuinely out-of-distribution person, and the talent is the asset; the company’s claims and the YC stage are the discounts. Falsifiers / what would move him to T0: released, reproducible benchmark code; a clear demonstration of the autonomy frontier (how much Aster did vs. how much he steered); or evidence of a pre-seed round where a $500K–$1M check lands at true inception terms. What would move him down: if the autonomy claims stay unfalsifiable and the SOTA pattern keeps running hot.
