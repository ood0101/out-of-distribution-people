The Spike — With Evidence

Singh is the builder half of the Kiela–Singh partnership. Where Kiela is the research-visionary public face (RAG’s co-inventor), Singh is the person who ships the system. His most-cited fingerprint is across three eras:

1. FAIR multimodal (2018–2020) — the framework + the benchmark. He is the author and long-time maintainer of MMF (originally Pythia), “a modular framework for vision & language multimodal research from FAIR” — 5.6k GitHub stars, the de-facto codebase for a generation of VQA work; Pythia won the VQA 2018 challenge. He is 1st author of TextVQA (“Towards VQA Models That Can Read,” CVPR 2019, 2,344 cites) and 1st author of FLAVA (“A Foundational Language And Vision Alignment model,” CVPR 2022, 1,245 cites, last author Douwe Kiela). Also on Hateful Memes (Kiela et al., NeurIPS 2020), Winoground, TextCaps. This is a genuine 1st-author research record, not just an engineering credit.

2. NYU / benchmarks (pre-FAIR) — the eval DNA. 2nd author on GLUE (ICLR 2019, 10,830 cites, his single most-cited paper) and SuperGLUE (3,479 cites), the benchmarks that defined the BERT era of NLU. Also CoLA / “Neural Network Acceptability Judgments” (1,949 cites). The eval/benchmark instinct visible here recurs 6 years later as LMUnit at Contextual.

3. Hugging Face → Contextual (2021–present) — open models, RAG, GRIT. Co-author of BLOOM (176B open multilingual LM, 2,484 cites), OBELICS (open interleaved image-text web corpus, 2023, ~497 cites), GritLM / Generative Representational Instruction Tuning (2024, ~326 cites — one model that is SOTA at both generation and embedding; authors incl. Niklas Muennighoff … Amanpreet Singh, Douwe Kiela, confirming this is our Aman), and OLMoE (open MoE, ~258 cites, with the Ai2/Muennighoff open-models group).

What He Built / Owned at Contextual (CTO worldview, in his words)

From the Weaviate Podcast #114 (~58 min, ~early 2025 — references DeepSeek R1/R0 & OpenAI Operator), the single best “you can hear him think” asset. His two founding mottos:

  • “System over models” — “we strongly believe that single model will not be able to serve the full system… you need a system to solve actual problems… they’re working in conjunction with each other and then they improve together.”
  • “Specialization for AGI, specifically for Enterprises” — “even a general agent… has to learn if it goes into the enterprise… specialization matters for enterprises a lot because they’re sitting on a lot of in-domain data that is not available otherwise.”

His recurring intellectual obsession — the seed of whatever he builds next — is the verification–generation asymmetry as the engine of progress:

  • “Even as a human, verification of the answer is easier than… saying things step by step… this imbalance helps us continue moving the field forward.”
  • On why RAG must learn in production: “in production you want the model to learn automatically from the feedback it’s getting… you want the full system to improve.” (This is the “RAG 2.0” / weight-updating thesis.)
  • On the gap in test-time compute: “you can do reasoning, but are you learning something from that reasoning? next time you do that reasoning you can do faster… the model weights are not really changing.” — note the near-overlap with the continual-learning thesis several other T0/T1 names in this repo are chasing.

Products/components he ran or shipped at Contextual: the RAG platform itself, LMUnit (natural-language unit tests for LLM eval — “a credit report of the model”), KTO-style preference optimization, groundedness & conflict-resolution models trained in-house. Engineering ownership of the full retrieve–rank–generate–align stack is his lane.
