The Spike — With Evidence

His ICML 2025 paper “Train for the Worst, Plan for the Best” (co-first author with Kulin Shah) solved a fundamental question: why do masked diffusion models underperform autoregressive models despite their flexibility? The answer: MDMs face an exponentially large set of fill-in-the-blank subproblems during training, many of which are computationally intractable. But at inference time, by adaptively choosing which token to unmask first based on model confidence, MDMs can sidestep these hard subproblems entirely.

The numbers are striking: on Sudoku puzzles, adaptive decoding boosted MDM accuracy from <7% to ~90%, outperforming autoregressive models with 7x as many parameters that were explicitly trained with teacher forcing to learn the correct decoding order. Similar gains appeared on Zebra puzzles and language modeling perplexity.

This is not an incremental result. It was recognized as 1 of 6 Outstanding Papers from 12,000 submissions (top 0.05%). ICML reviewers cited “clarity, insight, creativity, and potential for lasting impact.” The paper has 109 citations as of March 2026 — extraordinary for a paper published in July 2025.

He also had a second ICML 2025 oral (LoRA theory) in the same conference — 2 orals at ICML in a single year is extremely rare for any researcher, let alone a first-year PhD student.

What He’d Build

The Masked Diffusion Thesis

Jaeyeon Kim is building the theoretical and practical foundations for masked diffusion models as an alternative to autoregressive generation. His research arc tells a clear story:

  • Understanding MDMs (ICML 2025): Why they struggle at training, how to fix inference
  • Flexible-length MDMs (ICLR 2026): Removing the fixed-length constraint that limits practical deployment
  • Self-correction (PRISM, 2025): MDMs that detect and revise low-quality tokens at inference time
  • Faster training (Progressive Unmasking, 2026): Making MDM training practical at scale

Each paper fills a gap in the MDM production stack. If he decided to commercialize, the natural company would be a diffusion-based language model lab — building MDMs that can rival GPT/Claude-class models with faster inference, parallel decoding, and bidirectional context.
