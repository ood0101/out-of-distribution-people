◆ The Spike

Zeeshan Patel was the first hire on xAI’s video generation team. He built Grok Imagine from scratch in approximately 3 months. It became, by xAI’s own account, “the most used video model in the world, generating millions of videos per day.” He was 21. He left after 9 months to go stealth.

What makes this exceptional is the stack beneath it. Patel didn’t just ship fast — he came from the deepest possible academic pedigree in computer vision. His MS thesis at Berkeley was supervised by Alexei Efros (pioneer of visual self-supervised learning) and Jitendra Malik (one of the most cited CV researchers alive). His thesis on scaling diffusion models for perceptual tasks was accepted at CVPR 2025. Before that, he was Tech Lead on NVIDIA’s video foundation model training pipeline (NeMo) and co-authored the Cosmos world model paper that was covered by the NYT, WSJ, Forbes, and BBC.

The pattern: elite theoretical foundations (Efros + Malik, diffusion scaling laws, CVPR) combined with proven ability to build from zero to millions-of-daily-users production systems (Grok Imagine), all compressed into ~4 years from undergrad to stealth founder. He also founded an education nonprofit at 15, scored perfectly on USACO Silver, and was doing neuroscience deep learning research at Berkeley while still in high school. The velocity is the signal.

Background

Zeeshan Patel grew up in Fremont, California and attended Irvington High School. By senior year, he had 6+ years of programming experience (Python, Java, JS, C++), scored in the top 5% on AMC 12A, and achieved a perfect 1000 on USACO Silver (January 2021). While still in high school, he was a research intern at UC Berkeley’s MCB Department (Adesnik Berkeley Center lab), developing deep learning tools for 3D cell membrane segmentation.

At 15, he founded Coding Cosines, a STEM nonprofit that provided free education to 250+ students regardless of socioeconomic background. His students went on to qualify for Math Kangaroo nationals, AMC 8 honor roll, and win Bay Area hackathons. The nonprofit was featured on the Bay Area Beat podcast.

UC Berkeley for undergrad (2021–2024): dual B.S. in Computer Science & Statistics, graduated with honors. Taught CS 170 (Algorithms) and CS 70 (Discrete Math & Probability). Then Berkeley EECS MS (2024–2025) under Alexei Efros and Jitendra Malik at BAIR. Thesis: “Scaling Properties of Diffusion Models for Perceptual Tasks” (CVPR 2025).

Career trajectory: Apple AI/ML (intern, foundation models) → NVIDIA Research (Cosmos world model, NeMo Tech Lead) → xAI (first hire on video team, built Grok Imagine, May 2025 – Jan 2026) → Stealth (Jan 2026–present). Delta Institute Cohort 1 fellow.

The Overlooked Angle

The narrative is “NVIDIA researcher builds xAI’s video model, leaves to start something.” That misses the structural pattern. Patel sits at the exact intersection of three capabilities that almost never coexist in one person: deep theoretical grounding (Efros + Malik, diffusion scaling laws, CVPR), big-lab infrastructure knowledge (NVIDIA NeMo, Cosmos, large-scale training pipelines), and proven 0-to-millions product shipping (Grok Imagine in 3 months).

Most researchers can’t ship. Most builders can’t publish at CVPR. Patel does both, and the timeline compression makes it more unusual: he went from undergrad to first-author NVIDIA paper to building xAI’s most-used product to stealth founder in about 4 years. The Coding Cosines nonprofit at 15 is also a tell — most technical prodigies don’t build education infrastructure as teenagers. The combination of shipping velocity, research depth, and community-building instinct at this age is the real outlier.

The 9-month xAI tenure is worth reading carefully. He didn’t leave because the product failed — it became the most-used video model in the world. He left because he could build something better on his own. That’s the conviction signal.
