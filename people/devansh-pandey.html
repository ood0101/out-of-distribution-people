The Spike

Devansh Pandey’s career reads like a sequence of premature commitments that turned out to be exactly right. He dropped out of a Bay Area high school to become the first employee of the Atlas Fellowship — a program selecting 111 students from 7,000+ applicants to work on existential risk and AI safety. He didn’t just answer emails. He ran infrastructure, managed admissions, and co-founded Atlas Academy, a small high school for fellows who, like him, had opted out of the conventional path. Then he went to Oxford, where instead of disappearing into tutorials he founded the Bread Society and helped run the Taylor Swift Society — the kind of person who builds community as a reflex. He organized HackUBA, a 20-person weeklong hackathon in rural Japan. Now he’s co-founding Standard Intelligence with Galen Mead, building models for general computer use. The pattern: he shows up before the institution exists, builds the scaffolding that lets everyone else do their best work, then moves to the next frontier. He’s the operational backbone of every ambitious project he touches.

Background

Dropped out of high school in the Bay Area to join the Atlas Fellowship as its first employee in 2022. The Atlas Fellowship — co-founded by Sydney Von Arx, Ashley Lin, and Jonas Vollmer — ran five 11-day programs in Berkeley and Oxford for 111 fellows from 18 countries, selected from over 7,000 applicants (1.6% acceptance). Backed by Open Philanthropy and the Future Fund.

Even before dropping out, the institution-building instinct was visible: he hosted Gavel Talk, a Model UN podcast (7 episodes) while still in high school. The format — a teenager producing a niche podcast about competitive debate — is a miniature version of everything that comes after.

At Atlas, Devansh handled infrastructure and admissions (April 2022 – March 2023). Co-founded Atlas Academy, a small high school for fellows. Organized HackUBA — a 20-person, weeklong hackathon in rural Japan.

Then attended the University of Oxford, pursuing a BA. Founded the Bread Society. Helped run the Taylor Swift Society. Reads finance blogs — Patrick McKenzie and Matt Levine are favorites. Cooks for friends. Bakes bread.

Now co-founding Standard Intelligence with Galen Mead, Neel Redkar, Yudhister Kumar, and others. The company is pretraining models for general computer use — training and aligning models that learn like humans do in full generality.

The Overlooked Angle

The Galen Mead profile writes itself: solo researcher builds AGI lab. But labs don’t run on research alone. Someone has to build the organizational infrastructure — handle admissions, manage operations, create the conditions where researchers can focus. Devansh did that for Atlas Fellowship at 17 or 18. He’s doing it again at Standard Intelligence.

The HackUBA hackathon in rural Japan reveals the operating model. Twenty people. One week. Remote location. This isn’t scale-seeking behavior — it’s depth-seeking. He creates high-intensity collaborative environments for small groups. The Bread Society at Oxford is the same instinct at lower temperature. He builds the social infrastructure that turns a collection of talented individuals into a functioning team.

The Atlas Fellowship connection is structurally important for Standard Intelligence. The Atlas network includes many of the most ambitious young people in the AI safety ecosystem. Devansh doesn’t just know them — he built the institution that selected and connected them. That’s a different kind of network effect.
