◆ Investment Thesis — T1 (High Conviction, Watch for T0)

Isha Puri is on the single hottest research thread of 2025–26 — inference-time compute / RL-for-reasoning — with a first-author result the field actually uses. Rollout Roulette (NeurIPS 2025, arXiv 2502.01618) reframes test-time scaling as probabilistic inference: instead of optimizing toward a reward model’s mode (which invites reward hacking), it runs a particle filter that samples the typical set. Verbatim from the abstract: “4–16× better scaling rate over our deterministic search counterparts … Qwen2.5-Math-7B-Instruct scales to o1-level accuracy in only 32 rollouts.” Training-free, model-agnostic, 52★ repo — strong traction for a paper this young.

The cadence is the signal: first/co-first papers at NeurIPS 2021, NeurIPS 2025, ICLR 2026, and ICML 2026, all on one accelerating thread (RL + test-time compute + distributional/uncertainty-aware reasoning — Beyond Binary Rewards trains LMs to reason about their own uncertainty; Vector Policy Optimization argues against collapsing reward to a single scalar). Co-advised by Yoon Kim and Marzyeh Ghassemi, joining Google DeepMind for a research stint, and an Assistant Program Chair for NeurIPS 2025 — plugged into the org layer of the field, not just publishing into it. Harvard ’23 (declined the Kempner and Princeton Gordon Wu fellowships to go to MIT).

The honest read on tier — T1. A ~3rd-year PhD with a DeepMind internship and no founding/stealth signal, so not active pursuit. But the research line is dead-center thesis, the trajectory is accelerating, and the pedigree is elite. High-conviction relationship-build now; re-tier to T0 on any stealth/founding/leave signal. ◆ The Read — interpretation, not fact

The throughline of her work is a single conviction: don’t collapse rich signal into a point estimate. Rollout Roulette samples the distribution instead of optimizing the reward mode; Beyond Binary Rewards makes models reason about their uncertainty; Vector Policy Optimization’s own framing — “It’s never made sense to me that RL collapses all reward signals to a single scalar” — is the same instinct stated outright. And it rhymes with how she lives: a 650-postcard wall, each inscribed with the date, a story, and who was there — a refusal to compress a moment to a scalar.

Our read: she’s a distributional thinker who optimizes for understanding the full shape of a problem, paired with an unusually strong "get this stuff out there / make research usable" drive (her talks teach the method from first principles; her teen work was free dyslexia screening for kids who’d otherwise go undiagnosed). That combination — frontier reasoning research + a builder’s urge to ship it usefully + social-good motivation — is a founder shape. The way in is the idea (uncertainty-aware test-time compute), not a pitch. Falsifiers: if she settles into a pure big-lab research-scientist track with no build/ship impulse, or the DeepMind stint converts to a long-term IC role.
