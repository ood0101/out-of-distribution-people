Origin & Early Spike

The seed is visible at 17–18. Two artifacts in his own teenage voice — a college-thesis-grade film analysis and a 2019 product that was, in effect, retrieval-augmented document QA — prefigure the exact research that later made his name.

◆ You Could See It At 17–18

As a senior at Savannah Arts Academy (a public visual/performing-arts magnet — not a STEM school), he wrote, narrated, and edited a 10-minute “Dunkirk Film Analysis” that reverse-engineers the structural architecture of Christopher Nolan's non-linear narrative. His thesis, in one sentence: “Nolan presents time in a non-traditional manner… by creating a story arc that places equal importance on three different narratives, distorts the rates of action, and purposefully disregards exposition and dialogue” (Jon Saad-Falcon's own words, Dunkirk Film Analysis, YouTube, 01:28). He then builds a quantitative model of the film, with a hand-made graphic: “Nolan equates one hour in the plane to one day in the sea to one week on land… the vertical distance each line travels highlights the length of each narrative in real-time” (05:39). At ~18 he is already doing what he does now — imposing a measurable, decomposed structure on a messy, high-entropy system.
