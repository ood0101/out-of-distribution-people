◆ Investment Thesis

Karan Dalal is a core architect of Test-Time Training (TTT) layers — a fundamentally new sequence modeling primitive where the hidden state is itself a neural network that learns via gradient descent during inference. This is not incremental: TTT layers achieve linear complexity like RNNs while matching Transformer expressiveness, and unlike Mamba, they continue improving with longer context past 16K tokens. The TTT group has published 4 papers in 18 months (ICML 2025, CVPR 2025, two arXiv), with Dalal as lead or co-lead author on 3 of them. The video generation paper (CVPR 2025) demonstrated TTT layers enabling one-minute coherent video generation, beating Mamba 2 by 34 Elo points — with only a 5B model. TTT-E2E achieves 2.7x faster inference than full attention at 128K context on H100s while scaling identically. The TTT paradigm is being adopted by Thinking Machines Lab (Mira Murati, $2B raised, NVIDIA gigawatt partnership) for their Tinker API. Dalal is 22, has prior startup experience ($1.6M raised at 17), and sits at the intersection of architecture research and practical systems. If TTT layers become a standard primitive — replacing or augmenting attention in long-context and video workloads — the people who invented them are the ones to back.

Evidence

TTT Layers: The Core Innovation

Test-Time Training layers replace the fixed hidden state of an RNN with a machine learning model (linear model or 2-layer MLP) whose update rule is a step of self-supervised learning. This means the model continues training during inference, compressing context into weights rather than caching tokens.

CVPR 2025: One-Minute Video Generation

Lead author. Added TTT layers to a pre-trained 5B-parameter Transformer to generate one-minute videos from text storyboards — a feat current Transformers cannot achieve due to quadratic attention costs. Dataset: Tom and Jerry cartoons as proof-of-concept benchmark.

  • +34 Elo points over Mamba 2, Gated DeltaNet, and sliding-window attention in human evaluation (100 videos per method)
  • Videos generated in a single shot — no editing, stitching, or post-processing
  • TTT-MLP preserved character appearance and smooth motion across scene transitions; baselines showed body distortion, character inconsistency, and duplicated narrative elements
  • GitHub repo: 2,400 stars (as of March 2026)

Acknowledged limitations: artifacts remain (morphing, unnatural physics, inconsistent lighting), attributed to 5B model scale. The architecture contribution is the point — scaling to larger models would improve quality.

TTT-E2E: Long Context Without the Memory Tax

Co-authored (Dec 2025). Reframes long-context language modeling as continual learning: a standard Transformer with sliding-window attention continues learning at test time via next-token prediction, compressing context into weights. Meta-learning during training optimizes the initialization for test-time adaptation.

  • 2.7x faster than full attention at 128K context on H100 hardware
  • 35x faster at 2M token context
  • Constant inference latency regardless of context length
  • Training cost: ~3.4x slower than standard approaches at 8K context (meta-learning overhead)
  • GitHub: 570 stars

ICML 2025: Learning to (Learn at Test Time)

Core contributor (with Yu Sun and Xinhao Li). The foundational TTT paper that introduced TTT-Linear and TTT-MLP. Evaluated at 125M to 1.3B parameters against Transformer and Mamba baselines.

  • Established that TTT layers match Transformer scaling behavior on long context
  • GitHub: ttt-lm-jax (454 stars) + ttt-lm-pytorch (1,300 stars)
  • Poster at ICML 2025. Workshop on test-time adaptation held at ICML 2025

TTT-Discover: Reinforcement Learning at Test Time

Co-authored (Jan 2026). Applies RL during test time so the LLM continues training with experience specific to each test problem. New state-of-the-art on: Erdős’ minimum overlap problem, GPUMode kernel competition (2x faster than prior art), AtCoder algorithm competitions, and single-cell denoising. All achieved with open-source model at a cost of only a few hundred dollars per problem, using Thinking Machines Lab’s Tinker API.

Market & Competitive Landscape

TTT sits at the intersection of two massive markets: efficient long-context architectures (replacing or augmenting attention) and long-form video generation. Both are bifurcating from the Transformer status quo.
