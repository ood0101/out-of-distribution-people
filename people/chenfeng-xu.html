The Spike — May 2026

Two Best Paper Awards at MLSys 2026 (one week ago)

MLSys 2026 was held in May 2026. Chenfeng was a co-author on two award-winning papers in the same proceedings — an extraordinarily rare double. The Best Paper / Best Research Paper distinction is on the order of 1–2 awards out of ~150 accepted.

1. LEANN — Best Paper Award (MLSys 2026)

“LEANN: A Low-Storage Vector Index” — Yichuan Wang, Shu Liu, Zhifei Li, Yongji Wu, Ziming Mao, Yilong Zhao, Xiao Yan, Zhiying Xu, Yang Zhou, Ion Stoica, Sewon Min, Matei Zaharia, Joseph E. Gonzalez — with Chenfeng as a contributor. UC Berkeley Sky Computing Lab.

  • 97% storage savings: index size reduced to <5% of raw data (vs. standard ANN indexes that need 150–700 GB to index 100 GB)
  • 90% top-3 recall, <2 seconds latency on real-world Q&A benchmarks
  • Combines graph-based structures with on-the-fly recomputation — the wedge is “100% private RAG on personal devices” (consumer-laptop deployment)
  • Open-source community traction: GitHub yichuan-w/LEANN, multiple thousand-star trajectory

2. StreamDiffusionV2 — Best Research Paper Award (MLSys 2026)

“StreamDiffusionV2: A Streaming System for Dynamic and Interactive Video Generation” — Tianrui Feng, Zhi Li, Shuo Yang, Haocheng Xi, Muyang Li, Xiuyu Li, Lvmin Zhang, Keting Yang, Kelly Peng, Song Han, Maneesh Agrawala, Kurt Keutzer, Akio Kodaira, Chenfeng Xu (last author position, project lead).

  • 58.28 FPS with a 14B-parameter model on 4× H100; 64.52 FPS with a 1.3B-parameter model on 4× H100. First frame within 0.5 seconds
  • Training-free system. Key innovations: SLO-aware batching scheduler, block scheduler, sink-token-guided rolling KV cache, motion-aware noise controller, parallel pipeline orchestration
  • Scales across heterogeneous GPU setups; no TensorRT or quantization required
  • GitHub chenfengxu714/StreamDiffusionV2 (473☆)
  • Lvmin Zhang as a co-author is significant — he is the ControlNet creator (Stanford / SUSTech), Maneesh Agrawala’s student. Song Han + Agrawala + Keutzer as senior advisors stack three of the most important efficient/generative ML faculty into one paper

Reference class for StreamDiffusionV2’s product surface: Decart AI, Krea, Pika, Genmo Mochi, Luma Dream Machine, Black Forest Labs — all building real-time generative video infra. Chenfeng’s system is positioned as the open infrastructure layer (analogous to LEANN’s positioning vs. closed RAG infra).

Aurora — ICML 2026 (accepted May 2026, Seoul Jul 6–11)

“Aurora: When RL Meets Adaptive Speculative Training — A Unified Training-Serving System” — project leads: Xiaoxia Wu, Chenfeng Xu, Junxiong Wang. Co-authors include Junxiong Wang (Cornell + Together), Fengxiang Bie, Jisen Li, Zhongzhu Zhou (Together Turbo team), Zelei Shao, Qingyang Wu, Yubo Wang, Yinghui Liu, Avner May, Sri Yanamandra, Ben Athiwaratkun, Leon Song. Advisors: Ce Zhang, Tri Dao, Percy Liang.

  • Reframes speculative decoding as a joint learning-and-serving problem: RL learns directly from live inference traces and continuously updates the speculator without interrupting serving
  • 1.5x day-0 speedup on frontier models (MiniMax M2.1 229B, Qwen3-Coder-Next 80B)
  • Adapts to traffic distribution shifts: 1.25x additional speedup over a well-trained but static speculator on Qwen3 / Llama3
  • Open-source — the public Together AI productization vehicle (together.ai/blog/aurora)

I-DLM — Introspective Diffusion Language Model

Recent preprint. Unifies introspection + generation in a single forward pass. Co-authors include Tri Dao + Ben Athiwaratkun + James Zou. From the Together AI Turbo team context. Diffusion-LLM lineage with introspection — a competitive thread vs. Inception Labs (Mercury) and Sakana (CycleQD).

Squeeze Evolve — 97.5% ARC-AGI-V2 SoTA

Multi-model orchestration + verifier-free evolutionary search. Lead: Monishwaran Maheswaran (Berkeley). Co-authors include Leon Lakhani, Zhongzhu Zhou, Shijia Yang, Junxiong Wang, Coleman Hooper, Yuezhou Hu, Rishabh Tiwari (in repo, T1), Jue Wang, Harman Singh, Qingyang Wu, Yuqing Jian, Ce Zhang, Kurt Keutzer, Tri Dao, Xiaoxia Shirley Wu, Ben Athiwaratkun, James Zou, Chenfeng Xu. The 97.5% ARC-AGI-V2 figure is the SoTA bar as of May 2026 — positioned against OpenAI’s o-series reasoning models. Squeezed Attention (ACL 2025) is the published predecessor.
