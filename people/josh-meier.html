Origin & Early Spike

The “you could see it early” case is unusually well-documented: a three-year self-directed stem-cell research program begun as a freshman class assignment, national science-fair wins, and a 15-person teen biotech he led to an FDA presentation — all before college.

◆ The Spike at 14–16

Started programming at 8; opened his first email account at 5 (Weizmann USA). At 14, a single open-ended freshman assignment — his magnet school ran a class with “no tests, just one assignment: come up with a research proposal” (CIRM) — became a 3-year stem-cell research program on controlling iPS-cell aging via mitochondrial DNA deletions. It won him a 4th-place finish at the 2014 Intel Science Talent Search ($40,000) and made him one of CNN’s “Eight Whiz Kids Who Are the Future of Medicine” (May 2014, age 18).

  • Competition record (owner-valued signals, dated): 2014 Intel STS — 4th place, $40,000 (Science News for Students; Society for Science). Siemens Competition regional win at MIT, 1st-place individual + $3,000 (PRNewswire). 2012 Google Science Fair finalist at 16 (Patch, Fast Company). 1st place in Cellular & Molecular Biology at Intel ISEF (Origin brief; competition/category attribution conflicts across sources — unverified).
  • The literal “see it on video” artifact: his 2012 Google Science Fair entry, “The Missing Genome: Mitochondrial DNA Deletions in Stem Cells” (youtube.com/watch?v=r5DqI0unvRU) — a 16-year-old presenting stem-cell genetics on camera. The video is now set to Private; documented for archival retrieval, content not independently verified (unverified).
  • Led a 15+ person biotech at 16. Provita Pharmaceuticals (founded 2009 at Bergen County Academies) was a school-based but operationally real “virtual company” of high-schoolers; its “flying syringe” project genetically engineered mosquitoes to deliver a West Nile vaccine via saliva, and the team presented to the FDA (Fast Company / inhabitat). Meier was the leader directing 15+ peers. The Provita-Website GitHub repo survives. This is his strongest, most concrete leadership signal.
  • Upbringing: Teaneck, New Jersey; born and raised Modern Orthodox Jewish (Jew in the City). School: Bergen County Academies (Academy for the Advancement of Science & Technology), a public STEM magnet that was “one of the rare high schools to have both a stem cell lab and a faculty member recruited to run it” (CIRM) — real scaffolding, not a from-nowhere story.
  • Range-seeking at 18: won a scholarship (via ISEF) to the Weizmann Institute’s month-long Bessie Lawrence ISSI program and, instead of the obvious stem-cell-lab fit, chose particle physics — working in Prof. Eilam Gross’s ATLAS/Higgs group. “These scientists are studying the fundamental units of nature. They’re trying to understand the universe.” (Weizmann USA)
  • Polymath breadth, ~age 20: simultaneously continued the STS-winning research with new AI work, founded a nonprofit for homeless kids (name/scale unverified), placed highly in the Chidon HaTanach (international Bible competition), interned at a fintech, and shipped iPhone apps — TaxiLater (schedule-an-Uber app, covered by TIME / Gizmodo / Mashable) and iWillNotProcrastinate (Jew in the City; GitHub joshim5).
  • First-author-grade tooling as an undergrad: GUIDES: sgRNA design for loss-of-function screens, Nature Methods, Sept 2017 (with Neville Sanjana & Feng Zhang, Broad Institute). ML-driven CRISPR tooling years before ESM — the direct conceptual ancestor of “computational tooling for biology.”
  • Network tell: his Harvard CS51-FinalProject teammates were “Joshua Meier, Elana Simon, Erik Godard, and Mandela Patrick” — Elana Simon is herself a notable bio-AI figure (Stanford; Fei-Fei Li / cancer genomics). Early co-location with another frontier bio-ML talent.

Clarity of Thought — In His Own Words

  • The durable through-line, in his teenage words: “What I love about science is the ability to make something. It’s the act of creation.” (Weizmann USA) — the same build/create instinct visible in Provita, the apps, and Chai.
  • On the founding bet (No Priors Ep. 121, Jul 2025): “It was just a single protein structure at a time… In drug discovery, you need to understand how multiple molecules interact with one another.” And: “We made a bet that structure prediction, protein folding was going to get a lot better… and we would be able to take this to the next level.”
  • On the platform thesis: “Could you actually have some kind of portable AI platform, something that would actually be generalizable and could be applied to lots of different areas?” (No Priors Ep. 121)
