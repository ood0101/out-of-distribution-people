The Spike — With Evidence

1. VideoPoet (ICML 2024 Best Paper) — 1st author of 27 — 512 cites

Kondratyuk, Yu, Gu, Lezama, Huang, Schindler, Hornung, Birodkar, Yan, Chiu, Somandepalli, Akbari, Alon, Cheng, Dillon, Gupta, Hahn, Hauth, Hendon, Martinez, Minnen, Sirotenko, Sohn, Yang, Adam, Ming-Hsuan Yang, Essa, Wang, Ross, Seybold, Jiang. ICML 2024, awarded Best Paper (one of 10 from 2,610 papers). The architectural bet: a decoder-only transformer for video generation that treats images, video, text, and audio as discrete token sequences — contra the diffusion consensus (DDIM / SDEdit / DiT / Sora / Dream Machine / Veo / Pika). VideoPoet was Google’s answer to “can autoregressive LLMs do video at quality.” The published model produced state-of-the-art zero-shot generation across text-to-video, image-to-video, video stylization, inpainting, outpainting, and audio generation from a single backbone. Joshua V. Dillon (his rekursiv co-founder) is the 15th author on this paper — they collaborated on this work before they co-founded.

Direct framing (Google Research blog, Kondratyuk + Ross authors): “One of the current bottlenecks in video generation is in the ability to produce coherent large motions.” That sentence is the thesis statement of his Google tenure.

2. MoViNets (CVPR 2021) — 1st author — 411 cites

Kondratyuk, Yuan, Li, Zhang, Tan, Brown, Gong. The Mobile Video Networks family — a stream-of-frames architecture for efficient online video recognition on mobile-class hardware, built during his Google AI Residency under Boqing Gong. MoViNets shipped via Google’s open-source video model zoo and is still the canonical citation for “efficient video recognition that runs on phone-class compute.” This is the older work but it’s the credential that opened the path to VideoPoet — he was on Google’s video understanding team before he was on its video generation team.

3. UDify (EMNLP 2019) — 1st author — 371 cites

“75 Languages, 1 Model: Parsing Universal Dependencies Universally.” His MS thesis output at Charles University + Saarland: a single multilingual BERT fine-tune that parses dependency trees across 75 languages at SOTA-level performance per language. This is what got him recruited into the Google AI Residency. His authorship pattern is unusually consistent — 1st author on the 3 highest-cited papers across 3 completely different fields (NLP → video understanding → video generation). The through-line is “general architectures that subsume many specialized ones.” Rekursiv’s thesis (one autonomous-discovery loop that subsumes many specialized scientific workflows) is the same instinct at a higher level of abstraction.

4. Other notable work

  • MoE for Integrated Multimodal Perception (NeurIPS 2023, Akbari + Kondratyuk + ...) — alternating gradient descent + mixture-of-experts; 2nd author; 34 cites. Bridge paper between MoViNets and VideoPoet.
  • Wisdom of Committees (ICLR 2022, 2nd author, 96 cites) — ensembles of small models beat single large models on cost-quality frontier.
  • When Ensembling Smaller Models is More Efficient (2020 1st author, 52 cites) — sets up the “committee” thesis.
  • MALT Diffusion (CVPR 2025 Workshop Oral, 3rd author of 9, Sihyun Yu 1st) — Memory-Augmented Latent Transformers for arbitrarily-long video. FVD 220.4 on 128-frame UCF-101, vs 648.4 prior SOTA. Recent collaboration with the Google video team (Hahn + Lezama + Gupta + Essa + Ross + Huang). This means he kept publishing with Google co-authors through 2025 — he didn’t scorch-earth on departure.
  • Improved Immiscible Diffusion (2025, 3rd of 6 incl. Chenfeng Xu + Keutzer + Tomizuka) — reduces miscibility to accelerate diffusion training. This is a direct network bridge to the Chenfeng Xu node in the repo (T2, Berkeley/Together).
  • CamViG (2024, 4th of 6) — camera-aware image-to-video w/ Marmon + Schindler + Lezama + Seybold + Essa.

Citation profile honesty check: 1,648 total / h-10 is not a Tri-Dao / Albert-Gu / Jiaming-Song citation deck. It’s a working-engineer-with-3-landmark-papers deck. The signal is concentration and recency: 3 first-author papers across 3 different fields, each cleanly cited, the most recent one (VideoPoet, ICML Best Paper, Dec 2023 release) the highest-cited; he’s on an accelerating slope, not a plateau.
