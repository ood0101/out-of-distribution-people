◆ Investment Thesis

Kevin Lu is one of a handful of people who has both invented a foundational RL paradigm (Decision Transformer, 1,951 citations — the conceptual ancestor of reasoning models like o1/o3) and shipped a frontier production model at scale (led GPT-4o-mini from zero to launch in ~3 months at OpenAI, the most widely-used model in the GPT-4 family). He is now at Thinking Machines Lab under Mira Murati, working on post-training and fine-tuning infrastructure (Tinker). His intellectual thesis — that the internet is the only important technology for pretraining but RL lacks its equivalent data source — points directly at what he would build if he founded a company: the “internet of RL data.” His co-author network reads like an AI founder directory (Aravind Srinivas/Perplexity, Michael Laskin/Reflection AI, Pieter Abbeel/Covariant). He is not yet a founder, but his rate of acceleration, intellectual clarity, and network density make him a Tier 1 relationship to build now.

Evidence

Decision Transformer: The Paper That Reframed RL

“Decision Transformer: Reinforcement Learning via Sequence Modeling” (NeurIPS 2021). Co-first-author with Lili Chen. Cast RL as conditional sequence modeling: condition an autoregressive transformer on desired return, past states, and actions → generate optimal future actions. No value functions, no policy gradients, no dynamic programming bootstrapping.

Pretrained Transformers as Universal Computation Engines

First author. AAAI 2022. Demonstrated that a GPT-2 model pretrained on natural language, with all self-attention layers frozen, can be fine-tuned to match or beat task-specific models on numerical computation, vision (CIFAR-10), and protein fold prediction. 228 citations, 24 highly influential. GitHub: 2,600+ stars, 248 forks. Featured in VentureBeat, TWIML podcast.

This result anticipated the “foundation model” thesis by ~1 year — that pretraining on language creates universal representations transferable to arbitrary modalities. Lu was an undergrad when he published this.

OpenAI: GPT-4o-mini, o1-mini, o3

Joined OpenAI in March 2024. Within ~3 months, led the team that shipped GPT-4o-mini (July 2024) — a multimodal reasoning model supporting image+text input, long context, optimized for speed and cost. GPT-4o-mini became the most widely deployed model in the GPT-4 family by volume.

Specialized in reinforcement learning, small models, and synthetic data. Contributed to the development of o1-mini and o3 reasoning models. Departed OpenAI in July 2025 to join Thinking Machines Lab.

Thinking Machines Lab: Tinker & Post-Training

Joined August 2025. Working on Tinker — Thinking Machines’ fine-tuning API for open-weight LLMs (Llama, Qwen families). Tinker launched October 2025, GA December 2025. Early adopters: Berkeley, Princeton, Stanford, Redwood Research. Lu’s specific focus: RL-based post-training, the exact intersection of his Decision Transformer expertise and production model training experience from OpenAI.

In His Own Words

“Why you should stop working on RL research and instead work on product. The technology that unlocked the big scaling shift in AI is the internet, not transformers. I think it’s well known that data is the most important thing in AI, and also that researchers choose not to work [on data].” — @_kevinlu, late 2025

Provocative thesis from the co-inventor of Decision Transformer. He’s arguing that the RL community is repeating the mistakes of 2015-2020 — doing research that doesn’t matter because the bottleneck is data, not algorithms. This reveals his founder thesis: whoever solves the RL data problem wins.

“The internet and next-token prediction go hand-in-hand… Our poor models! They know so little and yet still have so much hidden from them.” — “The Only Important Technology Is The Internet,” kevinlu.ai, July 2025

Invoking Alec Radford. His core argument: transformers are “a distractor” — the internet is the substrate that made language model scaling work. RL has no equivalent. The “dual of reinforcement learning” remains undiscovered. This is a thesis statement for a company.

“Long-standing dream” + “democratize training” = founder language. He frames Tinker as a step toward his personal mission, not just a job.

Intellectual Thesis (From His Blog)

Lu writes a substantive technical blog at kevinlu.ai. Three posts from 2024-2025 form a coherent thesis:

  • “Spending Inference Time” (Mar 2024) — Framework for allocating inference compute across breadth (best-of-n sampling) vs. depth (tree search). Key insight: “Best-of-16 13B outperforms best-of-1 175B” when using reward models. Predicted the inference-scaling paradigm that became o1/o3
  • “LoRAs as Composable Programs” (Feb 2024) — Frames LLMs as operating systems with LoRAs as programs. Argues for backward compatibility and composability in model ecosystems. Directly relevant to Tinker’s architecture
  • “The Only Important Technology Is The Internet” (Jul 2025) — The internet is the “dual of next-token prediction.” RL has no equivalent data substrate. The Microsoft Phi models prove curated datasets cannot match internet-scale pretraining

The through-line: inference scaling + composable fine-tuning + internet-scale data = the three pillars of whatever he builds next. He has been publicly developing this thesis for 2+ years.
