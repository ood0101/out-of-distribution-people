The Spike — With Evidence

In Fall 2024, Newhouse and Jeremy Bernstein published “Old Optimizer, New Norm: An Anthology” (arXiv 2409.20325), which recast Adam, Shampoo, and Prodigy as steepest descent under different norms — and in Appendix A, recommended Newton-Schulz iteration as the computational strategy that became Muon’s core. Simultaneously, they published “Modular Duality in Deep Learning” (arXiv 2410.21265, accepted at ICML 2025), providing the full theoretical framework: assign operator norms to each layer, recursively induce a duality map on the weight space, and derive GPU-friendly algorithms for Embed, Linear, and Conv2D layers.

Keller Jordan then implemented the Newton-Schulz orthogonalization idea as the Muon optimizer, which Newhouse describes himself as having “co-created.” The empirical results are extraordinary:

  • NanoGPT: 1.35x faster training to 3.28 validation loss on FineWeb. Muon persisted across 12 consecutive speedrunning records by 7 different researchers
  • CIFAR-10: Improved speedrun record from 3.3 to 2.6 A100-seconds
  • 1.5B transformer: GPT-2 XL level on HellaSwag in 10 8xH100-hours vs. 13.3 hours with AdamW
  • Kimi K2 (1T params): Moonshot AI scaled Muon (via MuonClip) to 15.5T tokens with zero loss spikes
  • Moonlight (3B/16B MoE): Better Pareto frontier than prior models with far fewer training FLOPs
  • FLOP overhead: <1% for typical LLM training (0.5% for Llama 405B scale)

Beyond Muon, Newhouse built a ThunderKittens kernel for symmetric matrix multiplication that achieves 1510 TFLOPS vs. PyTorch’s 774 TFLOPS on 8192×8192 symmetric matrices on H100 — a ~2x throughput gain in the Newton-Schulz iteration that powers Muon, translating to an overall 30% speedup in Newton-Schulz.

His most recent work, “Training Transformers with Enforced Lipschitz Constants” (arXiv 2507.13338, Jul 2025), with Bernstein and Isola, pushes the frontier further: using Muon + spectral soft cap, they trained a 2-Lipschitz transformer on Shakespeare reaching 60% validation accuracy, and a 10-Lipschitz transformer at 145M params reaching 21% on internet text — without layer norm, QK norm, or logit softcapping. This is the first demonstration that stability techniques can be replaced entirely by direct weight spectral norm constraints.

What He’d Build

The Training Infrastructure Thesis

Newhouse’s work points toward a company that makes neural network training fundamentally more efficient and stable. Three convergent threads:

  • Next-gen optimizers beyond AdamW: Muon already delivers 2x computational efficiency. His modular duality framework is a design space for building custom optimizers per architecture. The thesis: every major model lab is leaving 30–50% of their compute budget on the table by using AdamW
  • Lipschitz-enforced training: Training without layer norm, QK norm, or logit softcapping by directly constraining weight spectral norms. This could eliminate entire categories of training instability (loss spikes, gradient explosions) that cost labs millions in wasted GPU-hours
  • Custom kernels for training primitives: His ThunderKittens work (2x speedup on Newton-Schulz) shows he can go all the way to the metal. Optimizer + kernel co-design is an extremely narrow wedge

Market

  • AI model training compute: $30B+ annually and growing rapidly. A 2x efficiency gain on training runs costing $100M+ is worth tens of millions per customer
  • If Muon or its successors become the default optimizer for LLM training, the creator has unmatched credibility to build the picks-and-shovels layer
