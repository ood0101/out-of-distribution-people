The Spike — With Evidence

1. Co-first author on the two papers that defined a method (ReFT) and punctured a hype cycle (AxBench)

ReFT: Representation Finetuning for Language Models (Wu*, Arora*, Wang, Geiger, Jurafsky, Manning, Potts — NeurIPS 2024 Spotlight, 255 cites). ReFT learns interventions on a frozen model's hidden representations instead of updating weights — its LoReFT variant is 15–65× more parameter-efficient than LoRA while matching or beating it. This is one of the most-cited efficient-finetuning methods to come out of academia in the LoRA era, and Aryaman shares first authorship with Zhengxuan Wu.

AxBench: Steering LLMs? Even Simple Baselines Outperform Sparse Autoencoders (Wu*, Arora*, Geiger, et al. — ICML 2025 Spotlight, 153 cites). A scalable benchmark for interpretability methods on concept detection and steering. The finding was a cold shower for the SAE-maximalist wing of the field: "prompting and finetuning are still best, supervised interp methods are effective, SAEs lag behind." Influential precisely because it was contrarian and rigorous.

2. Sole-led an ACL Outstanding Paper

CausalGym: Benchmarking Causal Interpretability Methods on Linguistic Tasks (Arora, Jurafsky, Potts — ACL 2024, 53 cites). This one he led himself, with only his two advisors as co-authors — and it won an ACL 2024 Outstanding Paper Award plus a Senior Area Chair Award. Leading a paper to a top-venue best-paper award, solo, as a 1st/2nd-year PhD is the strongest individual-research signal in his record.

3. Builder of field infrastructure (pyvene + pyreft)

pyvene (NAACL 2024 Demo; Wu, Geiger, Arora, et al. — 879★, 104 forks): a unified, extensible library for performing interventions on PyTorch models — activation patching, causal abstraction, Distributed Alignment Search (DAS / Boundless DAS), knowledge localization. It is the de-facto plumbing for a large share of academic mech-interp experiments. pyreft (1.6k★, 134 forks): the reference implementation of ReFT, used as a drop-in efficient-finetuning library. Aryaman is a named author/contributor on both. The point: most strong interp researchers produce papers; far fewer produce adopted tooling. He does both.

4. He has already been inside the interp-commercialization world

His site lists internships at Transluce (Jacob Steinhardt + Sarah Schwettmann's interpretability lab), Redwood Research (AI-safety / interp), and Apple, plus a research stint with Ryan Cotterell at ETH Zürich. The Transluce + Redwood line items are the load-bearing ones: they make "watch for the interp commercialization inflection" a concrete, near-term path rather than a hypothetical.

Background

Born in India; his family immigrated to the United States when he was about five, and he was raised bilingual in Hindi and English. In high school, conscious that his Hindi was slipping, he started editing Wiktionary, eventually becoming a site administrator — expanding coverage of South Asian languages and writing code to support the dictionary. That self-directed, build-the-tool-you-need instinct is the through-line from a teenage Wiktionary admin to a PhD student who ships pyvene and pyreft. Punjabi surname (Arora); flagged indian per repo sourcing thesis.

Georgetown 2020–2023, B.S. in Computer Science and Linguistics, working closely with Nathan Schneider in computational linguistics. He published heavily as an undergrad — presenting at ACL as a first-year — on Hindi/Punjabi NLP, morphology (UniMorph, SIGMORPHON), and information-theoretic properties of language. Was a 2022 Goldwater Scholar (one of three Georgetown College recipients) and won the Georgetown Tropaia Computer Science Award (2024). He then started his Stanford CS PhD in 2023, NSF GRFP-funded, co-advised by Christopher Potts and Dan Jurafsky, and pivoted from computational linguistics to mechanistic interpretability — "how and why self-supervised systems work." Stanford HAI affiliate.

Note on olympiads: the comp-linguistics origin makes a NACLO/IOL (linguistics olympiad) background plausible, but it was not confirmed in available sources — the verified origin story is the Wiktionary-administrator path, which is more distinctive anyway.
