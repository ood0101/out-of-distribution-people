The Spike — With Evidence

1. “LoRA Learns Less and Forgets Less” (TMLR 2024, Featured Certification)

Lead author. The most rigorous empirical comparison of LoRA vs. full fine-tuning to date. Tested on Llama-2 7B and 13B across programming and mathematics domains in two regimes: instruction fine-tuning (~100K prompt-response pairs) and continued pretraining (20B unfiltered tokens). Key finding: in standard low-rank settings, LoRA substantially underperforms full fine-tuning on target-domain tasks, but LoRA better maintains performance on out-of-distribution tasks — mitigating catastrophic forgetting more effectively than weight decay or dropout. Full fine-tuning learns perturbations with ranks 10–100x greater than typical LoRA configurations, explaining the gap. Published May 2024 on arXiv, accepted TMLR August 2024 with Featured Certification (top ~5% of TMLR papers). Co-authored with researchers from MosaicML/Databricks (Jonathan Frankle, now Chief Scientist) and Columbia.

GitHub repo (danbider/lora-tradeoffs): 20 stars. All trained checkpoints released publicly on HuggingFace. Paper has become a standard reference in the LoRA/PEFT literature. An AAAI 2025 follow-up paper (“LoRA Unlearns More and Retains More”) directly extends his findings — evidence of downstream impact.

2. Minions: Local-Cloud LLM Collaboration (ICML 2025)

Co-author with Avanika Narayan and Sabri Eyuboglu (both Ré lab PhDs), plus Avner May, Scott Linderman, James Zou, and Christopher Ré. Proposes a protocol where small on-device LLMs (1–8B params, e.g., Llama 3.1-8B via Ollama) collaborate with frontier cloud models (GPT-4, etc.) on token-intensive workloads. Two protocols:

  • Minion (naive chat): 30.4x reduction in remote costs, recovers 87% of cloud-only performance
  • MinionS (task decomposition): 5.7x cost reduction, recovers 97.9% of frontier model performance

Benchmarked on FinanceBench (100-page 10-K reports), LongHealth (medical records), QAsper (scientific papers). Critical finding: local models below 3B parameters are not effective — the protocol became viable only after mid-2024 releases (Llama 3.1-8B). Ollama blog integration drove significant real-world adoption. The paper reads like a product spec, not just research.

3. Lightning Pose (Nature Methods 2024)

Lead author of PhD work. Semi-supervised deep learning framework for animal pose estimation that reduces labeling requirements through Bayesian ensembling and unlabeled video with motion continuity, multi-view geometry, and posture plausibility penalties. Won Columbia’s Titus M. Cowan Dissertation Prize in biomedical research. GitHub: 291 stars. Published in Nature Methods (IF ~48).
