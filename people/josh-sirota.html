◆ The Spike

Josh Sirota is a non-technical founder who built a deeply technical AI company to a $100M valuation in ~7 months. His background is enterprise sales at Oracle and Salesforce — not ML research, not engineering. Yet he assembled a team (Berkeley CS PhD, MIT PhD), got into a16z Speedrun, landed enterprise customers, organized an event at Chase Center with Vinod Khosla speaking, got Arielle Zuckerberg to lead his $12M round, and appeared on the All-In Podcast — all before turning 30.

The thesis is maximalist: “Software is dead.” Eragon aims to replace the entire enterprise software stack with a single prompt-driven LLM interface. Fine-tuned open-source models (Qwen, Kimi) deployed in each customer’s cloud, connecting to CRMs, ERPs, data warehouses, ticketing, identity providers. The boldness of the claim is part of the signal — he’s competing with Microsoft, Glean, and every major enterprise vendor from a loft across from Oracle Park.

The overlooked pattern: Sirota saw the enterprise software problem from the sales floor, not from a research lab. He watched knowledge workers waste hours navigating tooling at Oracle and Salesforce. That firsthand distribution insight — knowing exactly how enterprises buy, deploy, and resist change — is a structural advantage that most ML-PhD-led AI startups don’t have. The hockey goalie background is also a tell: goalies are the only players who see the entire field.

Background

Josh Sirota, born July 10, 1996, grew up in Armonk, NY — an affluent hamlet in Westchester County, about 40 miles north of Manhattan. Notably the same town as IBM’s world headquarters. Attended Penn State University where he played ACHA Division II hockey as a goaltender (5’11”, 165 lbs, catches right).

After college, joined enterprise go-to-market teams at Oracle and then Salesforce. Describes having built a strong network among senior Oracle leadership. The experience gave him firsthand exposure to how much time knowledge workers spend navigating enterprise tooling — the pain point that would later become Eragon’s thesis.

Suffered what he calls a “quarter-life crisis,” moved to San Francisco, and in August 2024 founded Eragon AI out of a live-work loft across from Oracle Park (the Giants’ stadium). Named after Christopher Paolini’s fantasy novel. Within months: a16z Speedrun selection, enterprise customers, Chase Center event with Vinod Khosla, and a $12M raise at $100M valuation announced March 18, 2026.

Companies & Projects

Eragon AI (Aug 2024–present)

Prompt-driven AI Operating System for enterprise. Thesis: buttons, dialog boxes, and pull-down menus are obsolete. Future business is done by prompt. Fine-tunes open-source LLMs (Qwen, Kimi) on customer data; deploys within each customer’s cloud; isolates model weights per tenant. Connects to email, CRMs, ERPs, data warehouses, ticketing tools, identity providers.

Architecture: Reasoning layer (interprets intent) + Knowledge layer (grounds responses) + Action layer (triggers automations).

The Overlooked Angle

The standard AI startup narrative features an ML PhD who builds a model and then struggles with distribution. Sirota is the inverse: a distribution expert who recruited the ML talent. He spent years on enterprise sales floors at Oracle and Salesforce, watching knowledge workers drown in tooling. That buyer-side intuition — knowing exactly how enterprises evaluate, procure, and deploy software — is the structural advantage. The 18-minute close (first call to $12K signed contract) is not just a flex; it’s evidence that he built the product from the buyer’s perspective, not the builder’s.

The convening power is the other signal. At 29, as a first-time founder with no Silicon Valley pedigree (Penn State, Armonk, enterprise sales), he got Vinod Khosla to speak at his event, Arielle Zuckerberg to lead his round, Mike Knoop to angel, and appeared on All-In. That network-building velocity from a cold start is a leading indicator of founder-market fit in enterprise: if you can sell to VCs and speakers, you can sell to CIOs.

The hockey goalie metaphor is real, not cute. Goalies are the only players who face the entire opposing attack alone, making split-second decisions under pressure with full-field visibility. Enterprise AI sales requires the same pattern: seeing the whole org chart, reading the attack vectors (objections, procurement, security review), and making the save.
