The 2025–2026 Spike

ICML 2025 Outstanding Paper Award

“Roll the dice & look before you leap: Going beyond the creative limits of next-token prediction” — Vaishnavh Nagarajan, Chen Henry Wu, Charles Ding, Aditi Raghunathan. One of ~6 Outstanding Papers out of ~3,000+ accepted at ICML 2025. Oral presentation, Vancouver, Jul 16 2025.

  • Designs abstract minimal tasks that mimic real-world open-ended challenges (analogies, puzzle design) where success requires discovering new connections or constructing novel patterns
  • Shows standard next-token prediction is short-sighted and over-relies on memorization, while teacherless training and diffusion models produce more diverse, original outputs
  • Introduces seed-conditioning: adding randomness at the input rather than the output — can improve coherence without sacrificing creativity
  • Code at github.com/chenwu98/algorithmic-creativity

This is the rare ML paper that bridges theory of creativity and practical generation algorithms — the framing that next-token prediction is a creative ceiling rather than a floor is exactly the conceptual move that justifies investment in alternative generation paradigms (Mercury, Inception Labs, diffusion-LLMs).

ICLR 2025 SCOPE Workshop Outstanding Paper + ICBINB Entropic Paper Award

“Overtrained Language Models Are Harder to Fine-Tune” — Jacob Mitchell Springer, Sachin Goyal, Kaiyue Wen, Tanishq Kumar, Xiang Yue, Sadhika Malladi, Graham Neubig, Aditi Raghunathan. ICML 2025 poster + dual workshop awards.

  • Identifies “catastrophic overtraining”: extended pre-training can make models harder to fine-tune, leading to degraded final performance — counter to the standard assumption that more pre-training is always better
  • Headline empirical result: instruction-tuned OLMo-1B pre-trained on 3T tokens scores >2% worse on multiple benchmarks than the 2.3T-token counterpart
  • Theoretical analysis: catastrophic overtraining arises from systematic increase in broad sensitivity of pre-trained parameters to perturbations
  • VentureBeat coverage Apr 2025: directly relevant to enterprise-scale training-budget decisions

ICML 2025 Memorization Sinks

“Memorization Sinks: Isolating Memorization during LLM Training” — Gaurav Ghosal, Pratyush Maini, Aditi Raghunathan. Proposes MemSinks: a paradigm that promotes isolation of memorization by design, leveraging a sequence identifier that activates a unique set of memorization neurons per repeated sequence. Direct privacy + copyright commercial relevance (the DatologyAI / Anthropic data-curation surface).

In Her Own Words

“My research focuses on advancing our scientific understanding of frontier models by uncovering why they fail and building systems that remain reliable under pressure.” — CMU faculty page, 2026

The framing “uncovering why they fail” is the explicit anti-thesis of frontier-lab marketing (which emphasizes capability gains). This is the safety/reliability-research commercial wedge: every enterprise deploying LLMs at scale needs someone who can map failure modes before deployment, not after.

“Recent projects in our group expose hidden bottlenecks in data curation and quantization pipelines, develop new evaluations for agent safety and distribution shift, and highlight failure modes in post-training defenses such as jailbreaks and context fidelity.” — CMU faculty page, 2026
