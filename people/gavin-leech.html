◆ Investment Thesis — T2 (Track; adjacent-not-core)

Gavin Leech is a credible, visible AI-safety / forecasting / evals figure with a prestige Stripe Press credit — the most substantial of the two OLO DIN operators. The headline the Galatenko dossier under-described: he co-authored The Scaling Era: An Oral History of AI, 2019–2025 (Stripe Press, 2025) with Dwarkesh Patel (interviews with Amodei, Hassabis, Sutskever, Yudkowsky), runs the research consultancy Arb Research (clients incl. Open Philanthropy, Schmidt Futures, Stripe, Renaissance Philanthropy, FHI), and writes rigorous alignment essays (gleech.org / LessWrong).

The honest read — adjacent, not core. For a fund that prizes deeply technical frontier-ML builders, Leech is off the bullseye: the Stripe Press role is editorial/curatorial (not original ML); his peer-reviewed publications (Science/PNAS/NeurIPS) are mostly COVID epidemiology / Bayesian causal-effect estimation (genuine quantitative work, but applied stats, not deep learning); his one core ML paper is a meta-science critique (“Questionable practices in machine learning,” arXiv 2407.12220 — benchmark contamination / eval malpractice). His spike is judgment, synthesis, forecasting, and evaluation, not training models or shipping technical AI products.

Why T2 (not higher, not lower): real named credibility (Stripe Press + Dwarkesh, Open Phil / Schmidt clients, real pubs) puts him above an indexed node; but the inflection is soft — OLO DIN is pre-product, and his primary vehicle (Arb) is a consultancy, not a venture-scale company. The honest shape of OLO DIN is Galatenko’s owner-vehicle (he kept 75%+ but resigned the board 20 May 2026) operated by Leech & Yagudin. T1 trigger: OLO DIN ships a concrete technical alignment/agent-reliability artifact with Leech as full-time technical lead, or it raises. He’s a valuable relationship thread in his own right (the Dwarkesh / Stripe-Press / Open-Phil orbit alone).
