The Spike — With Evidence

Conway’s core claim is vertical integration of the operations stack: detection, investigation, and decisioning built in-house rather than stitched together from point solutions. This creates feedback loops where the software self-improves from deployment outcomes. The published metrics:

  • 34% reduction in false positives vs. incumbent rule-based systems
  • 28% reduction in false negatives (catching more actual fraud/AML violations)
  • 1,247 investigator hours saved per month per deployment

Conway deploys in backtestable use-cases — meaning customers can validate performance against historical data before going live. This is a shrewd go-to-market for regulated industries where trust is the bottleneck.

From a technical standpoint, Sudarshan’s background maps precisely to what Conway needs: his Purdue research at the SERIS Research Group focused on securing ML models (published at ACM/IEEE ISLPED 2024: “Securing deep neural networks on edge from membership inference attacks using trusted execution environments” — 3 citations). His focus areas on LinkedIn: AI, distributed systems, reinforcement learning. His HuggingFace profile shows models up to 22B parameters and interests in RL + deep learning. On GitHub, recent activity (Jan–Feb 2026) shows heavy commits to conway-engineering/claude-agent-sdk-python and conway-engineering/supabase-pydantic — indicating Conway’s stack uses Claude for agent orchestration and Supabase for data infrastructure.

What Conway Is Building

The Thesis: Operations as Software

Conway’s seed announcement frames the problem clearly: “There is an inevitable tension between building and sustaining products. The more aggressively companies innovate, the more problems arise: New interfaces open up fraud vectors; new features cause security vulnerabilities.” The solution: decision support systems that automate from detection to decision, with vertically integrated feedback loops that make the system self-improving.

The company tagline — “In the Business of Selling Discoveries” (from Sudarshan’s personal site) — hints at ambitions beyond fraud/AML. The seed announcement confirms: “Conway will build decision support systems at higher and higher orders of abstraction, perhaps even on the order of a company.”

Current Focus Areas

  • Fraud detection for neobanks and card processors
  • Anti-money laundering — automated alert triaging and SAR recommendations
  • Content moderation — preventing harmful content across platforms
  • IT operations — automated root cause analysis for infrastructure
