◆ Why This Name Matters (Indexed, Not Pipeline)

Jiaming Song is 1st author on DDIM — one of the 3–4 most consequential papers in the history of generative AI, alongside Ho/Jain/Abbeel’s DDPM, Rombach et al.’s LDM (Stable Diffusion), and Yang Song’s score-based models. Without DDIM, no commercial diffusion product could exist: DDPM’s 1000-step sampler is too slow to ship; DDIM compresses that to 20–50 steps at the same quality. Every image and video model in production — Stable Diffusion, DALL·E 2/3, Imagen, Midjourney, Sora, Veo, Runway, Pika, Kling, Hailuo, Genmo Mochi, and his own Dream Machine — runs on a DDIM-family sampler. He is now Chief Scientist at Luma AI, the highest research seat at one of the four leading video-generation companies in the world ($1B+ raised, $4B+ valuation). For an inception-stage fund this is not a founder pipeline target — he just joined a venture-backed Chief Scientist seat in mid-2023 with model freedom + product responsibility. He is T2: indexed as a permanent network node: angel investor candidate, advisor for diffusion-stage founders, sourcing surface for the Ermon-lab + Tsinghua cohort, and a 3–5 year departure watch.

The Spike — With Evidence

One sentence: Jiaming Song wrote the algorithm that makes commercial diffusion possible (DDIM), then co-wrote the algorithm that makes Stable Diffusion img2img possible (SDEdit, ICLR 2022 Outstanding Paper), then took the Chief Scientist seat at Luma AI and shipped Dream Machine + Ray2 + Ray3.

1. DDIM (ICLR 2021) — the sampling algorithm of the diffusion era

Song, Meng, Ermon. ~14,734 citations on Google Scholar (May 2026 retrieval, ranking among the top diffusion papers ever). DDIM constructs a class of non-Markovian diffusion processes that share the DDPM training objective but admit a deterministic reverse process you can take large steps through. The headline result: 10×–50× faster sampling than DDPM at equal quality, with deterministic generation that enables latent-space interpolation and editing. The DDIM sampler (or a direct descendant: DPM-Solver, PNDM, Heun, Euler-A — all DDIM-family) is the default sampler in Stable Diffusion, DALL·E 2/3, Imagen, Midjourney, every modern video diffusion model, and Luma’s own Ray2/Ray3.

2. SDEdit (ICLR 2022 Outstanding Paper Award) — the img2img methodology

Meng, He, Song, Song, Wu, Zhu, Ermon. ~2,919 citations. SDEdit is the technique that lets you take an existing image (or a stroke / a layout / a sketch), partially noise it, and denoise it through a diffusion model to produce a photo-realistic edited version. This is the algorithm under Stable Diffusion’s “img2img” tab, ControlNet’s sketch-guided generation, every “upload a photo, restyle it” product in market, and the inpainting/outpainting pipelines in DALL·E and Midjourney. ICLR 2022 Outstanding Paper Award (one of only a handful per year).

3. Dream Machine + Ray2 + Ray3 at Luma (2024–2025) — running the field

Joined Luma as Chief Scientist July 2023. Led training of Dream Machine (launched Jun 12, 2024) — 1M users in 4 days with zero promotion fees, 25M+ registered users by 2025, one of the viral product launches of the year. Led Ray2 (540p–1080p, coherent motion) and Ray3 (Sep 18, 2025) — the first reasoning video model, also first model to produce true 10/12/16-bit HDR ACES2065-1 EXR. Adobe Firefly integration. Dentsu Digital adoption. He is the technical anchor of the Luma product line.

4. Other major papers

Loss-Guided Diffusion Models (ICML 2023). CSDI: Conditional Score-Based Diffusion for Time-Series Imputation (NeurIPS 2021, ~1,271 cites). eDiff-I (NVIDIA 2022, ~1,148 cites) — NVIDIA’s text-to-image ensemble of expert denoisers, his NVIDIA Research era. Plus co-authorship on BIG-bench / Beyond the Imitation Game (~2,632 cites).

In His Own Words

  • On model intelligence & multimodality (Cognitive Revolution interview): “Models need to be more intelligent… you have to tell them or program them to achieve some particular type of task… eventually, it will be intelligent enough to basically take in other tasks in the multimodal sense.”
  • On 3D perception emerging from video models: “Models are very great priors for things like 3D vision tasks like depth estimation… I do believe that some degree of interpretability exists, but it’s also up to humans how interpretability exists.”
  • On alignment as method, not goal: “It might be good to seek more alignments or try to get the models aligned with what you want, and try to reason from there.”
  • On dataset design: “What is the right dataset? It’s actually very hard to have this right answer… a more objective-driven approach works better than statistical assumptions.”
  • On consistency models stability: “With one sample, the consistency model is unstable… with four samples or more, the training process actually becomes more stable.”
  • MIT TR profile: positioning Dream Machine to “fundamentally change the way people create and consume content in fields such as entertainment, education, marketing, and communication.”
