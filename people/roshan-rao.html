◆ Investment Thesis

Roshan Rao is one of a handful of people who created the field of protein language modeling. From the TAPE benchmark (NeurIPS 2019 Spotlight, first standardized evaluation for protein embeddings) through the MSA Transformer (ICML 2021, 470+ citations), ESMFold (Science 2023, first single-sequence structure prediction rivaling AlphaFold at 60x speed), to ESM3 (98B parameters, Science cover Jan 2025, generated a functional protein equivalent to 500M years of evolution) — he has been on every milestone paper. He co-founded EvolutionaryScale, raised $142M in seed (Nat Friedman, Daniel Gross, Lux Capital, Amazon, NVIDIA), and built the team that produced the most commercially significant protein AI model in existence. The CZI/Biohub acquisition (Nov 2025) removed EvolutionaryScale from the commercial market, but Rao’s combination of deep technical depth + startup co-founding experience + proven ability to ship at scale makes him a perpetual flight risk from a nonprofit research lab. If he leaves Biohub, he would be the single most credentialed founder in AI-for-biology. The window is not immediate (he just joined Biohub), but this is a relationship to build now for the next inflection.

Evidence

ESM3: Simulating 500 Million Years of Evolution (Science, Jan 2025)

Co-authored “Simulating 500 million years of evolution with a language model” — published as a Science cover story in January 2025. ESM3 is a 98-billion-parameter multimodal generative model that jointly reasons over protein sequence, structure, and function.

  • Trained on 2.78 billion protein sequences — the largest biological language model ever
  • Generated a novel fluorescent protein (esmGFP) at 58% sequence identity from nearest known fluorescent protein — equivalent to ~500 million years of natural evolution
  • Models available at 1.4B, 7B, and 98B parameter scales
  • Rohil Badkundri (our tracked person, Harvard early grad) is a named co-author on this paper
  • GitHub: evolutionaryscale/esm (2.3k stars, 283 forks)

ESMFold: Evolutionary-Scale Structure Prediction (Science, Mar 2023)

“Evolutionary-scale prediction of atomic-level protein structure with a language model” — published in Science. ESM-2 (15B parameters) + ESMFold achieved near-AlphaFold accuracy from single sequences (no MSA required), running 60x faster.

  • Predicted structures for 617+ million metagenomic protein sequences (ESM Metagenomic Atlas)
  • 225+ million structures predicted with high confidence
  • GitHub: facebookresearch/esm (4k stars, 780 forks, now archived)

MSA Transformer (ICML 2021)

First author. Introduced attention across rows and columns of multiple sequence alignments for protein modeling. The first method to show that transformers on MSAs capture protein contacts with high accuracy.

  • 470+ citations (Semantic Scholar)
  • Co-authored with Jason Liu, Robert Verkuil, Joshua Meier, John Canny, Pieter Abbeel, Tom Sercu, Alexander Rives

TAPE Benchmark (NeurIPS 2019 Spotlight)

First author. “Evaluating Protein Transfer Learning with TAPE” — created the first standardized benchmark for protein embeddings, analogous to GLUE for NLP. This paper catalyzed the entire field of protein language modeling by giving researchers a common evaluation framework.

  • NeurIPS 2019 Spotlight (top ~3% of submissions)
  • 5 biologically relevant tasks: secondary structure, contact prediction, remote homology, fluorescence, stability
  • TAPE GitHub: songlab-cal/tape (734 stars, 134 forks)
  • Advisors: Pieter Abbeel and John Canny (Berkeley); Yun Song (co-PI)

GPU-Accelerated t-SNE (HPML 2018 — Outstanding Paper Award)

Built tsne-cuda, a GPU-accelerated implementation of t-SNE that became widely used in the ML community.

  • GitHub: CannyLab/tsne-cuda (1.9k stars, 137 forks)
  • Outstanding Paper Award at HPML 2018

Additional Papers

  • “Language models enable zero-shot prediction of the effects of mutations on protein function” (NeurIPS 2021) — showed protein LMs predict mutation effects without any supervised training data
  • “Transformer protein language models are unsupervised structure learners” (ICLR 2021) — demonstrated that attention maps in protein transformers directly encode 3D contact information
  • “A High-Level Programming Language for Generative Protein Design” (bioRxiv 2022) — programming abstraction for protein generation

The ESM3 launch tweet was his highest-engagement post. He shared the website, blog, open code, notebook, paper, and beta API access — emphasizing open science even as a startup.

This explains the departure signal: the team hit the ceiling of what a non-bio company could support. The same dynamic could repeat at Biohub if the nonprofit structure constrains their ambition.

“Language models trained on tokens generated by evolution can act as evolutionary simulators to generate functional proteins that are far away from known proteins.” — ESM3 paper abstract, Science, Jan 2025

This is the core thesis: protein sequences are a language shaped by evolution, and large-scale language models can learn to “speak” that language well enough to create genuinely novel biology. This insight has been Rao’s research focus since his PhD (2017–2022).

“I occasionally get asked how to train a protein language model on new data. Since fairseq has added support for reading directly from fasta files, here’s a 5 min tutorial on using fairseq to train a new language model.” — @proteinrosh, Nov 2020

Early signal of builder mentality — making tools accessible, writing tutorials, lowering barriers for the community. This is the kind of developer-first instinct that translates to product thinking.

Market & Competitive Landscape

AI-powered protein design is a $1.5B market in 2025, projected to reach $7B by 2033 (21% CAGR). The broader protein engineering market is $4B+ today, heading to $9B+ by 2030. The thesis: generative AI models replace decades of wet-lab trial-and-error in drug discovery, enzyme engineering, and materials design. ESM3 is the most capable model in this space, and Rao helped build it from scratch.

Risks

  2. Nonprofit mission alignment. EvolutionaryScale was a public benefit corporation. The CZI mission (“cure or prevent all disease by 2110”) may genuinely resonate with Rao’s values. He may not want to build a for-profit company
  3. No solo founder signal. Rao was one of 5 co-founders. Alex Rives was clearly the CEO/leader. There is no evidence Rao has the “CEO gene” — he may prefer the research scientist role. Any future company would likely require him to recruit a CEO co-founder

  5. Market timing. Profluent ($106M), Generate:Biomedicines (Phase 3), and Absci (Phase 1) are already far ahead in commercialization. A new entrant from Rao in 2028 may face a crowded market
  6. No visible product/business instinct. Rao’s entire track record is research papers, open-source code, and academic benchmarks. EvolutionaryScale launched an API but was acquired before generating meaningful revenue. There is no evidence of commercial traction
