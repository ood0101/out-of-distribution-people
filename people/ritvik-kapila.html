◆ Investment Thesis

Ritvik Kapila is one of the few people who has built a 24-trillion-token pre-training dataset from scratch at Essential AI under Ashish Vaswani (the inventor of the Transformer), co-authored research on reflection in pre-training, Muon optimizer efficiency, and zero-knowledge proofs for neural networks, and now left to co-found NeoSigma — a company building custom evaluation and post-training for real-world AI agents. His co-founder Gauri Gupta (MIT Media Lab, Apple MLR, Parallel Web Systems) brings complementary post-training and evaluation expertise. Together they cover the full pipeline: Ritvik understands pre-training data curation at massive scale (24T tokens, 23.6B documents, 32K sustained requests/sec), Gauri understands post-training, RLHF, and evaluation. Both are IIT Delhi alumni, both have frontier lab pedigree, and both are in their mid-20s. NeoSigma is pre-product but actively hiring post-training researchers. The post-training/eval market is exploding — every company building AI agents needs this infrastructure, and nobody fully couples evaluation with post-training. The window to build a relationship is NOW, before they raise.

Evidence

Essential-Web v1.0: The 24T Token Dataset

Co-authored Essential-Web v1.0 (arXiv 2506.14111, June 2025) — a 24-trillion-token web dataset where every document is annotated with a 12-category taxonomy covering topic, format, content complexity, and quality. Taxonomy labels produced by EAI-Distill-0.5b, a fine-tuned 0.5B-parameter model achieving annotator agreement within 3% of Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct. The inference infrastructure (powered by Daft) processed 23.6 billion LLM queries in 7 days at 32,000 sustained requests/sec per VM with zero crashes.

With nothing more than SQL-style filters, practitioners obtain competitive datasets: math (-8.0% relative to SOTA), web code (+14.3%), STEM (+24.5%), and medical (+8.6%). This is the picks-and-shovels work that makes frontier model training possible — and Ritvik did it at a company founded by the person who invented the architecture those models use.

Rethinking Reflection in Pre-Training

Co-authored (arXiv 2504.04022, April 2025) with Vaswani et al. at Essential AI. Key finding: a language model’s ability to catch and fix its own mistakes begins emerging during pre-training, not exclusively through reinforcement learning. Proposed an exhaustive framework to measure reflection across six datasets spanning mathematics, coding, logical reasoning, and knowledge acquisition. Evaluated OLMo-2 family checkpoints, showing reflection is ubiquitous across domains and improves steadily through training.

Practical Efficiency of Muon for Pretraining

Co-authored (arXiv 2505.02222, May 2025) with 22 Essential AI researchers including Vaswani. Demonstrated that Muon (second-order optimizer) expands the Pareto frontier over AdamW on the compute-time tradeoff, retaining data efficiency at large batch sizes beyond the critical batch size. Validated through experiments with models up to 4B parameters and batch sizes up to 16M tokens. Practical recommendation: use Muon + maximal update parameterization (muP) for compute-efficient hyperparameter search at scale.

UCSD Research: Zero-Knowledge Proofs + Wearable AI

ZKROWNN (DAC 2023): First automated end-to-end framework using zero-knowledge proofs for neural network ownership verification. Enables model ownership validation in <1 second with only a few KBs of communication. Co-authored with Prof. Farinaz Koushanfar at UCSD’s Adaptive Computing lab.

ZeroHAR (AAAI 2025): Zero-shot wearable action recognition augmented with sensor context features (sensor type, Cartesian axis, body position). Published at AAAI 2025, the premier AI conference.

Market & Competitive Landscape

NeoSigma is an “intelligence layer that closes the loop between customers, products, and AI systems” via custom evaluation and post-training for real-world AI agents. The market gap: evaluation companies measure but don’t improve models; fine-tuning platforms improve but don’t evaluate in-the-loop. NeoSigma’s thesis is that evaluation and post-training must be coupled — the eval signal directly drives the training.
