The repeatable signature — clarity of thought, his own (written) words

His "you can see it" quality lives in writing, not video. The recurring thesis across his work: the vendor library is not the floor — build the primitive yourself and beat it. All quotes verbatim:

  • On multi-GPU kernels (first author, "One Kernel for All Your GPUs," Hazy Research, Sep 2025): "NVIDIA leaves much of this information undocumented or scattered and generally points us toward NCCL, but with the right understanding and abstractions, building multi-GPU kernels from scratch is far simpler than it appears." And: "We were very surprised to find that even the most basic operation, such as all-reduce, already surpasses pure NCCL performance when implemented from scratch."
  • On rebuilding the MoE layer ("1.5x faster MoE training with custom MXFP8 kernels," Cursor, Aug 2025): "We rewrote the entire MoE layer from scratch at the GPU kernel level with zero dependencies on any CUDA libraries. Instead, we used pure, good old CUDA and PTX."
  • The whole man in six words (his own tagline): "Passionate about ML systems research, startups, and loud guitars." (linkedin.com/in/stuartsul).

The polymathy is one disposition — build the primitive yourself — applied identically across compilers (LLVM comp), OS (bare-metal ELF linker), hardware (VHDL matmul accelerators), GPU kernels (ThunderKittens, megakernels, all-reduce beating NCCL), and even music tooling (coding music in ChucK). It is not a list of hobbies; it is one instinct across stacks.
