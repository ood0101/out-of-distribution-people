Evidence

HOP: Hand-Object Interaction Pretraining from Videos (ICRA 2025)

First-author paper presenting a framework to learn general robot manipulation priors from 3D hand-object interaction trajectories extracted from in-the-wild videos. The key innovation: lifting both the human hand and manipulated object into a shared 3D space, retargeting human motions to robot actions, then training a causal transformer as a task-agnostic base policy.

  • 3× greater robustness to out-of-distribution perturbations (external forces on grasped objects)
  • 2× improved generalization to unseen YCB dataset objects
  • Sample-efficient finetuning with both RL and behavior cloning
  • Real-world robotic manipulation demos on diverse objects
  • 116 GitHub stars (github.com/hgaurav2k/hop) — strong adoption for a robotics paper

Co-authors: Antonio Loquercio, Carmelo Sferrazza, Jane Wu, Haozhi Qi, Pieter Abbeel, Jitendra Malik. Funded by DARPA (Machine Common Sense + TIAMAT) and ONR MURI.

TrackR: Deep Sensorimotor Control from Human Motion (Aug 2025)

First-author paper proposing a radical simplification: instead of reconstructing 3D scenes from human videos (expensive real2sim), train a neural network to predict human hand keypoint trajectories and use those predictions as reward signals for robot RL. Key insight: keypoints on human-inspired robot end-effectors closely mirror corresponding human body keypoints, enabling zero-shot transfer from human data to robot data.

  • Single reward model works across Allegro, Xhand, and SVH hands without retraining
  • Performance “almost matches” hand-engineered dense rewards
  • Eliminates gradient-based kinematic retargeting and adversarial losses entirely
  • Scales with internet-scale human video data — the more videos, the better the reward model

This is the paper that matters most for founder potential. It demonstrates that internet-scale human video is sufficient to train robot manipulation policies — no expensive teleoperation data needed. This is the same thesis driving Physical Intelligence and Skild, but with a cleaner technical approach.

Flow Policy Gradients for Robot Control (Feb 2026)

Co-authored with Brent Yi, Rocky Duan (Covariant co-founder, now Amazon), Pieter Abbeel, Karen Liu (Stanford), and Angjoo Kanazawa. Uses flow matching policy gradients — bypassing likelihood computation entirely — to train expressive robot control policies. Demonstrated robust sim-to-real transfer on two humanoid robots for locomotion, motion tracking, and manipulation.

Note: Rocky Duan is Pieter Abbeel’s former student and Covariant co-founder (acqui-hired by Amazon for ~$200M+). Singh is now co-authoring with the Covariant lineage directly.

Video-GMAE: Tracking by Predicting 3D Gaussians Over Time (Dec 2025)

Co-authored with Jathushan Rajasegaran and Jitendra Malik. Self-supervised approach encoding video sequences into moving 3D Gaussian representations. 34.6% improvement on Kinetics dataset, 13.1% improvement on Kubric, outperforming other self-supervised video methods. Zero-shot tracking comparable to SOTA.

JEEBench: LLM Problem Solving Benchmark (EMNLP 2023)

Co-authored with Daman Arora and Mausam (IIT Delhi). Created a benchmark of 515 challenging problems from JEE Advanced exams to evaluate LLM reasoning. Found GPT-4 achieves <40% even with chain-of-thought. 39 GitHub stars. Published at EMNLP 2023 — a top NLP venue while still an undergrad.

What He’d Build (Thesis Reconstruction)

Most likely: Video-to-robot foundation model. His two core papers (HOP + TrackR) establish a pipeline: (1) extract 3D hand-object interaction trajectories from internet-scale video, (2) use those trajectories to train a task-agnostic manipulation prior, (3) finetune with minimal robot-specific data. This eliminates the teleoperation bottleneck that constrains every existing robot learning company. Physical Intelligence requires expensive human demonstrations; Skild uses sim-to-real which has a domain gap. Singh’s approach uses free, abundant human video as the data source. If it scales, it’s the most capital-efficient path to a general robot brain.
