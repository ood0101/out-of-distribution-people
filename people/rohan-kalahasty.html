a37 (applied37) — Current Company

What It Does

a37 describes itself as “an AI Research and Product Lab” reimagining DevOps. Their flagship product, Forge, is an AI-native DevOps workspace that serves as the control plane for agentic developer operations. Teams define agents in plain English to automate procedures, capture critical knowledge, and scale operational support without adding headcount.

How It Works

  • Event-driven, reusable agentic workflows integrated with AWS, Kubernetes, Terraform, Datadog, PagerDuty
  • Specialized AI agents that understand complex dependencies, predict vulnerability hotspots, and automate routine tasks
  • From provisioning cloud resources to optimizing CI/CD pipelines
  • Agents can reason across the full enterprise DevOps stack — not just single-tool automation

Cloudscript — Open Source IaC Language

a37 open-sourced Cloudscript, a next-generation Infrastructure as Code language that unifies Terraform, Ansible, and Kubernetes into a single .cloud file. Multi-provider support (AWS, GCP, Azure). HCL-based syntax with simpler for-loops, custom types, and calculated fields. 22 GitHub stars + VS Code extension (8 stars). cloudscript.ai

Market Context & Alpha Insight

◆ Alpha Insight

DevOps is the unsexy but massive pain point that AI coding tools have not solved. Cursor, Copilot, and Devin help write code, but nobody helps manage the infrastructure that code runs on. Every enterprise has a sprawl of Kubernetes clusters, Terraform configs, CI/CD pipelines, monitoring dashboards, and incident response runbooks — all managed manually by expensive platform engineering teams. a37’s bet is that AI agents can reason across the full DevOps stack, not just automate individual tools. The Cloudscript open-source project (unifying Terraform + Ansible + K8s) shows they are thinking about the problem at the language level, not just the tooling level. If Forge becomes the “Cursor for DevOps,” the TAM is enormous: every software company has a DevOps team, and those teams are perpetually understaffed.

Risks

  • Young team, limited enterprise experience. All founders are ~20 years old. Enterprise DevOps sales require trust from CTOs and VPs of Engineering who may hesitate with a team this young. The FinTech case study helps, but need more logos
  • Vytal outcome unclear. First company appears to still exist but Kalahasty moved on. Not a clear exit. Investors may ask: what happened?
  • Caltech was brief. ~5 months at Caltech before dropping out. Could be read as impulsive rather than conviction-driven (though the fundraising speed suggests the latter)
  • Crowded adjacency. While no one does exactly what a37 does, incumbent DevOps platforms (Harness, PagerDuty, Datadog) will bolt on AI features. Microsoft (Copilot for Azure DevOps) is an obvious threat
  • Open-source traction modest. Cloudscript has 22 GitHub stars. Not yet a community-driven project. Compare to Terraform (40K+ stars) at a similar stage
  • Seed investors are student-tier. Dorm Room Fund is a signal of potential but not of institutional validation. No name-brand seed fund (YC, a16z Scout, Sequoia Scout) yet visible
  • Technical moat unproven. The product description (“define agents in plain English”) sounds like it could be replicated by any team with LLM access + DevOps integrations. Need to see what makes Forge defensible beyond first-mover
