◆ The Spike

Yuandong Tian has been at or near the frontier of four distinct AI waves: computer vision (CMU, ICCV Marr Prize HM, 2013), game AI (DarkForest/OpenGo, first open-source Go AI to beat pros 20-0, 2015–2018), self-supervised learning theory (explained why BYOL/SimSiam work, ICML Outstanding Paper HM, 2020–2022), and LLM reasoning/efficiency (StreamingLLM, GaLore, MobileLLM, Coconut, 2022–2025). Very few researchers have published award-winning first-author work across this many subfields.

The game AI chapter is the clearest signal. He built DarkForest at Facebook concurrently with DeepMind’s AlphaGo — one of only a handful of people in the world independently building deep learning Go engines at that moment. Then ELF OpenGo became the first open-source system to beat professional Go players (20-0 sweep), trained on 2,000 GPUs, with all code and 20M self-play games released publicly. Only DeepMind had previously achieved superhuman Go; Tian did it open-source.

After 11 years at Meta FAIR, he was laid off in the October 2025 cuts (his team had been redirected to fix Llama 4 problems). He rejected offers from OpenAI, Nvidia, Google DeepMind, and Tencent to co-found Recursive — likely focused on the continuous latent reasoning paradigm he pioneered with Coconut. He is also a published Chinese science fiction novelist with multiple books. The combination of world-class theory, production engineering, creative writing, and the conviction to leave big tech at 42 is singular.

Background

Yuandong Tian (田渊栋) is from Shanghai, China. BS and MS in Computer Science from Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU, C9 League, 2001–2008). PhD in Robotics from Carnegie Mellon University (2008–2013), advised by Srinivasa G. Narasimhan. Thesis: “Theory and Practice of Globally Optimal Deformation Estimation.” Committee included Martial Hebert, Fernando De La Torre, and C. Lawrence Zitnick (Microsoft Research). Won ICCV Marr Prize Honorable Mention (2013) for PhD work.

Spent a year at Google [X] Self-Driving Car (2013–2014) before joining Facebook AI Research (FAIR) in 2014, where he spent 11 years rising to Research Scientist Director. Led three major research threads: game AI (DarkForest, ELF, OpenGo), self-supervised learning theory (DirectPred, dimensional collapse), and LLM efficiency/reasoning (StreamingLLM, GaLore, MobileLLM, Coconut). Collaborated extensively with Yann LeCun, Jason Weston, Larry Zitnick, and Song Han.

In January 2025, his team was redirected from core research to fix Llama 4 problems with a 2-month deadline. In October 2025, the entire team was laid off (~600 people). Tian publicly stated the people responsible for the issues were not the people laid off. He rejected offers from OpenAI, Nvidia, Google DeepMind, and Tencent to co-found Recursive (stealth, Series A reportedly nearly closed). Trilingual: Chinese (native), English (fluent), Japanese (JLPT Level 2). Published science fiction novelist.

The Overlooked Angle

The standard narrative is “senior Meta researcher starts an AI company.” That undersells what’s actually distinctive. Tian built DarkForest at the same time DeepMind built AlphaGo — independently arriving at the same insight that deep neural networks could play Go at superhuman levels. He was one of perhaps 5–10 people in the world who saw this simultaneously. Then he open-sourced what DeepMind kept proprietary.

The theoretical depth is the other tell. His ICML 2021 Outstanding Paper didn’t just build a better self-supervised model — it explained mathematically why existing ones work. His ICLR 2022 paper proved contrastive learning is approximately PCA. This is a researcher who doesn’t just engineer systems but derives the theory underneath them. The Coconut work extends this: instead of asking “how do we make chain-of-thought better?” he asked “what if reasoning doesn’t need to happen in token space at all?”

The science fiction writing reveals something about cognitive style. His novels feature PhD researchers grappling with consciousness and ambition. This is not a researcher who optimizes metrics — this is someone who thinks about what intelligence means. That philosophical depth, combined with the engineering chops to ship systems that beat world champions, is why the Recursive bet matters.
