Background

Pushmeet Kohli grew up in Dehradun, a hill town in Uttarakhand, India. As a child he aspired to become a teacher. He studied Computer Science & Engineering at the National Institute of Technology (NIT), Warangal, graduating with a BTech in 2004. He then moved to the UK for doctoral studies at Oxford Brookes University under Philip H. S. Torr, completing his PhD in 2007 with a thesis on graph cut-based energy minimization for computer vision. The thesis won the BMVA Sullivan Doctoral Thesis Award and was runner-up for the BCS Distinguished Dissertation Award.

After a postdoctoral stint at Trinity Hall, Cambridge (Psychometric Centre), he joined Microsoft Research where he spent nearly 11 years across labs in Seattle, Cambridge (UK), and Bangalore. He served as Director of Research for the Cognition Group and as technical advisor to Rick Rashid, Microsoft's Chief Research Officer. In 2017, he joined DeepMind (now Google DeepMind), where he founded and leads the AI for Science & Strategic Initiatives Unit.

The Overlooked Angle

The conventional narrative focuses on Demis Hassabis and John Jumper for AlphaFold's Nobel. But Pushmeet Kohli is the organizational architect who made the entire AI-for-Science portfolio possible. He didn't build one system — he built the factory that produces breakthrough systems across domains. His background is the anti-pedigree story: NIT Warangal (not IIT), Oxford Brookes (not Oxford), and yet he outperformed the entire field.

His PhD thesis on graph cuts — an elegant piece of optimization theory — is the intellectual seed for his career pattern: finding mathematical structures that unlock practical problems at scale. AlphaFold is graph cuts at protein scale. AlphaEvolve is search at algorithm scale. The through-line is always: optimization under structure.

The other overlooked dimension: he wanted to be a teacher as a kid in Dehradun. And in a sense, that's exactly what he became — his systems teach us the structure of proteins, the geometry of algorithms, the grammar of scientific discovery. He just teaches through AI instead of a classroom.
