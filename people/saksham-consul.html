2. FLUX.1 Kontext (arXiv 2506.15742, Jun 17, 2025)

Co-authored: “FLUX.1 Kontext: Flow Matching for In-Context Image Generation and Editing in Latent Space.” Architecture: a unified flow-matching model that uses a simple sequence-concatenation approach to handle both local editing and free-form generation in one network, removing the typical hand-off between text-to-image and inpaint/edit pipelines. Released both as FLUX.1 Kontext [dev] (open weights, 12B-scale rectified-flow transformer lineage) and FLUX.1 Kontext [pro / max] via the BFL and partner APIs (Replicate, Fal, Together, Cloudflare). The release shipped with a paired benchmark, KontextBench (1,026 image-prompt pairs across five edit categories), which BFL has positioned as the successor to GenAI-Bench for editing evals. 61 citations on the lead Scholar entry already (with a second entry at 15 — a parallel preprint version) within a year of release.

Background

Indian-origin (Punjabi surname “Consul”, Khatri caste). Schooling in India, then BITS Pilani for B.E. Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering with a CGPA of 9.99/10 and branch-topper rank. His third-year industrial-research internship was at IIT Delhi working on EEG-based motor-task classification + a cognitive tutor for children with Autism Spectrum Disorder. He then did a bachelor’s thesis at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems in Tübingen, in Falk Lieder’s Rationality Enhancement group — cognitive science meets RL meets planning, the same group that published “Discovering Robust Planning Strategies” (CogSci 2020) with him as a co-author. He is listed on the MPI site as “has transitioned from the institute (Alumni)”.

He landed Stanford MS (initially EE, ultimately Mechanical Engineering) for Fall 2021, where he joined ARMLab under Monroe Kennedy III (Assistant Professor of ME + CS by courtesy; NSF Faculty Early Career Award) — the Assistive Robotics & Manipulation Lab. He interned at Nvidia in Autonomous Driving during his MS, then took a short post-MS role at Anyware Robotics, then pivoted to LLM systems as a Founding ML Systems Engineer at Lamini (Sharon Zhou, Greg Diamos — the post-MosaicML, Llama-fine-tuning platform). At Lamini he co-built the DeepLearning.AI “Finetuning LLMs” course and was a co-author on the “Banishing LLM Hallucinations Requires Rethinking Generalization” Memory-Tuning paper. He moved to Black Forest Labs as Member of Technical Staff (Training) sometime in 2024, and is now a named author on FLUX.1 Kontext (Jun 2025).
