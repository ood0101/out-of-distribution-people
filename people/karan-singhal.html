◆ Investment Thesis

Karan Singhal is the first — and so far only senior — member of the Med-PaLM/AMIE cluster to have publicly left Google for another of the 20 CLAUDE.md tracked companies (OpenAI). The recursive-discovery thesis from the Vivek Natarajan dossier is that the most-likely next wave of healthcare AI founders is the Med-PaLM first-author bench; Karan is the most senior name on that list and the only one to have already moved. He has not founded anything. He is currently running OpenAI’s entire health surface (ChatGPT for Healthcare, HealthBench, the Penda Health RCT, ChatGPT Health for consumers) and has consolidated enough internal weight to also lead the AGI Benefits team — per his own personal site. The OpenAI Foundation conversion (the non-profit arm with the $250M Economic Futures program + AI Resilience program, both launched May 2026) is the new departure-watch surface: senior OpenAI researchers (Yo Shavit publicly, May 25 2026) are already moving from the corporate side to the Foundation. Karan retweeted Shavit’s resignation post and his bio still says “Health AI, AGI benefits, safety @OpenAI” — same affiliation as of late May 2026 — but the team adjacency is now active. Tier 1. Reference class: Roshan Rao (T1, FAIR → EvolutionaryScale, the bio analogue) and Vivek Natarajan (T1, the canonical anchor he’s one career step ahead of). What would push him to T0: a LinkedIn or X-bio change, an OpenAI Foundation role announcement, an OpenAI Health spin-out or acquisition of his sub-team (the Jan 2026 Torch acquisition signals OpenAI is comfortable buying healthcare orgs — the reverse could come), or any Khaled Saab / Yo Shavit / Boaz Barak co-departure signal.

What he was hired to build (his own framing on the Cog Rev podcast)

“I have a bit of a background as a researcher who cares about safety and has worked on safety research in the past. Part of the motivation for coming to OpenAI and working on health for me was thinking about the setting as a place that can provide concrete grounding for technical work on safety and alignment. I had a feeling — and this was about two years ago — that a lot of the most ambitious, medium and long-term work on safety and alignment was going on in toy settings or with math problems or things like this. What the shape of the migration tells us

Karan didn’t move from Google to OpenAI to do incrementally what he was doing at Google. He moved to commercialize what he was doing at Google. AMIE is still a research artifact at Google DeepMind (Beth Israel feasibility study Mar 2026, 100 patients, supervised). ChatGPT for Healthcare is a shipped HIPAA-compliant product at 8 named hospital systems and serving >230M weekly health-question users 17 months after he joined. The Vivek-side AMIE work is the better-evaluated science; the Karan-side ChatGPT for Healthcare is the better-deployed product. This is the divergence between the two halves of the Med-PaLM founding bench.

OpenAI Health: What It Is, Publicly

OpenAI Health is not a separately incorporated entity. It is a research + product surface inside OpenAI that maps to two consumer-facing and one enterprise-facing product family:

  • ChatGPT Health (consumer): launched Jan 2026. Lets users upload medical records, connect Apple Health / wearables, and ask health questions. >230M weekly health-question users on ChatGPT broadly; ChatGPT Health is the dedicated surface with privacy protections.
  • OpenAI for Healthcare (enterprise): launched Jan 8 2026. HIPAA-compliant ChatGPT + API with BAA. Includes evidence retrieval with citations, institutional policy integrations (SharePoint), reusable templates, SAML SSO + SCIM, customer-managed encryption keys, audit logs. Built on GPT-5.2 with healthcare-specific evaluation.
  • ChatGPT for Clinicians (clinician-facing, free): launched Apr 22 2026 for verified US physicians, NPs, pharmacists. Documentation + clinical research + decision support. HealthBench Professional released the same day on HuggingFace.
