As an undergrad she co-authored AttentionViz (IEEE VIS 2023, 171 cites) on transformer interpretability in Wattenberg + Viégas’s lab — then joined Decagon in Sep 2024 when the company was ~10 people and personally built the company’s Trace View observability platform. 2. AttentionViz — the real research credential

Co-authored AttentionViz: A Global View of Transformer Attention (Yeh, Y. Chen, Wu, C. Chen, Viégas, Wattenberg) at IEEE VIS 2023 / TVCG 2024. 171 Scholar citations as of May 2026. The paper introduces an interactive tool for visualizing self-attention patterns at scale across both language (BERT, GPT-2) and vision (ViT) transformers — foundational interpretability infrastructure. Authored as an undergraduate in the Insight + Interaction Lab (Wattenberg + Viégas, both ex-Google PAIR co-founders). The lab page now lists her as “Undergraduate → SWE @ Decagon.”

3. Solo-built Decagon’s Trace View — observability is the moat layer

Direct quote (Decagon “17 Questions” interview, Jan 20, 2026): “I built our Trace View product, which is an agent observability platform that helps both customers and internal teams debug conversations faster.” Trace View turns Decagon’s Agent Operating Procedures (AOPs) into auditable, step-by-step traces — exposing inputs, model instructions, tool calls, and decisions at every step. Per the Dec 12, 2025 launch post: “At Decagon, we believe AI agents shouldn’t be black boxes.” She is also the named author of the AOP Copilot launch blog (Sept 23, 2025) — an AI assistant that converts natural language to production-ready agent workflows. Through-line: AttentionViz (visualizing transformer internals) → Trace View (visualizing agent internals). Her thesis is interpretability/observability of complex AI systems, applied first to research models and now to production agents.
