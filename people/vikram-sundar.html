FLIGHTED — the technical contribution. “Fitness Landscape Inference Generated by High-Throughput Experimental Data” (bioRxiv 2024; Sundar, Boqiang Tu, Lindsey Guan, Esvelt). High-throughput protein assays produce noisy readouts — the measured signal is not the true fitness. FLIGHTED is a Bayesian method that generates probabilistic fitness landscapes with calibrated errors: it accounts for experimental uncertainty and infers the latent fitness landscape, so that downstream ML fitness models train on de-noised, uncertainty-aware labels rather than raw assay counts. It is deliberately general — applicable to any high-throughput assay and any ML model — and he applied it to engineer alternate substrate specificities of TEV protease (the PhD thesis payoff), validating it on an ultra-high-throughput activity assay. This is exactly the “data quality / label noise is the bottleneck” insight that the best protein-ML founders (Stanton’s lab-in-the-loop work at Genentech, EvolutionaryScale’s ESM data pipelines) build companies around.
