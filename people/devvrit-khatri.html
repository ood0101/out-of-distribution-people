The Spike — With Evidence

1. ScaleRL: 400,000+ GPU-Hour RL Scaling Study (ICLR 2026 Oral)

Lead author of “The Art of Scaling Reinforcement Learning Compute for LLMs” (Oct 2025) — the first large-scale systematic study defining a principled framework for analyzing and predicting RL scaling in LLMs. Key findings: (1) different algorithmic recipes do not converge to identical asymptotic performance; (2) loss aggregation, normalization, curriculum, and off-policy algorithms primarily modulate compute efficiency without shifting the asymptote; (3) stable recipes follow predictable trajectories enabling extrapolation from smaller runs. The proposed ScaleRL recipe was validated on a single RL run scaled to 100,000 GPU-hours. Accepted as an Oral at ICLR 2026 — top ~1% of submissions. 28-page paper with 20 figures. Co-authors span Meta, UT Austin, UCL, UC Berkeley, Harvard, and Google.

2. MatFormer: Nested Transformer for Elastic Inference (NeurIPS 2024)

First author. A Matryoshka-style Transformer architecture that trains one model and extracts hundreds of accurate smaller models at zero additional training cost. An 850M MatLM decoder-only model yields submodels from 582M–850M parameters, each outperforming independently trained counterparts on validation loss and one-shot downstream tasks. MatFormer-based ViT encoders preserve metric-space structure for adaptive large-scale retrieval. Speculative decoding with MatFormer submodels provides significant inference latency reduction. Won Best Paper at ENLSP@NeurIPS 2023 workshop before main conference acceptance at NeurIPS 2024.

3. DiskANN: Billion-Point Nearest Neighbor Search (NeurIPS 2019)

Co-author (during BITS Pilani final year at Microsoft Research India). DiskANN indexes, stores, and searches 1 billion vectors on a single workstation with just 64GB RAM and an SSD. On SIFT1B: >5,000 queries/sec at <3ms mean latency with 95%+ 1-recall@1, where FAISS and IVFOADC+G+P plateau at ~50% recall with similar memory. DiskANN is now deployed across Microsoft Bing, Microsoft 365, Azure Cosmos DB, and Azure PostgreSQL. The paper has accumulated 600+ citations and spawned follow-up systems (SPANN, SPFresh, Filtered-DiskANN, OOD-DiskANN) powering hundreds of billions of vectors at Microsoft scale. Led by Harsha Vardhan Simhadri (Senior Principal Researcher, Microsoft).

What He’d Build (If He Started a Company)

RL Scaling Infrastructure for Frontier Models

His ScaleRL work defines the first principled framework for predicting RL scaling in LLMs. This is the missing piece for every lab doing RL post-training: how to allocate compute, which recipes scale, and how to predict performance before committing 100K+ GPU-hours. A company built on this could sell RL training infrastructure or consulting to every model lab that is not OpenAI/Anthropic/DeepMind.

Efficient Inference / Elastic Model Serving

MatFormer’s core insight — train once, deploy hundreds of size-variants — addresses the #1 deployment pain point for every company serving LLMs across heterogeneous hardware (cloud, edge, mobile). Combined with his work on many-shot compression and test-time training, he could build an inference optimization layer that sits between model providers and deployment.
