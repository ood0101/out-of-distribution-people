◆ The Alpha — what’s actually special, measured honestly

The one genuinely distinctive idea in her record is her first-author “Theory of Mind Using Active Inference”: a mechanism for multi-agent cooperation with neither a shared generative model nor an explicit communication channel — each agent infers the others’ intent and coordinates on that. If it holds up, that’s a clean, principled alternative to the two standard routes (shared reward / centralized training, or learned communication protocols).

But calibrate it honestly — this is the part the alpha-first lens is for. That paper is brand new (~1 citation) and unproven; it is essentially the only first-author idea of its kind in her record so far. Her headline Scholar numbers are not her AI alpha: the big-cite items are a 2018 education-psychology paper (~114 cites, pre-Oxford) and a large multi-author VERSES manifesto (~86 cites). Her own first-author active-inference papers are each <10 cites. So the precise read is: a promising new framing from an early-career researcher — potential, not yet a demonstrated alpha.

What is already real is positional: she is unusually trusted inside active inference for her stage — IWAI 2024 organizing chair, IWAI 2025 proceedings co-editor, and a genuine Karl Friston protégé (he is her DPhil co-supervisor, her former VERSES employer, and a co-author). That lineage + community trust is the distinction today; the independent body of special work is still ahead of her.
