What Effective AI Is Building

“Intelligence Infrastructure for Insurance” — AI agents for P&L teams at insurers (actuaries, product managers, business teams). Three core products:

  • Rate Implementation: Agents read insurance filings (thousands of pages of rate tables, territory definitions, class plans, exception logic), extract every rule, and produce a complete executable rater. A formal verification language captures the rating logic in a readable, auditable format — each rule traced to its regulatory source. Actuaries verify rule-by-rule instead of re-deriving.
  • Competitive Analysis: Pick a state, select competitors, define a population — agents run simulations to break down where you win, where you lose, and why, down to individual rating factors.
  • Filing Preparation: Produces filing-ready exhibits with loss development, rate need, and competitive positioning — formatted to each state’s regulatory expectations, every figure cited.

The key insight: insurance actuarial work is high-value, deeply regulated, and currently manual. Rate implementation involves months of human work to process filings into executable rating engines. Effective collapses this to hours. The formal verification layer is the moat — it makes AI output auditable enough for actuaries to sign off on, which is non-negotiable in a regulated industry.
