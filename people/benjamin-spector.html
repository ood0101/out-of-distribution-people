The pattern: every time he touches a system — a competition, an incubator, a GPU abstraction, a company — it becomes a platform that generates outsized returns for everyone around it. He's not an optimizer. He's a catalyst.

Research & Publications

ThunderKittens: Simple, Fast, and Adorable AI Kernels

ICLR 2025 Spotlight. 3,200+ GitHub stars. Co-authored with Simran Arora, Aaryan Singhal, Daniel Y. Fu, and Christopher Ré. A framework for writing performant AI kernels that maps abstractions to GPU hierarchy levels — warp-level 16×16 matrix tiles as fundamental data structures, PyTorch-like parallel compute operations. The Flash Attention kernel in TK is 100 lines and outperforms FlashAttention-2 on H100 by ~30%. Design philosophy: keep tensor cores busy (94% of H100 compute). Ecosystem: ThunderKittens 2.0 (Blackwell GPUs), ThunderMLA (35% faster than FlashMLA, 400 LOC), HipKittens (AMD), ParallelKittens (multi-GPU), ThunderMittens (Apple Metal).

Scaling Laws for Precision

ICLR 2025 Oral. With Tanishq Kumar, Zachary Ankner, et al. Precision-aware scaling laws for training and inference. Key findings: lower-precision training reduces "effective parameter count"; post-training quantization degradation increases with more data, eventually making additional pretraining harmful; training larger models in lower precision may be compute-optimal.

Staged Speculative Decoding

ESFOMO @ ICML 2023. Technique for accelerating LLM inference through staged draft-verify cycles. Achieves 3.16× latency reduction.

LoLCATs

ICLR 2025. Linearizing LLMs via low-rank approximation of attention — converting standard transformers into linear attention models while preserving quality.
