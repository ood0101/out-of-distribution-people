What She’s Building: Topological

The Company

Topological (topological.com, YC S25) develops physics-based foundation models for CAD optimization. The thesis: hardware teams should iterate at the same speed as software teams. Traditional topology optimization uses finite element methods that take hours per simulation. Topological’s UToP-v1 learns the underlying physics of how materials behave under stress as patterns (not equations to re-solve from scratch), enabling 1,930x speedup at <5% compliance error. Team: 2 people (Zeyneb Kaya, CTO + Nicole Ma, CEO). YC primary partner: Brad Flora.

Market

  • Simulation software market: $5B+ (dominated by Ansys, Siemens Simcenter, Altair, COMSOL)
  • CAD/CAM/CAE market: $15B+ and growing with additive manufacturing adoption
  • Incumbents are adding AI features to 30-year-old architectures. Topological is building from scratch with modern ML infrastructure

  • Extremely young. Both founders are ~19–20 (Stanford undergrads). No industry experience. No enterprise sales experience. Hardware/manufacturing companies buy from people they trust
  • Deep tech risk. 1930x speedup on toy problems may not generalize to complex real-world multi-physics, multi-material, multi-constraint optimization problems. Benchmark validation against incumbents is critical
  • 2-person team. No engineering team, no sales team, no domain experts. Need to hire aggressively. Deep tech + enterprise = expensive talent
  • Academic competitors. OAT (MIT/Northeastern, Oct 2025) achieves sub-1-second inference across resolutions up to 256x256. The academic community is converging on this approach
  • Market adoption. Hardware engineers are conservative. Simulation tools must be trusted before adoption. This is a multi-year sales cycle market
  • Business model unclear. SaaS subscription? Per-simulation charge? API integration? The answer determines whether Topological is a tool, a platform, or a feature
