The Spike — With Evidence

1. ScaleRL: 400,000+ GPU-Hour RL Scaling Study (ICLR 2026 Oral)

Co-lead author of “The Art of Scaling Reinforcement Learning Compute for LLMs” (arXiv:2510.13786, Oct 2025) — the first principled framework for predicting RL scaling in LLMs. The paper fits sigmoidal compute–performance curves for RL training and finds: (1) different algorithmic recipes do not converge to the same asymptotic performance — the asymptote is recipe-dependent; (2) details such as loss aggregation, normalization, curriculum, and off-policy correction primarily modulate compute efficiency without shifting the asymptote; (3) stable recipes follow predictable trajectories that allow extrapolation from small runs. The proposed ScaleRL recipe was validated on a single RL training run scaled to 100,000 GPU-hours — the first time anyone has demonstrated predictable RL scaling at that magnitude. Accepted as an Oral at ICLR 2026 (top ~1%). The work was done inside Meta and is one of the foundational empirical papers for how the next generation of post-trained LLMs (DeepSeek R1, OpenAI o-series, Anthropic thinking models) will be scaled.

2. Rethinking Thinking Tokens / LLMs as Improvement Operators — PDR (Oct 2025)

First author on arXiv:2510.01123, with Aniket Didolkar, Suchin Gururangan, John Quan, Ruan Silva, Ruslan Salakhutdinov, Manzil Zaheer, Sanjeev Arora, and Anirudh Goyal. Reframes the LLM as an “improvement operator” on its own thoughts and proposes Parallel-Distill-Refine (PDR): (i) generate diverse drafts in parallel, (ii) distill them into a bounded textual workspace, (iii) refine conditioned on the workspace and feed forward to the next round. This decouples context length from total generated tokens — a structural fix to the cost/latency blow-up of long chain-of-thought reasoning. On math benchmarks PDR delivers +11% on AIME 2024 and +9% on AIME 2025 over single-pass baselines at matched compute. Endorsed publicly by Russ Salakhutdinov. This is a high-impact technical lever for any company doing test-time compute / thinking models, and Lovish is the first author.

4. Beyond Verifiable Rewards — Scaling RL to Unverifiable Data (NeurIPS 2025)

Author on “Beyond Verifiable Rewards: Scaling Reinforcement Learning for Language Models to Unverifiable Data” (NeurIPS 2025). One of the first systematic attempts to extend the RLHF/RLVR playbook beyond domains with cleanly verifiable reward signals (math/code) into open-ended generation. 5. MLGym — First Gym Environment for ML Research Agents (COLM 2025)

Co-author on “MLGym: A New Framework and Benchmark for Advancing AI Research Agents” (COLM 2025) — the first Gym-style environment and benchmark for evaluating LLM agents on actual ML research tasks. Highly relevant to the entire “AI scientist” thesis (Sakana, FutureHouse, etc.).

6. Treeformer — Dense Gradient Trees for Efficient Attention (ICLR 2023)

First author with Prateek Jain (Google Research India) and Srinadh Bhojanapalli on Treeformer at ICLR 2023. Tree-structured attention computation that delivers efficient sub-quadratic attention while preserving gradient flow. ICLR first authorship while still at Google Research India, pre-PhD — an early-career signal of independent research capability.
