The Spike — With Evidence

Hussein has three concrete, high-signal technical contributions that separate him from the typical Stanford new grad:

1. One-Minute Video Generation with TTT (CVPR 2025)

Third author on a collaboration between Stanford and NVIDIA. The paper augments a 5B-parameter pre-trained Transformer with Test-Time Training layers — whose hidden states are themselves neural networks — to generate one-minute Tom & Jerry cartoons from text storyboards in a single pass with no editing or stitching. TTT layers led by 34 Elo points over Mamba 2, Gated DeltaNet, and sliding-window attention baselines in human evaluation of 100 videos per method. This work was done during his research stint at SAIL under Yu Sun (the inventor of test-time training) in collaboration with NVIDIA’s Jan Kautz and Jiarui Xu. Paper: arXiv:2504.05298.

2. π*0.6: a VLA That Learns From Experience (Physical Intelligence, Nov 2025)

Co-author on Physical Intelligence’s flagship RL paper. RECAP (RL with Experience and Corrections via Advantage-conditioned Policies) enables vision-language-action models to self-improve through real-world deployment. Results: more than doubled task throughput and roughly halved failure rate on the hardest tasks. After training with RECAP, π*0.6 made espresso drinks continuously from 5:30am to 11:30pm (18+ hours), folded 50 different novel laundry items in a new home, and assembled/labeled 59 boxes in a real chocolate factory. Over 90% success rates on complex tasks. Paper: arXiv:2511.14759. 51 co-authors (full Physical Intelligence team).

3. CyBench (ICLR 2025 Oral)

Co-author on the cybersecurity evaluation framework with Percy Liang and Dan Boneh. 40 professional-level Capture the Flag tasks across 4 competitions. Evaluated 8 frontier models (GPT-4o, o1-preview, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Gemini 1.5 Pro, etc.). Adopted by both the US AISI and UK AISI as the only open-source cybersecurity benchmark in their joint pre-deployment tests on Claude 3.5 Sonnet and OpenAI o1. ICLR Oral acceptance = top ~1% of submissions. Paper: arXiv:2408.08926.
