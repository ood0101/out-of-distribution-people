ICCV 2025: TOTO (Autoregressive Pre-training from Videos)

Co-authored with Jathushan Rajasegaran, Ilija Radosavovic, Yossi Gandelsman, Christoph Feichtenhofer (Meta AI), and Jitendra Malik. Key results:

  • Trained LLaMA architecture on 1 trillion+ visual tokens via next-token prediction on video
  • Model sizes: 120M, 280M, 1.1B parameters with 4,096-token context (16 frames)
  • Scaling law: L(C) = 7.32 × C-0.0378 (cf. GPT-3: L(C) = 2.57 × C-0.0480)
  • First to show competitive Kinetics-400 action recognition with autoregressive pre-training
  • 63% real-world robot manipulation success (cube picking) with TOTO-base features
  • Competitive ImageNet classification among autoregressive models; viable Ego4D object detection

The insight that vision models follow power-law compute scaling similar to language models (at a different rate) is thesis-level: it implies the same scaling playbook that worked for LLMs can work for video/embodied AI. This is the intellectual foundation for a company.

CVPR 2025: Diffusion Scaling Laws

“Scaling Properties of Diffusion Models for Perceptual Tasks” — co-first author (equal contribution) with Zeeshan Patel, plus Jathushan Rajasegaran and Jitendra Malik. Also filed as his Berkeley EECS senior thesis (EECS-2025-38). Unifies depth estimation, optical flow, and amodal segmentation under diffusion. Key results:

  • ETH3D depth: AbsRel 4.8 (vs Marigold 6.5, DPT 7.8) — 58% improvement over DPT
  • 3-4 orders of magnitude less compute than Marigold for competitive results
  • Concrete scaling law: L(C) = 0.23 × C-0.0098
  • MoE upcycling improves AbsRel by 5.3%, delta1 by 8.6%
  • 16 citations as of March 2026
