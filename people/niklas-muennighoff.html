The Spike — With Evidence

1. s1: Replicated o1 Reasoning for <$50

First-authored s1: Simple test-time scaling (Jan 2025). Curated just 1,000 training examples (s1K), fine-tuned Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct, and introduced “budget forcing” — appending “Wait” tokens to force the model to double-check its reasoning. Result: s1-32B exceeded o1-preview on competition math by up to 27% (MATH: 92.6% vs. o1-preview baseline; AIME24: 50% → 57% with budget forcing). Total training cost: ~$20 in H100 rentals, <30 minutes on 16 GPUs. The s1 repo has 6,650 GitHub stars. Published at EMNLP 2025; won ICLR 2025 Reasoning Workshop Best Paper. Co-authors include Li Fei-Fei, Percy Liang, Emmanuel Candès, and Tatsunori Hashimoto.

2. MTEB: The Standard for Embedding Evaluation

Created MTEB (Massive Text Embedding Benchmark) at Hugging Face in Oct 2022. 8 embedding tasks, 58 datasets, 112 languages, 33 models benchmarked. Now expanded to 1,000+ tasks with MMTEB follow-up. 6M+ total installs. Used by OpenAI, Google, and Meta to evaluate their embedding models. 200K monthly active users on the leaderboard. Published at EACL 2023. ~1,700 citations. Muennighoff is first author.

3. OLMoE: First Fully Open MoE LLM

Led a team of 23 researchers at AI2 to build OLMoE-1B-7B — 7B total parameters, 1.3B active per token, trained on 5 trillion tokens. Outperformed all open models with similar active parameters, surpassing Llama2-13B-Chat and DeepSeekMoE-16B. Fully open: weights, training data, code, logs, and 244 intermediate checkpoints. Published at ICLR 2025. GitHub: 988 stars.

4. GRIT: Unified Generation + Embedding

First-authored GRIT (Generative Representational Instruction Tuning) at Contextual AI (Feb 2024). Trained a single LLM to handle both generative and embedding tasks via instructions. GritLM 7B set SOTA on MTEB while outperforming all models up to its size on generative benchmarks. Sped up RAG by >60% for long documents by eliminating the need for separate retrieval and generation models. GitHub: 689 stars.

5. Additional First-Author Work

  • Crosslingual Generalization through Multitask Finetuning (ACL 2023) — Best Paper Award. Released BLOOMZ and mT0 models. ~1,100 citations.
  • Scaling Data-Constrained Language Models (NeurIPS 2023) — Outstanding Main Track Runner-Up. First to train LLMs across 1000s of AMD GPUs. ~600 citations.
  • SGPT: GPT Sentence Embeddings for Semantic Search (2022) — First work (concurrent with OpenAI) to use LLMs for embeddings. All top embedders now use this approach.
  • OctoPack (2023) — Code LLMs (OctoCoder, CommitPack) trained on largest dataset of git commits.
