Technical Deep Dive

Smoothie — why label-free routing matters

LM routing (pick the right model for each request) is a $100M+ ARR category today (Martian raised ~$9M, NotDiamond raised ~$2.3M, OpenRouter is at material scale, Portkey, LiteLLM, Together’s own router). The commercial problem is that routing quality depends on labels — you need to know which model was right to train a router. Enterprises don’t have those labels. Smoothie removes the label requirement entirely. The algorithm: embed every generation from every LLM with a small encoder (e.g., BERT); generations clustering near the centroid get high quality scores; outliers get low scores. Two variants: Smoothie-Global (one score per LLM across the whole dataset) and Smoothie-Local (per-sample k-NN). Results: +15 avg / +27 max points on AlpacaEval, +9.6 points on multi-task, 75%+ optimal-routing rate. This is a meaningful algorithmic step over voting / self-consistency baselines.

Why a company could be built on this: Smoothie is the first plug-in routing algorithm that a Series-A customer can deploy without retraining. Martian sells training-based routing; NotDiamond sells a hosted layer; a Smoothie-style product could be an SDK that any multi-model deployment drops in and gets 10–30% quality uplift at zero labeling cost.

Agentic Information Theory — why the compressor is the product

The ICLR 2026 paper reframes agentic systems (Deep Research, Claude Code, Cursor, Devin, Manus) as compressor–predictor pipelines: a smaller model distills context into a compact representation; a larger model consumes it. The authors model the compressor as a noisy channel and ask: how many bits of task-relevant information survive? Their mutual-information estimator is a task-agnostic metric — you can optimize it without ground-truth labels on the downstream task.

Empirical punchline: compressor scaling dominates predictor scaling. 1B→7B compressor with fixed predictor → +60% accuracy; 70B→405B predictor with fixed compressor → +12%. A 7B compressor conveys 5.5× more bits per token than a 1.5B. On DeepResearch Bench, compute-optimized systems hit 102% of frontier performance at 28% of cost. This is the quantitative justification for a wave of “small local model + big cloud model” architectures (Minions, Factorial, specialized agents) — and Ishan is inside the paper that defines how to measure when this works.

WONDERBREAD — the benchmark your portfolio company uses

2,928 documented workflow demonstrations, 6 novel BPM tasks sourced from real enterprise scenarios. The benchmark tests whether multimodal foundation models can watch screen recordings and produce structured process artifacts (flowcharts, SOPs). This is the evaluation layer underneath every computer-use agent company — Kinetic Systems (Wornow), Adept (defunct-but-team-dispersed), Anthropic Computer Use, OpenAI Operator. Ishan is a co-author with both Wornow and Avanika Narayan.
