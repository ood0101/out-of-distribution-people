◆ Investment Thesis — Honest Call: T2 (Track, Don’t Pursue)

Alberto Taiuti is exactly the kind of founder this fund is built to find — a real-time-graphics/GPU engineer who has spent his career shipping at the frontier (Apple Vision Pro, founding-team Luma, Skydio autonomy) and is now CEO of a world-models company, the single thesis the owner cares most about. The problem is timing, and it is dispositive: his company Reactor emerged from stealth on May 28, 2026 with a $59M round led by Lightspeed Venture Partners (with Jeffrey Katzenberg’s WndrCo). That is a funded Series A — squarely the “too late for us” zone the fund’s mandate calls out. We cannot write a $500K–$1M inception check into a company that just raised $59M from a multistage lead. This is a relationship-and-tracking entry, not an active pursuit.

Calibrate the pedigree honestly, too. His site says “cofounded Luma AI,” and his LinkedIn lists “CTO & Cofounder, Oct 2021–Jul 2022.” He was a genuine founding-team member from Luma’s first months — but the publicly recognized co-founders are Amit Jain (CEO) and Alex Yu (CTO); a16z’s and TechCrunch’s founding narratives name Yu, not Taiuti, as CTO. He left after ~10 months — before Dream Machine, before the video pivot, before Luma became a $1B+ company — and went to an IC role at Skydio. So: real early-Luma credibility, but not “the co-founder of $4B Luma.” Don’t over-market the line; the honest version is strong enough.

Why this dossier still earns its place: it is the anchor for the real-time-world-models cluster — the map of who is building in the space (Decart, World Labs, Overworld, Odyssey, Genie) and where the next inception-stage founder in this lineage will come from. The strongest thread the research surfaced was Alex Yu (Luma’s recognized Cofounder & CTO; PlenOctrees / Plenoxels) — but verification settled it: Yu left Luma in early 2025 and joined OpenAI’s image-generation team (on stage with Sam Altman demoing ChatGPT Images 2.0 in Apr 2026), so he is not building now either. Neither Alberto nor Yu is a live inception target. The cluster’s value is as a watch-list: Yu becomes a hard T0 the moment he leaves OpenAI (ex-Luma-CTO + ex-OpenAI-image-lead = top-decile second-time founder), and Reactor’s ex-Apple/Netflix/Meta early team are future spinout candidates.

Reactor — the Company (now public)

Reactor (reactor.inc) emerged from stealth on May 28, 2026 with $59M led by Lightspeed — with WndrCo (Jeffrey Katzenberg, board observer), Amplify Partners, Sky9 Capital, Abstract Ventures, and FPV Ventures (Variety frames it as a Series A). Founded July 2025. Co-founders: Alberto Taiuti (CEO) and Bryce Schmidtchen (CTO) — both ex-Apple Vision Pro technical leads; the team is drawn from Apple, Netflix, Meta, Google, Adobe, Replicate, and Microsoft.

What it is: the inference / infrastructure layer for real-time interactive video (“world model”) models — positioned as “the critical layer between the model labs and the developers.” The pitch is latency: sub-50ms frame latency, persistent state, bidirectional streaming, and real-time control input, by optimizing open-source research models with custom CUDA + torch. Unified SDK/API, usage-based pricing, on AWS. Targets media/entertainment (in talks with major Hollywood studios — hence the Katzenberg tie), physical AI, and robotics. Named partner/customer: Overworld (a world-model foundation lab).

His own framing: “World models are redefining what AI can do, moving from systems that generate content in isolation, to ones that perceive and respond in real time… the time to first frame with our platform is basically nil” (vs. traditional pipelines needing minutes per clip). A third-party live demo had him swapping a face to Einstein’s, in real time, over Zoom. “Reactor is the culmination of our entire careers.”

The Spike — Real-Time Graphics / GPU Engineer Who Layered ML On Top

Taiuti is not a publishing researcher (no Scholar profile / papers found) — he is a builder, and the GitHub record (@snowzurfer, ~53 repos) is a clean real-time rendering + GPU + AR fingerprint, exactly the substrate Reactor’s “custom CUDA for real-time video models” sits on:

  • Graphics / GPU (C++): Vulkan Forward+ and deferred renderers (vulkan-forward-plus, vulkan-visbuff, vulkan-sagres-deferred), a software rasterizer in C, a raytracer, a SPIR-V parser, an LLVM/Kaleidoscope toy, forks of RenderDoc / USD / assimp, and a curated GPU-architecture reading list (graph-prog-resources, 19★).
  • AR / iOS (Swift): SCNHighlight (27★), avantindietro (ARKit undo, 18★), portar, invisibile — the Apple Vision Pro / RealityKit lineage.
  • ML / 3D-gen (recent, telling): DepthAnythingV2→CoreML conversion, a Blender ML-tools plugin (25★), forks of TRELLIS (structured 3D latents) and Gaussian-splatting / three.js. The pivot from rendering into generative 3D/video is visible in the repo history.

Track record of shipping: Slide AR (App Store, Jun 2019) — an iOS AR layout app he built and released solo; Apple AR/VR (Nov 2019–Oct 2021, RealityKit / the Measure app for the first LiDAR iPad/iPhone Pro); Skydio Senior Autonomy/Software Engineer (Jul 2022–Dec 2024, drone autonomy + 3D maps / WebGL stack); Codeplay Software (SYCL / GPU compute, pre-2019). His own line: “I love working on, and shipping meaningful products.”
