The Spike — With Evidence

EHRSHOT (NeurIPS 2023): The first publicly released longitudinal EHR benchmark for few-shot evaluation of clinical foundation models. Contains deidentified structured data from 6,739 patients from Stanford Medicine. Unlike MIMIC-III/IV, EHRSHOT is longitudinal and not restricted to ICU/ED patients. Released alongside CLMBR-T-base, a 141M parameter clinical foundation model pretrained on 2.57M patient records — one of the first fully released clinical foundation models for coded EHR data.

MedHELM (Nature Medicine 2025): Holistic Evaluation of Large Language Models for Medical Tasks. The most comprehensive medical LLM evaluation framework published in a top-tier medical journal.

Context Clues (ICLR 2025): Showed that longer-context models improve clinical predictions. Their Mamba-based model surpassed prior SOTA on 9 of 14 tasks on the EHRSHOT benchmark.

ECLAIR / Automating the Enterprise (VLDB 2024): Framework for using foundation models to automate enterprise workflows. The paper quantifies a $4T/year productivity opportunity from enterprise automation. Co-authored with Avanika Narayan and Chris Ré. This is the direct intellectual foundation of Kinetic Systems.

WONDERBREAD (NeurIPS Benchmarks 2024): Benchmark for evaluating multimodal foundation models on business process management tasks. First rigorous eval framework for computer-use agents in enterprise settings.

Test-Time Plan Caching (NeurIPS 2025): Cost-efficient serving of LLM agents. Co-authored with Kunle Olukotun (Stanford EE, co-founder of SambaNova). Infrastructure paper for making agent systems economically viable at scale.

Risks

  • Healthcare sales cycles are brutal. 12–18 month enterprise sales cycles. Regulatory compliance (HIPAA, SOC2). Hospital IT procurement is slow
  • Olive AI cautionary tale. Reached $4B valuation doing healthcare RPA automation, then collapsed. Market has been burned before on this thesis
  • Computer-use agents are early. The underlying technology (browser/screen-based agents) is still maturing. Reliability in healthcare contexts (where errors = patient harm) is a high bar
  • Academic-to-founder transition. Wornow has never run a company. PhD research ≠ GTM execution. Needs strong commercial co-founder
  • Crowded space. Every healthcare AI startup claims “workflow automation.” Differentiation will depend on clinical credibility + agent reliability
  • Stealth = limited visibility. No public product, no announced customers, no disclosed metrics. All conviction is based on research pedigree and advisor network
