◆ Investment Thesis

Ameen Patel is one of the few people who has built production-grade LLM inference systems at three different scales: big-tech (Amazon, 5.5 years building Alexa’s deep learning recommendation infrastructure), venture-backed startup (Together AI, Staff ML Engineer scaling their inference engine), and now frontier open-source lab (Prime Intellect, where he co-authored INTELLECT-3 — a 106B MoE that outperforms DeepSeek on math and code). His move from Together AI ($3.3B–$7.5B valuation) to a 32-person decentralized AI lab signals a thesis bet on open, distributed compute as the future of AI infrastructure. He sits at the exact intersection of inference optimization and RL post-training — the two hardest bottlenecks in scaling open-source AI. If he were to start a company around distributed inference infrastructure or RL-optimized serving, he would have rare credibility spanning both research and production.

The Spike — With Evidence

At Prime Intellect, Patel is a co-author on INTELLECT-3 (released Dec 2025), a 106B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model (12B active) trained with large-scale RL on 512 H200 GPUs. The model achieves SOTA for its size class:

His specific role at Prime Intellect: LLM Inference lead. He is responsible for the inference stack that powers both the model’s serving (via the Prime Intellect API) and the rollout generation during RL training — a critical bottleneck that determines training efficiency.

At Together AI, he co-authored “Beat the Long Tail: Distribution-Aware Speculative Decoding for RL Training” (Nov 2025, arXiv:2511.13841) with Percy Liang and Tri Dao. The paper proposes DAS (Distribution-Aware Speculative decoding), which reduces RL rollout time by up to 50% while preserving identical training curves. This is a directly productizable contribution — it makes RL post-training cheaper for every lab.

At Amazon (2017–2024), he published at EMNLP 2021 on scaling NLU improvement via implicit user feedback for Alexa, and co-authored work on graph-guided reasoning for multi-hop QA in LLMs (Nov 2023).

What He’d Build

The Distributed Inference Thesis

Patel’s career arc points to one thesis: inference is the bottleneck for AI scaling, and distributed/decentralized inference is the unlock. His move from Together AI (centralized cloud inference) to Prime Intellect (decentralized, consumer-GPU inference over public internet) is the signal. Prime Intellect’s blog explicitly describes their vision as “planetary-scale inference” — serving models across heterogeneous GPUs with 100ms latencies.

Possible company angles if he were to start:

  • RL-optimized inference infrastructure — the DAS paper shows he can make RL training 50% faster via smarter inference. Every lab doing RLHF/RLAIF needs this.
  • Distributed serving for open-source models — making 100B+ models deployable on commodity hardware
  • Inference-as-a-service for post-training — rollout generation is the most expensive part of RL; specialized infra could be a picks-and-shovels play

Market

  • AI inference market: $38B (2025) → $150B+ (2030), growing faster than training
  • RL post-training compute: emerging segment, every frontier lab needs rollout generation at scale
