Her Investment Philosophy — In Her Own Words

From Asian Tech Collective interview (Jul 2025, by Michelle Moon) + Kauffman Fellows profile + Bridge Funding Global

How She Evaluates Fund Managers

Her framework is team → strategy → track record, with team as the non-negotiable first filter:

  • “The first non-negotiable consideration is the team.” She wants to understand why the team is building this fund and what’s driving them
  • “Clarity of purpose” is key — managers with strong internal motivation and personal connection to their thesis tend to stand out
  • “Earned the right to win” — less focused on whether a manager found “white space” and more on whether they have deep domain expertise from lived experience or professional immersion
  • “Transparency — be proactive in explaining why you’re raising a fund and what your thesis is” but also ask your LPs questions. Transparency is a two-way street

On Building LP Relationships

  • “Build relationships early — many of our strongest partnerships were built over time through off-cycle conversations.”
  • They get to know the manager as a person, follow their journey, build conviction — so when fundraising comes, they’re ready to commit early and help close other LPs

Three Takeaways (Asian Tech Collective summary)

  1. “Back purpose-driven, high-character managers”
  2. “Relationships > transactions”
  3. “Innovate your VC processes, not just your portfolio”

Meeting Prep

What to Lead With

  1. Thesis clarity above all. She says “clarity of purpose” is the non-negotiable. Open with: why this fund exists, what specific problem it solves, what conviction drives it. Be precise — “inception-stage AI/ML, backing frontier lab departures and competition winners before they have decks”
  2. The “earned right to win” argument. She cares less about white space, more about domain expertise. Show the 6-agent research architecture, the frontier lab departure tracking (20 companies), the competition winner pipelines (IOI, IMO, ICPC). This is proof you do diligence differently, not just a slide about “sourcing edge”
  3. Specific deal flow examples. Name 3–4 portfolio companies sourced through differentiated channels. Show the research depth on each — she evaluates managers on whether they truly understand their founders
  4. Team > everything. Her #1 criterion is team. Be prepared to answer: Why you? What makes you uniquely suited? What’s your personal connection to this thesis?

How to Frame the Ask

  • Emerging Leaders Fund fit: “We understand Next Legacy’s Emerging Leaders strategy — $1–5M checks into managers generating contrarian returns. Our fund is inception-stage AI, which is definitionally contrarian. Here’s our portfolio and how we source.”
  • Don’t ask for a commitment in the first meeting. She values “off-cycle conversations” and “building relationships over time.” Frame this as: “I’d love for you to follow our journey and see how conviction builds.”
  • Philanthropy angle: Next Legacy’s model means returns go to charity. If the AI fund generates strong returns, those distributions amplify philanthropic impact. This is not a throwaway — it’s core to their identity

  • Don’t be transactional. “Relationships over transactions” is their #1 value. Don’t push for a timeline
  • Don’t underestimate her fund evaluation depth. She built CICC’s fund-of-funds business. She has institutional-grade diligence frameworks
  • Don’t ignore the philanthropy mission. It’s not a nice-to-have — it’s why their LPs invest. Acknowledge it sincerely
