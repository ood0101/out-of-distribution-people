The Spike — With Evidence

Energy-Based Transformers (EBTs)

Gladstone’s core invention. Published July 2025 (arXiv 2507.02092). EBTs learn an energy function that scores the compatibility of any input-prediction pair. Instead of predicting the next token in one forward pass, EBTs start with a random candidate and iteratively refine it via gradient descent to find the lowest energy (most compatible) state. This gives the model a built-in verifier — it can both generate and check its work.

Scaling Results (the numbers)

  • 35% higher scaling rate than Transformer++ across data, batch size, parameters, FLOPs, and depth — tested in both text and vision
  • +29% improvement over Transformer++ on language tasks via System 2 thinking (iterative refinement at inference)
  • Outperforms Diffusion Transformers on image denoising while using 99% fewer forward passes
  • Larger performance gains on data that is farther out-of-distribution — generalization improves with OOD distance
  • 33–34% higher scaling rate for video modeling (width and parameters)

EBT-Policy (follow-up, Oct 2025)

  • EBT-Policy outperforms Diffusion Policy across simulated and real-world robotic tasks
  • Converges in as few as 2 inference steps vs. Diffusion Policy’s 100 — a 50x reduction in inference compute
  • Exhibits zero-shot recovery from failed action sequences using only behavior cloning, without explicit retry training — an emergent capability

What He’d Build

The Energy-Based Architecture Thesis

Gladstone’s research points to a fundamental bet: the feed-forward transformer is not the final architecture. EBTs replace one-shot prediction with iterative energy minimization, giving models a built-in verifier that can “think” over any prediction on any modality. His thesis is that EBMs are a generalization of both diffusion models and standard transformers — they subsume both.

Likely company directions (post-PhD, ~2028–2029):

  • Next-generation foundation model company — EBT-based models that scale more efficiently than transformers for training and inference. The “what comes after the transformer” play
  • Robotics/embodied AI — EBT-Policy already outperforms Diffusion Policy. A robotics foundation model with built-in physical reasoning could be the product
  • Multimodal world model — his thesis is explicitly on world models. A company building world models for video, robotics, and planning across modalities
