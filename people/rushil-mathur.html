◆ Investment Thesis

Rushil Mathur is one of India’s four IMO 2024 Gold medalists from the country’s historic best-ever team (4th place, 167 points). The signal is unambiguous — IMO Gold representing India sits at the very top of the credential hierarchy in this repo. But unlike most IMO-tier credentials in the index, Mathur is not at an inflection point: he is a 19-year-old second-year Oxford undergraduate in Math + CS. The reason to index him now is forward-looking. He has already stacked three non-trivial post-Gold signals in 18 months: (1) 1st in his cohort at Oxford Math+CS Prelims (Department of CS Prize), beating every other student in his year; (2) Jane Street (per LinkedIn current title) — the standard high-end quant pipeline for Oxford olympiad medalists; (3) published author at age 18 (An Exploration of Olympiad Combinatorics, Bhaskaracharya Pratishthana, May 2024) plus a Sydney research internship in AI-driven energy retrofitting (first applied ML work). The reference class in the repo is Akshat Bubna (first Indian IOI Gold, 2014 → MIT → Modal CTO, $1.1B). The window for him to declare a path — quant lifer, ML researcher, or founder — is 2027–2029. Build the relationship now while the inbox is empty. Tier 2 today; very plausible T1 within 12 months if Jane Street converts to FT or he publishes ML research.
