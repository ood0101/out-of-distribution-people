◆ Investment Thesis

Ritvik Singh is one of the strongest dexterous manipulation researchers in the world — the person who built NVIDIA’s robot learning simulation infrastructure (ORBIT/Isaac Lab, 6.8k GitHub stars, 3.3k forks, now the standard framework for GPU-accelerated robot learning) and pushed the frontier of vision-based dexterous grasping from privileged state to end-to-end RGB. His latest work achieves 93% real-world success rate on dexterous grasping using end-to-end RL with a novel disaggregated simulation architecture — the first demonstration of end-to-end RL for multifingered hand manipulation. He is a PhD student at Berkeley co-advised by Pieter Abbeel (co-founder of Covariant, $222M raised) and Jitendra Malik (one of the most cited computer vision researchers alive), after spending formative years at NVIDIA Robotics shipping production simulation tools. The robotics manipulation market is exploding: Physical Intelligence just raised at $11B valuation, Dexterity at $1.65B, Figure AI at $2.6B. Singh sits at the exact intersection of sim-to-real transfer, dexterous manipulation, and visuomotor policy learning that these companies need. The window opens when he finishes his PhD (~2026–2027).

Evidence

End-to-End RL for Dexterous Grasping (2025)

“End-to-end RL Improves Dexterous Grasping Policies” — first author. With Karl Van Wyk, Jitendra Malik, Pieter Abbeel, Nathan Ratliff, and Ankur Handa (NVIDIA + UC Berkeley). The first work to demonstrate end-to-end RL for dexterous grasping with multifingered hands in the real world.

  • 93% real-world success rate using depth-based teacher distillation with disaggregated simulation (vs. 87% baseline)
  • Novel disaggregated simulation architecture: separates simulator and learner across GPUs, doubling concurrent environments (8,400 vs. 4,096 at 160x120 resolution)
  • Key insight: end-to-end training enables emergent active vision behaviors — the arm learns to move to unblock visual obstructions rather than assuming perfect state information
  • Depth-to-RGB distillation preserves observability better than state-to-vision distillation

DextrAH-RGB: First RGB-Only Dexterous Grasping (Nov 2024)

“DextrAH-RGB: Visuomotor Policies to Grasp Anything with Dexterous Hands” — first author. The first work to demonstrate robust sim-to-real transfer of an end-to-end RGB-based policy for complex, dynamic, contact-rich dexterous grasping.

  • Trains fabric-guided policy (FGP) via RL in simulation, then distills into RGB-based policy using photorealistic tiled rendering
  • Competitive with depth-based dexterous grasping policies — no depth sensor required
  • Generalizes to novel objects with unseen geometry, texture, and lighting conditions in real-world deployment
  • Authors: Ritvik Singh, Arthur Allshire, Ankur Handa, Nathan Ratliff, Karl Van Wyk

ORBIT / Isaac Lab: NVIDIA’s Standard Robot Learning Framework (2023)

“ORBIT: A Unified Simulation Framework for Interactive Robot Learning Environments” — published at IROS 2023 and IEEE RA-L. Co-authored with Mayank Mittal, Calvin Yu, and others. ORBIT evolved into Isaac Lab, now NVIDIA’s official GPU-accelerated robot learning framework.

  • 6.8k GitHub stars, 3.3k forks — the most widely used GPU-accelerated robot learning simulation
  • Supports RL, imitation learning, motion planning with photorealistic rendering and fast rigid/soft body simulation
  • Used by hundreds of robotics labs and companies worldwide for sim-to-real transfer
  • Singh contributed core simulation and environment design components

DeXtreme: Sim-to-Real In-Hand Manipulation (ICRA 2023)

“DeXtreme: Transfer of Agile In-Hand Manipulation from Simulation to Reality” — NVIDIA Research team. Ritvik Singh contributed rendering pipeline development and pose estimation.

  • Vision-based policies significantly outperform prior vision policies in the literature on object reorientation
  • Competitive with policies given privileged state information via motion capture
  • Uses Allegro Hand + Isaac Gym GPU-based simulation

Synthetica: Synthetic Data for Robot Perception (IROS 2025)

“Synthetica: Large Scale Synthetic Data for Robot Perception” — first author. Leverages photorealistic ray-tracing to generate 2.7M training images.

  • State-of-the-art object detection with detectors running at 50–100Hz — 9x faster than prior SOTA
  • Demonstrates pipeline for real-world robotics with custom objects lacking existing datasets

Fast-Grasp’D: Differentiable Grasp Synthesis (IROS 2023, RA-L)

Differentiable grasping simulator used to synthesize Grasp’D-1M, a large-scale dataset for multi-finger grasping.

  • 10x faster than GraspIt! and 20x faster than prior Grasp’D simulator
  • Grasp’D-1M: 1M training examples across three robotic hands with multimodal visual inputs
  • Retrained grasping pipeline shows 30% more contact, 33% higher epsilon metric, 35% lower displacement

What He’d Build (Thesis Reconstruction)

Most likely: A dexterous manipulation company built on sim-to-real visuomotor policies. The thesis writes itself from his papers: (1) generate massive synthetic training data cheaply via photorealistic simulation (Synthetica), (2) train end-to-end visuomotor policies that work from RGB cameras alone (DextrAH-RGB), (3) transfer to real hardware with 93%+ success rate (End-to-end RL paper), (4) build on the simulation infrastructure he already created (ORBIT/Isaac Lab). The product: a dexterous manipulation stack that drops into any robot arm+hand platform and works out-of-the-box in unstructured environments. Customers: logistics, manufacturing, food handling, household. The moat is the sim-to-real pipeline — the hardest part to replicate — and Singh is arguably the person who knows it best in the world, having built it from both the simulation side (ORBIT) and the policy side (DextrAH/E2E RL).
