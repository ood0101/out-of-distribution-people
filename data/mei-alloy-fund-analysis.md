## 7. Key Findings & Strategic Implications

### Finding 1: Alloy Fund is a Micro-Fund Vehicle, Not the Real Story
Alloy Fund writes $10K–$200K checks and doesn't lead rounds. The PitchBook-tracked portfolio is only 3 companies. The real story is Mei's personal angel portfolio, which includes $6B+ in post-money value across AI hardware/infra companies. Alloy Fund is best understood as a **syndicate wrapper** that lets the three partners pool deal flow and LP capital for co-investments.

### Finding 2: Mei's Deal Sourcing is Unique
She sources from three channels most VCs don't have:
1. **EleutherAI** — open-source AI research community (academic founders before they incorporate)
2. **MIT Kavli/Media Lab** — deep tech research pipeline
3. **Hardware founder experience** (subatomiQ) — she can evaluate hardware startups from the inside

### Finding 3: The Formation-Stage Access Pattern
Mei claims "first ever investor" at three companies (Standard Intelligence, RadixArk, Mirendil). This means she sees companies at literal incorporation — before any institutional capital. This is the pattern of someone who is deeply embedded in the AI research community, not someone who sees deals through traditional VC channels.

### Finding 4: Co-Investment Opportunity
Alloy Fund explicitly does not lead rounds. They co-invest with Pear VC and Caffeinated Capital. For Boldcap, the opportunity is:
- **Build co-investment relationship** where Mei shares formation-stage deal flow
- **Offer follow-on capital** ($500K–$1M checks) that Alloy Fund cannot write
- **Provide diligence and network** that a micro-fund cannot

### Finding 5: The Full Career Arc Reveals Unusual Pattern
IMF (macro economics) -> Ares Management (PE/alternatives) -> MIT Kavli (astrophysics research) -> MIT Media Lab (AI research) -> subatomiQ (quantum hardware founder) -> Romulus Capital (seed VC) -> Alloy Fund (micro-fund partner) -> EleutherAI advisor -> AI hardware angel investor with $6B+ portfolio value

This is not a typical angel investor career. The IMF/Ares background suggests quantitative macro thinking. The astrophysics/quantum background suggests deep physics intuition. The combination produces an investor who can evaluate AI hardware from first principles — not from pattern matching.
